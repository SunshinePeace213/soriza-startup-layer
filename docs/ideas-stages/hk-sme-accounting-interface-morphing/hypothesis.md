# Hypothesis: SME Accounting Back-Office Per-User Interface Morphing

## Sharpened problem hypothesis (provisional, to-be-tested)

Hong Kong SME finance teams (owner, accountant, clerk; 2–8 people) using Xero/QuickBooks must switch between admin-locked role views multiple times per day, losing approximately 1–2 hours/week to navigation friction and interface mismatches, and lack a customization mechanism to surface only their job-critical data — a problem addressable via per-user layout morphing without role-permission redesign.

---

## Four dimensions (provisional, to-be-tested)

### WHO
**Named role + context + segment**: Hong Kong and Greater China SME finance teams — specifically the owner (needs cash-flow narrative), the accountant (needs transaction reconciliation queues), and the admin clerk (needs invoice entry forms) — working in companies of 2–8 people currently using Xero, QuickBooks, or legacy local accounting software.

### HOW OFTEN
**Frequency**: Multiple times per day. In a small multi-role team, roles typically switch daily: owner checks cash flow in morning, clerk enters invoices mid-afternoon, accountant reconciles end-of-day. Each role transition requires navigating through admin-locked interface constraints.

### HOW SEVERE
**Cost/pain magnitude**: Approximately 1–2 hours per week lost to role-switching friction and interface mismatches per team. Pain sources: (a) time lost navigating irrelevant menu structures per role; (b) forced workarounds (exporting to spreadsheets, printing reports); (c) error risk when non-specialists encounter data not relevant to their job.

### STATUS QUO
**What they do now**: SME teams use role-based permission systems (e.g., Xero's "Contacts" vs "Accountant" role), but these are admin-set and locked at the permission level. There is no per-user interface customization — users cannot rearrange dashboards, hide irrelevant data columns, or surface job-critical views without admin rebuild of the entire role definition.

---

## Rationale for advancement

- **Specific**: All four dimensions name concrete roles, quantifiable frequency, defensible pain magnitude, and a clear current state.
- **Falsifiable**: Claims are testable via customer interviews (frequency of switching, time lost, current workarounds), product audit (Xero/QuickBooks permission model), and prototype validation (do users find per-user layout morphing valuable?).
- **Grounded in founder fit**: The founder's finance minor, credit-finance internship, trilingual reach, and agentic-build speed align well with a vertical SaaS play targeting Hong Kong/Greater China SME accounting.
