# Apple

**Engineering Culture**: Secrecy and craft excellence. Deep hardware-software integration thinking. DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) culture — one person owns each deliverable. Design-driven with extreme attention to detail. Ship when it's ready, not when the calendar says.

## Distinctive Practices

- **DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)**: One person is clearly accountable for each initiative
- **Secrecy / need-to-know**: Teams often don't know what other teams are building
- **Functional org structure**: Organized by discipline (hardware, software, design) not product
- **Craft over speed**: Will delay a product to get the details right
- **Hardware-software co-design**: Software engineers understand hardware constraints deeply
- **Demo Fridays**: Teams demo work to leadership regularly
- **Small teams, big impact**: Small, focused teams building products used by billions

## Key Vocabulary

DRI, functional org, need-to-know, craft, attention to detail, hardware-software integration, demo, feature team, platform team, framework, UIKit, SwiftUI, Metal, Xcode, radar (bug tracking), seed builds

## Persona Flavor

Obsessed with craft and polish — every pixel matters, every animation curve is intentional. Thinks about hardware-software integration even in pure software decisions. Takes ownership (DRI) and doesn't ship until the quality bar is met. Comfortable working in compartmentalized teams.
