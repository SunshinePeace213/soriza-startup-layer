# Trends / Why-now — Findings

## Key findings

- **The capability line crossed "real white-collar work" in the last ~18 months.** METR measures the length of tasks AI can complete autonomously: ~30 min for 2024's best models → multi-hour by late 2025 (14.5 hrs for Claude Opus 4.6, Feb 2026). The doubling rate *accelerated* from every 7 months to every ~4 months in 2024–2025. Why it matters: a back-office task that takes a bookkeeper 1–3 hours (reconcile a month, prep a filing) is now inside the agent's reliable horizon — exactly the wedge an AI-native service needs. Pick tasks that fit *today's* horizon (hours, repetitive, structured-output), not week-long judgment work.

- **The "computer use" unlock (Oct 2024) is the structural enabler for back-office.** Back-office work lives in browser apps (Xero, QuickBooks, government e-filing portals, payroll systems). Anthropic shipped Claude "computer use" Oct 2024; by 2025 agents can drive these UIs. Why it matters: HK/APAC SMB back-office runs on portals with no clean API (MyInvois portal, eMPF portal, IRD eTAX) — computer-use agents can operate them like a human BPO worker does, which is the actual moat vs. pure API integrations.

- **Token costs fell ~10x/year, collapsing the unit economics of "do the work."** GPT-4-equivalent quality went from ~$20/M tokens (late 2022) to ~$0.40/M (2025); GPT-4o dropped from $36→$4/M in <2 years; open-weight tiers (Qwen/DeepSeek/Kimi) now handle 60–80% of agent traffic locally. Why it matters: the math behind "run an accounting firm with 1 human + 10 agents at software margins" only works because inference is now ~50–150x cheaper than 2022. A done-for-you service priced at human-BPO rates throws off SaaS-like margins.

- **YC / Alströmer thesis is explicit and fresh (S26 RFS).** Exact framing: "AI-native companies that don't sell software—they sell the service... total spend on services is many times larger than the spend on software. And a lot of these services are already outsourced, which makes them much easier to replace." Named sectors: insurance brokerage; accounting/tax/audit; compliance; healthcare admin. The "already outsourced" point is the key insight for the founder: replacing an existing BPO/outsourced relationship is easier than ripping out installed software — no behavior change, just a better/cheaper vendor.

- **Funding signal is real and concentrated in back-office.** AI took $202B / ~50% of all VC in 2025 (up from 34% in 2024). Accounting specifically: Basis raised $34M (Dec 2024) → $100M at $1.15B valuation (Feb 2026); YC funded multiple "full-stack AI accounting firm" startups (Cranston AI — "$900b accounting labor market"; Minerva, 2025). In 2024 ≥9 YC startups positioned as full-stack AI services (insurance, HR, accounting, mortgage, healthcare). Salient and others "directly replace workers with AI" rather than sell tooling. Implication: US accounting is getting crowded — the founder's edge is *HK/APAC SMB*, not competing head-on in the US.

- **HK has an acute, government-acknowledged accountant/bookkeeper shortage — the labor that AI services replace is genuinely scarce here.** HKICPA survey: 10% of firms had >50% vacancy rates; 61% rated talent shortage 7/10; junior roles hardest to fill. HK projects a 180,000-person manpower shortage by 2028; accounting was added to the official Talent List (Mar 1, 2025) for fast-track immigration. Why it matters: unlike the US (where AI services threaten existing jobs), in HK the work is *unstaffable* — an AI service sells into a vacuum, lowering buyer resistance and partner (firm) resistance.

- **Rising HK labor costs make the buy-vs-AI math favor automation now.** Statutory minimum wage rose to HKD 42.10/hr (May 1, 2025, +5.25%); SMEs face an estimated 8–12% payroll-cost rise. 360,000 SMEs = >98% of HK enterprises, ~45% of private employment (Census & Statistics, 2024). Why it matters: a huge, cost-pressured SMB base that can't afford to hire — the exact buyer for a cheap done-for-you back-office service. Lean-capital wedge: target the SMB that currently uses no one / a stretched in-house admin.

- **Regulatory mandates are forcing back-office change across HK/SG/SEA — a recurring, deadline-driven wedge.**
  - **HK eMPF:** centralized MPF platform launched Jun 2024; all schemes must migrate by end-2025; first mandatory eMPF contribution submission ~Oct 10, 2025; all employers on it by 2026. Every HK employer must re-learn payroll/MPF submission — a forced-change moment.
  - **Singapore InvoiceNow (Peppol):** GST e-invoicing phased in — Nov 1 2025 (new voluntary registrants) → Apr 2026 → full rollout to all GST businesses by Apr 2031. Gov funds up to S$5k.
  - **Malaysia MyInvois:** mandatory from Aug 2024 (>RM100M turnover) cascading to >RM1M by Jan 2026 (sub-RM1M now exempted/raised threshold). Penalties up to RM20k.
  Why it matters: mandates create non-discretionary, deadline-driven demand and a structured/validated data format (XML/JSON, Peppol PINT) that agents handle well. A compliance-onboarding or e-invoicing-submission service is a clean low-regulatory-appetite wedge (you're doing data prep + filing, not gated CPA/audit sign-off).

- **APAC SMB back-office is already a remote/outsourced norm — no behavior change needed to insert an AI service.** Philippines BPO: ~$40B revenue, 1.82M workers (60–70% remote/hybrid), 8.5% of GDP in 2025; back-office BPO growing ~4%/yr; firms explicitly do SMB bookkeeping/tax prep at 50–70% below Western cost. APAC data-entry outsourcing grew 46% (India/China/Philippines). Why it matters: SMBs already trust an unseen remote provider to touch their books — the founder isn't asking them to adopt new software or change habits, just to swap their offshore human team for an AI-native one. This is the lowest-friction entry path and validates "sell the service."

## Why-now and why-here (synthesis for idea generation)

- **Why now:** agent task-horizon crossed multi-hour reliability + computer-use can drive portal-based back-office + inference cost fell ~10x/yr → the "do the work at software margins" model became real in the last ~18 months, and capital + YC are explicitly funding it.
- **Why here (HK/APAC):** genuine accountant/admin *shortage* (not job-displacement resistance) + rising minimum wage squeezing 360k SMEs + a wave of 2024–2026 e-invoicing/eMPF mandates forcing back-office change + an existing outsourced-back-office culture that needs zero behavior change. Founder's local + technical edge fits a wedge that is back-office, B2B, deadline/mandate-driven, and *not* CPA/license-gated (data prep, e-invoicing submission, payroll/MPF admin, compliance onboarding — vs. avoid audit sign-off, insurance brokerage, regulated advice).
