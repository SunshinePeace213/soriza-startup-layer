# Adjacent / analogous markets — Findings

## Key findings

- **"Sell completed work, not seats" is the validated mechanism (service-as-software).** The structural winner across every vertical: buyers shift from "we need 50 CRM licenses" to "we need 5,000 tickets / returns / reconciliations done per month." Vertical AI agents sell *outcomes*, which lets you price against a labor/services budget that dwarfs software budgets. This is the transplantable core for APAC SMB back-office — pick a task that is already a line-item of outsourced labor (bookkeeping hours, support tickets, secretarial filings) and price per unit of output. (VC Cafe; AgentMarketCap)

- **Crescendo's "AI + managed human + Total Outcome Guarantee" is the most copyable model for a low-domain-experience, technical founder.** It bills ~$1.25 per resolved support ticket, resolves 100% of tickets (~90% AI, rest human fallback), and explicitly sells "a team you hire," not software. This hybrid wedge is ideal for APAC: it tolerates imperfect AI (humans backstop), produces an outcome SLA buyers understand, and the labor backstop can be staffed cheaply in the Philippines. Decagon (enterprise, ~$95K/yr, per-conversation) is the higher-end SaaS-flavored counterpart — too enterprise/regulated for a lean APAC SMB wedge. Copy Crescendo's structure, not Decagon's. (eesel.ai; Crescendo)

- **Accounting/bookkeeping is the most de-risked vertical and already has APAC-native incumbents to study and undercut.** Basis ($100M Series B, $1.15B val, used by ~30% of top-25 US accounting firms) automates tax/audit/advisory via agent + rules + accounting logic — sells *into firms* (B2B2B), not to SMBs. In APAC the analog wedge already exists: **Osome** (SG/HK/UK, 13,000+ SMB customers, 400 staff incl. a Philippines back-office, $17M Series B 2024) and **Atlas** (Singapore, $6M seed — explicitly "let small accounting practices scale revenue without headcount"). Signal: the labor-heavy, already-outsourced bookkeeping/corp-secretary task in HK/SG is being served by services firms still running human back-offices — that human layer is the disruption target. (SiliconANGLE; Osome PR; Atlas)

- **"AI for the accounting firm" (B2B2B) is a cleaner wedge than "AI for the SMB."** Basis, Atlas, and Juno ($12M seed, automates high-net-worth tax data entry/review with 90% AI + human-in-loop) all sell to *practitioners* who already do the work, not to end SMBs. For a founder with no domain experience, this is the safer structural play in APAC: the firm supplies domain/compliance knowledge and the existing client base; you supply the agent that removes their labor cost. Lower regulatory exposure (the firm remains the licensed party) — fits the "low regulatory appetite" constraint. (TFN; CPA Practice Advisor)

- **BPO disruption signals exactly where labor-heavy APAC back-office demand concentrates — and it's enormous.** Philippines IT-BPM = ~$40B export revenue, ~1.9M workers, with **contact-center work = 83% of revenue / 89% of employment** (i.e., a single automatable task category). India employs ~6M, 7% of GDP. Analysts project 1M+ jobs displaced by 2030; the Philippines is spending $25M/yr to shield workers. Translation for a founder: the "automation arbitrage > cost arbitrage" shift means anything currently offshored to Manila/Bengaluru (support, data entry, AP/AR, claims, KYC/AML ops) is a pre-validated, budgeted, already-outsourced task — the easiest category to replace per the YC thesis. (Outsource Accelerator; AMRO; MicroSourcing)

- **Vertical AI funding is concentrating in exactly the YC-thesis categories: legal, healthcare-admin, insurance, finance-regulation.** 2025 agentic AI funding hit $6.42B (>25% of all capital ever in the sector). Legal: Harvey ($1.5B→$8B in 18 months), Legora ($550M Series D, $5.55B — largest legal AI round). Healthcare admin: Hippocratic AI ($1.6B), Ambience ($243M Series C), Nabla ($316M Series E, $5.3B). The named "richest opportunities": healthcare administration, legal contract review, construction compliance, financial-services regulation — "industries drowning in manual work generic AI can't navigate." These verticals are proven in US/EU but largely **unbuilt for APAC localization** (HK/SG contract law, HKFRS, local-language contracts). (AgentMarketCap; 8seneca)

- **Localization moat = language + local compliance schema, and it's underserved.** HK-native players already differentiate on exactly this: 247 AICFO and Tofu auto-process **Chinese + English invoices** and bake in HKFRS / e-filing. The transplantable insight: US AI-native services don't handle Cantonese/Traditional-Chinese documents, multi-currency HK/CN banking, or local statutory formats — that gap is the defensible wedge for a technical APAC builder copying a proven US mechanism. Multilingual document understanding is the localization layer US incumbents won't prioritize. (aiaccounting.hk; Tofu)

- **AI SDR / sales-dev and AI recruiting are proven "digital worker" wedges but weaker fits for the APAC SMB B2B constraint.** AI SDR (e.g. Qualified — reasons over intent signals, acts across channels, books meetings autonomously) and autonomous recruiting agents (source on LinkedIn → personalized outreach → schedule screens) are clean, low-regulation, outcome-priced "AI employee" models. They transplant technically, but APAC SMBs have thinner outbound-sales budgets than US mid-market — so these are better as *horizontal* adjacencies to borrow the agent architecture from than as the primary APAC SMB wedge. Recruiting-ops is the more plausible of the two given HK/SG hiring-agency spend. (Presta; vertical-agent roundups)

## Mechanism transplant ranking (for an APAC SMB, B2B, low-reg, lean, no-domain founder)

1. **AI + managed-human, outcome-priced back-office task** (Crescendo structure) — staff the human backstop in PH, sell per-unit outcome to HK/SG SMBs.
2. **AI-for-the-firm (B2B2B) in bookkeeping/corp-sec** — firm holds the license + clients + domain; you remove their labor. Lowest regulatory exposure.
3. **Localize a proven US AI-native service** (support-as-a-service, AP/AR ops, KYC/AML ops) with Cantonese/Traditional-Chinese + local statutory schema as the moat.
