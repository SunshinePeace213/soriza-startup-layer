# Hypothesis: Corporate Training Platform with Per-Learner Interface Customization

## Sharpened Problem Hypothesis

**L&D managers at mid-size regulated companies (50–500 employees) in Hong Kong and Greater China currently lose 15–25% of their training cohorts to interface disengagement in mandatory compliance courses (2–4 times per year), forcing them to spend ~HK$5–20k per cycle on remedial training or accepting compliance risk — and would pay outcome-based fees if they could let employees self-select visual/Gantt/card layouts and measure completion gains.**

---

## Four Testable Dimensions

### WHO *(provisional, to-be-tested)*
Corporate L&D Manager or Compliance Officer at a mid-size regulated company (50–500 employees) in Hong Kong or Greater China — responsible for ensuring mandatory training completion rates meet regulatory thresholds, with budget authority and measured success against compliance % targets.

### HOW OFTEN *(provisional, to-be-tested)*
Each learner encounters the LMS interface 2–4 times per year in high-regulation sectors (financial services, safety compliance), with cohort-wide disengagement recurring each training cycle. Current dropout attributed to interface friction: ~15–25% of target cohort per cycle.

### HOW SEVERELY *(provisional, to-be-tested)*
Non-compliance drives measurable cost: regulatory fines (HK$50k–HK$M+), re-training overhead (~HK$5–20k per cycle), audit delays, and hiring delays. The interface is the stated limiting factor preventing L&D teams from hitting compliance targets (e.g., 95%+ completion required).

### STATUS QUO *(provisional, to-be-tested)*
L&D teams deploy Cypher Learning or Sana Labs on default settings (card layout, same for all learners). When completion falls short, they respond by:
- Re-sending reminder emails
- Running compliance "cram" sessions
- Accepting non-completion risk
- NOT customizing the interface (existing LMS platforms don't expose this layer)

---

## What Can Be Tested
1. What % of learners disengage from mandatory training due to interface design vs. other factors (time, relevance, support)?
2. Do L&D managers measure interface disengagement as a top complaint (as stated in market research)?
3. What do teams currently spend on remedial training per compliance cycle?
4. Would L&D managers adopt outcome-based pricing (pay per certification completion target hit)?
5. Does interface customization (visual/Gantt/card layouts) measurably improve completion rates in a pilot?
