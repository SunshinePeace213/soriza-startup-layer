# Hypothesis: personalized-ai-digest

## Hypothesis
Working knowledge-workers (PMs, marketers, developers, founders, ~25–45) who must apply AI in their jobs spend 60–120 minutes a day tracking 20+ creators AND official model-provider updates (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) across docs, YouTube, X, Reddit, Medium and newsletters — yet still miss or can't understand the pieces that matter — because the AI digests they rely on today (TLDR AI, The Rundown, Ben's Bites) are one-size-fits-all: not filtered to each person's interests or calibrated to their skill level.

## Who
Working knowledge-workers — PMs, marketers, developers, founders — ~25–45, who must apply AI day-to-day and keep up by following 20+ AI practitioner-creators plus official model-provider channels (Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini release notes & docs) across YouTube, X, Reddit, Medium and newsletters. Driven by job/career pressure, not hobby curiosity.

## How Often
Daily. New creator content, posts, newsletters and frequent model/feature releases arrive every day, so the "falling behind / can't digest it all" pain recurs daily.

## How Severely
~60–120 minutes/day (≈7–14 hours/week) skimming feeds and changelogs, most of it low-value — plus the career risk of discovering a productivity-changing tool, model feature or technique weeks late and lagging faster-adopting peers.

## Status Quo
A hybrid of free human-curated AI digests (TLDR AI, The Rundown AI, Ben's Bites), manual following/bookmarking, official provider release notes, and the YouTube/X algorithm. The gap: these are one-size-fits-all — not filtered to each reader's interests (e.g. generative-image vs not) and not calibrated to skill level, so content is often too advanced to understand or simply irrelevant.
