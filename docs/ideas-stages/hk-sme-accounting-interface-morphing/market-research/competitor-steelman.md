# Competitor Steelman — hk-sme-accounting-interface-morphing

*Written in the voice of the incumbent's strategist. This is the case that we beat you — not a balanced landscape. The founder's thesis is that no accounting tool ships the interface as user-malleable at the individual level, so the owner/accountant/clerk each navigate the same bloated menu. We will show that (a) we already ship the customer-relevant version of this, (b) the version we don't ship, we are about to make irrelevant with agents, and (c) the place you'd build it — the per-person finance layer — is owned upstream by the very platform you build on.*

## Per-tier threat

- **Direct — Xero & QuickBooks (Intuit).** This is not a greenfield. Xero already ships a customisable homepage where each user reorders widgets — account balances, bills to pay, invoices owed, favourite reports — in any order they want, and the widgets each person sees are already gated by their permission level. QuickBooks Online Advanced ships *custom roles* (granular per-area view/create/edit/delete/approve) **and** customisable dashboards "to track what matters most." So the founder's headline claim — "customization is locked to admin-set role permissions, not user-expressed layout preferences" — is half-false today: the owner already drags their own widgets around, the clerk already lands on invoice entry. The 20% of malleability the founder thinks is missing is a preference toggle we add in a sprint, not a moat. We have 4.5M+ (Xero) and millions more (QBO) SMEs already inside the product, an installed accountant-referral channel in Hong Kong where Xero is *the* most-recommended package among local firms, and the per-company billing relationship. The founder has to win that customer one cold conversation at a time.
- **Indirect — "Just Ask Xero" (JAX) and Intuit Assist agents.** This is the cheaper, "good-enough" answer that eats the whole premise. The founder's pain is "switching roles means navigating deep menus." Our answer isn't a better menu or a morphing layout — it's *no menu*. JAX is an agentic financial superagent, rolling out since Sept 2025 with conversational data exploration that "learns how your business runs"; the owner asks "what's my cash position this month" in plain language and gets the cash-flow narrative without a layout at all. Intuit Assist ships specialised agents (Accounting, Payments, Tax) that surface the right thing per persona in the background. Natural-language + agent retrieval is a *superset* of role-tailored layouts: it adapts to the individual without the individual ever expressing a layout preference. If the job is "see only the financial view I need," ask-and-receive beats arrange-your-own-dashboard for every non-technical SME user. The founder is building a better filing cabinet while we remove the cabinet.
- **Potential acquirer — Anthropic (the platform the founder builds on).** This is the most uncomfortable one. The founder's own hypothesis cites "Anthropic enterprise agent plugins (signal 11)" as targeting finance at the *org* level, leaving the per-person SME layer open. But Anthropic's Feb 2026 enterprise agents launch already ships **custom, interactive UI surfaced directly within Claude via an MCP app**, plus a finance plug-in with a stock "close the books at month-end" agent template. Generative, per-context UI rendered by the agent *is the founder's exact mechanism* — and it ships from the company whose models (Claude Code) are the founder's entire build stack and stated career bet. The "per-person layer" isn't an unaddressed gap; it's a default property of generative-UI agents. Anthropic doesn't need to acquire the founder; the founder is building a thin wrapper over a capability the platform gives away.
- **Adjacent — Kingdee (and the China-standards trap).** For the Greater China half of the founder's market, Kingdee has held the No. 1 SME enterprise-application share in IDC China for 21 consecutive years, runs Cloud Cosmic AI Agent 2.0 with native finance agents, and its small/micro AI assistant already has ~170,000 active users delivering ~80% accounting-efficiency gains. Kingdee KIS is also the default when a business spans HK and mainland accounting standards — the exact cross-border SME the founder claims as a trilingual edge. Kingdee can ship per-persona AI finance views to the China SME base faster than the founder can get a foothold, in Mandarin, with local tax/filing depth the founder doesn't have.

## The one that kills you

**Xero — specifically Xero with JAX.** Not because the static dashboard is better, but because Xero owns both the customer-relevant version of your idea *today* and the version that makes your idea obsolete *tomorrow*, inside one subscription the customer already pays for.

**Why their approach is better for the customer.** The SME owner, accountant, and clerk do not want to *design* their interface — that is the founder's projection of what a developer would want. They want the answer with zero configuration. Xero's customisable homepage already lands each role on a sensible default; JAX then lets any of the three *ask* for whatever they need in Cantonese-adjacent plain language and renders it on demand. "Arrange your own per-person layout" adds a configuration burden to a user who would rather type a question. For a 2–8 person finance team, ask-and-receive dominates arrange-your-own on every axis the buyer cares about: setup time, training, and the fact that nobody has to maintain anyone's custom view.

**Why customers choose us over you.** They already have us. Xero is the most-recommended package among Hong Kong accounting firms — the founder's "dense, relationship-driven SME community" is *our* referral channel, mediated by accountants who standardise their whole client book on one tool and will not add a second login for a layout layer. Switching cost is the entire books, bank feeds, and reconciliation history. The founder is asking the customer to bolt a per-person interface onto data that lives in our database, accessed through our API, governed by our permissions, on our pricing relationship. We change the terms of that API whenever we like.

**Why each claimed differentiator fails:**
- *"No tool ships interface as user-malleable at the individual level."* False as stated. Xero ships per-user customisable widgets; QBO Advanced ships custom roles + custom dashboards. The residual gap (free-form personal layout) is a roadmap toggle, cheaply copied, and — post-JAX — a feature nobody asks for because they ask the agent instead.
- *"The per-person layer within SME finance is unaddressed."* It's addressed two ways at once: by our existing per-user dashboards and by generative-UI agents (JAX, and Anthropic's own MCP interactive UI) that render per-person, per-question views with no layout step. The founder is naming a gap that two converging waves are closing.
- *"Vertical SaaS WTP premium 2–3x; outcome-based pricing — charge per month-end close completed on time."* We have already shipped the outcome the founder wants to charge for: Anthropic's finance plug-in has a stock "close the books at month-end" agent; Intuit and Xero are building month-end close automation directly. Owning the *interface* gives the founder no leverage over the *outcome* (the close), which is computed from data we hold. The founder would be charging a premium for a skin over our engine.
- *"Finance minor + credit-finance internship = domain credibility."* Against Kingdee's 21-year China-SME tax/filing depth and Xero's HK accountant network, a finance minor and one credit-finance internship is not non-obvious domain knowledge — it is below the floor. Credibility in this buyer is held by the accountant who already recommends Xero, not by the vendor.
- *"Trilingual reach / fast-to-revenue in dense HK SME community."* Trilingual UI is table stakes we and Kingdee both have; it is not defensible. And the dense, relationship-driven community is exactly what makes us *harder* to dislodge — relationships route through accountants who are standardised on us.

## Survival bar

For the founder to survive this, **all** of the following must be true — this is the bar to clear, not reassurance:

1. **Prove the job is "configure my own view," not "ask and receive."** The founder must show, in live HK SME discovery, that owners/clerks actually want to arrange a personal layout *and still prefer it after seeing JAX-style ask-and-receive* — because if the job is answered conversationally, the entire premise is dead. If discovery shows users would rather type a question, abandon the idea.
2. **Find a wedge the platform owners structurally will not cross.** Per-person layout is not it (roadmap toggle for Xero; default property of Anthropic's generative UI). The founder must identify a job that is too small for Xero/Intuit to bother with *and* too HK/China-specific for them to localise — e.g. a cross-border HK↔mainland month-end workflow that neither Xero (no China depth) nor Kingdee (weak HK-cloud UX) serves well — and own *that*, not the interface in general.
3. **Don't build on the sword pointed at you.** The founder's stack is Anthropic/Claude — the same company shipping generative finance UI via MCP. A wrapper over that capability has no moat. Survival requires owning something the agent can't render: proprietary cross-border reconciliation logic, a HK-specific data integration, or a distribution lock (an accountant channel of his own), not a UI layer.
4. **Break, don't borrow, the accountant channel.** Because Xero owns the HK accountant referral network, the founder must reach SMEs through a channel that does *not* route through Xero-standardised accountants — direct-to-owner, an industry vertical, or a partner accountants don't control. With no GTM track record and 1–2 months of runway, this is the single hardest condition, and it is non-negotiable.

If the founder cannot meet 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously, the honest read is that this is a feature we ship, not a company.
