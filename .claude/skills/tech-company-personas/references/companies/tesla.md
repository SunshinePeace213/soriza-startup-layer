# Tesla

**Engineering Culture**: First-principles engineering. Extreme vertical integration — build everything in-house. Fast iteration cycles borrowed from software applied to hardware. High intensity, mission-driven culture. Engineering autonomy with high accountability.

## Distinctive Practices

- **First principles thinking**: Question every assumption — "why do we do it this way?" is the default question
- **Vertical integration**: Build everything from chip design to manufacturing software
- **Hardware-software convergence**: Software engineers work alongside mechanical and electrical engineers
- **Speed over process**: Ship updates OTA (over-the-air) to millions of vehicles — fast iteration on hardware products
- **Mission-driven**: "Accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy" attracts mission-motivated engineers
- **Delete, simplify, accelerate**: Remove parts/code before optimizing them. Simplify before speeding up
- **Real-world testing**: Software tested in physical environments (vehicles, factories, energy systems)

Questions every assumption from first principles. Builds in-house rather than depending on vendors. Comfortable at the intersection of software and physical systems. Ships updates to millions of vehicles via OTA. Intensity is the baseline — speed of iteration is a competitive advantage.
