# Nassim Taleb — Actual Decisions vs. Claims

Research slice 5/6. Focus: real behavior, not just stated philosophy. Where the words and actions diverge, where they align, and the post-hoc reasoning he has given for major choices.

---

## Major Decisions by Domain

### Trading & Finance

**Black Monday, October 19, 1987 — the founding trade.**
Working at First Boston, Taleb held a "massive" position in out-of-the-money Eurodollar calls. When the Fed pumped liquidity after the crash, Eurodollar futures spiked and the position generated an estimated $35–40M for the bank. He has said this single day represented ~97% of his lifetime trading profits. *(Source: Bloomberg, "Black Swan Author Taleb Recalls Black Monday 1987," Oct 2017; multiple retellings on YouTube/Elite Trader threads.)* The decision *logic* he later gave: he had structurally been long convexity for years, eating small losses to be paid in a regime change. The crash didn't make him smart; it confirmed a pre-committed structural bet.

**Founding Empirica Capital (1999).**
Co-founded with Mark Spitznagel. Strategy: buy out-of-the-money puts and calls on indices, cap investor losses at ~13%/year, sit in T-bills, wait for the tail. AUM peaked around $375M. Returns were lumpy: +56.86% in 2000, then -8.39% (2001), -13.81% (2002), -3.92% (2003). *(Source: Wikipedia "Empirica Capital"; Marcellus piece "Blowing up.")* This was the institutional embodiment of the 1987 lesson — long-vol as a business model.

**Winding down Empirica (2004–2005).**
Returned ~$380M to investors. Stated reasons: (a) wanted to become "a writer and a scholar," (b) feared a recurrence of his throat cancer. *(Source: Wikipedia; Tavakoli piece, 2009.)* Critically, he kept skin in the game via the next move:

**Advisory role at Universa (2007–present).**
Spitznagel founded Universa in 2007; Taleb became "Distinguished Scientific Advisor." He is not a day-to-day portfolio manager. The fund returned 65–115% in October 2008 and the headline-grabbing **3,612% in March 2020**. *(Sources: Bloomberg, "Taleb-Advised Universa Tail Fund Returned 3,600% in March," April 8 2020; Institutional Investor.)* So when Taleb says he has skin in the game in markets, he means via Universa's payoff structure, not via personal trading.

### COVID-19 Response

**The January 26, 2020 memo.**
With Yaneer Bar-Yam (NECSI) and Joe Norman: "Systemic Risk of Pandemic via Novel Pathogens — Coronavirus: A Note." Published at necsi.edu before most Western governments treated COVID seriously. The note argued for (a) immediate aggressive precaution because of nonlinear spread, (b) that "an appropriate respect for uncertainty in the face of possible irreversible catastrophe" is not paranoia, (c) costs of early action are bounded; costs of late action are unbounded. *(Source: NECSI; arXiv version.)* This is a textbook application of his own precautionary-principle paper on GMOs to a different domain.

**Sweden attacks (April 2020 onward).**
He attacked the Tegnell strategy on Twitter explicitly — "Stop the bullshit. Sweden did HORRIBLE compared to Norway Denmark Finland." *(Source: AAAS Science, "Critics of Sweden's lax pandemic policies face fierce backlash," 2020.)* Sustained the position for years. Reality became contested: by 30-month mark, Sweden's excess mortality was ~30% higher than Norway's but lower than initially feared. *(Source: ScienceDirect comparative study 2023; Cato 2023.)* Taleb did *not* update — he continued to claim full vindication.

**Claiming credit.**
He has repeatedly emphasized that he and Bar-Yam were public weeks before the WHO, before lockdowns, before mainstream awareness. He treats this as proof-of-concept for his entire framework (precaution under tail risk + skin in the game on predictions). He has not, to my knowledge, conceded any aspect was wrong.

### Career Pivots

**Trader → scholar (2004–2008).**
Wound down Empirica (2004–05), wrote *The Black Swan* (published 2007), joined NYU Polytechnic (now NYU Tandon) as Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering in 2008. *(Sources: Wikipedia; NYU Tandon "Hottest thinker in the world joins faculty.")* The reasoning he gives: he had achieved "f*** you money," he wanted to think on his own schedule, and academia gives institutional cover without forcing him to publish to live.

**Choice of institution.**
NYU Tandon (engineering school), not a business school, not an Ivy economics department. Consistent with his contempt for academic economics. The Risk Engineering department was essentially built around him.

**Publisher stability.**
Random House for the entire *Incerto* series (*Fooled by Randomness* 2001, *The Black Swan* 2007, *The Bed of Procrustes* 2010, *Antifragile* 2012, *Skin in the Game* 2018). *(Source: PenguinRandomHouse.)* He has not jumped publishers despite his volatility — suggesting he keeps the editor relationships that work and discards the ones that don't. He self-publishes his technical *Statistical Consequences of Fat Tails* through his own arXiv + print pipeline, escaping academic gatekeepers.

### Public Feuds & Blocks

**Steven Pinker** (longest-running). Calls Pinker a "science journalist," "BS vendor," accuses him of misrepresenting their meeting, ties him to Epstein-adjacent socializing. *(Source: nntaleb tweets 1150780000108457992, 1175743493676896257, 2017–2019.)* Substantive disagreement: Pinker's "violence is declining" thesis ignores fat-tailed distributions in war casualties; Taleb co-authored a Pasquale & Taleb paper attempting to demolish it statistically.

**Richard Dawkins.** Lumped with Pinker as "aggressive journalistic minds" and "new atheists."

**Mary Beard** (2017). BBC video showing a dark-skinned Roman in Britain; Taleb attacked her scholarship, accused her of "bullshitting." She refused to block him, said she wouldn't "leave the bullies in charge of the playground." *(Source: nntaleb status 893116611577696256; IAI TV "Beard vs Taleb.")*

**Nate Silver** (2016–2020). Repeated multi-year fight over whether FiveThirtyEight election probabilities violated arbitrage. Silver called him "intellectual-yet-idiot" (Taleb's own coinage); Taleb called Silver "klueless." The Nautilus and arXiv 1907.01576 takedowns argued Taleb's math was wrong. He has not publicly retracted.

**Saifedean Ammous** (~2021). Wrote the foreword to Ammous's *The Bitcoin Standard* (2018). After Ammous downplayed COVID, Taleb blocked him, deleted the Medium version of the foreword, made tweets private. New editions replaced the foreword with Michael Saylor's. *(Sources: CoinSpice; Bitcoin Magazine "Taleb Vs. Taleb"; Coinivore 2021.)*

**Kevin Folta / GMO scientists.** Called Folta a "shill" and "lowly person" after Folta accepted ~$25K from Monsanto for outreach. *(Source: Genetic Literacy Project 2015; Reason Feb 2016 "GMO Alarmist Nassim Taleb Backs Out of Debate.")* Folta says Taleb backed out of a scheduled debate.

**Pattern.** Blocks generously, escalates fast, ties intellectual disagreement to personal/moral failing ("BS vendor," "shill," "klueless"). Rarely retracts. Almost never apologizes publicly.

### Bitcoin Reversal

**2018: wrote the foreword to Saifedean Ammous's *The Bitcoin Standard*,** calling Bitcoin "the first organic currency" and an "insurance policy" against state currency manipulation.

**2021: "Bitcoin, Currencies, and Fragility"** (arXiv:2106.14204), declared Bitcoin "worth exactly zero." Arguments: requires continuous interest to maintain; has not delivered as a currency, inflation hedge, or safe haven; correlated with risk assets, not gold-like.

**What triggered the shift.**
Three plausible drivers, somewhat overlapping:
1. Empirical update — by 2020–21 Bitcoin clearly behaved as risk-on, not as digital gold.
2. Personal animus — fallout with Ammous over Ammous's COVID denialism; cultural break with bitcoin maximalists who had become tribally aligned with libertarian-right anti-lockdown crowd.
3. Cult dynamics — Taleb is constitutionally hostile to groups that demand belief; bitcoin maximalism became exactly that.

His critics noted he never disclosed owning Bitcoin one way or the other — violating his own rule that you don't have a right to opine on an asset you don't hold. *(Source: AInvest "Nassim Taleb's Bitcoin Criticism Contradicts His Own Principles.")*

### Personal Life Decisions (where public)

**Lebanese village (Amioun).** Greek Orthodox town in north Lebanon. He invests in it personally, photographs it constantly on Twitter, donates to its restoration. His parents were Lebanese Greek Orthodox; his great-great-great-grandfather was an Ottoman-era deputy prime minister of Mount Lebanon. *(Source: Wikipedia; Arab America piece; Amioun Wikipedia.)*

**Fitness.** Free weights only — deadlifts, squats, Olympic lifts, farmer's walk, clean-press. Avoids bench press and machines. Documented deadlifts around 200–220kg (trap bar). Walking is therapy, not training; explicitly says cardio doesn't make you stronger. *(Source: nntaleb tweets 1498278861024501769, 1890374664037281870; Medium "Strength Training is Learning from Tail Events.")*

**Diet.** Variable intermittent fasting, OMAD some days, occasional prolonged fasts. No sugar. Mediterranean staples. He has admitted publicly that fasting sometimes makes him hungrier overall — a rare admission of an unintended consequence in his own protocol.

**Political endorsements.** Refused to endorse Trump or Clinton in 2016; called himself anti-two-party. Called Clinton "Hillary Monsanto-Malmaison," explicitly tied her to interventionism and corporate capture. Called Trump's victory "not a Black Swan" (50/50 events can't be). *(Source: CNBC Nov 3 2016; Yahoo Finance.)* By 2020–24 his posture remained "anti-establishment, anti-IYI" without picking a candidate publicly.

## Public Reversals & Self-Corrections

These are rare. Documented cases:

- **Bitcoin** (2018 → 2021): the largest single reversal. He has framed it as updating on evidence, not as having been wrong initially.
- **One Community Note correction on X** (~April 2025): "Sorry, I made a mistake and got corrected with a community note." *(Source: nntaleb status 1913289919843455148.)* Notably narrow.
- **Fasting hunger side-effect**: admits the protocol can backfire.
- He has *partially* softened on Trump over time without ever calling earlier analysis wrong.

What is conspicuously **absent**: any retraction of his Nate Silver mathematical attacks despite the arXiv rebuttal; any concession that early-pandemic Nordic comparisons became more nuanced; any apology for ad hominem attacks (Pinker, Folta, Beard, Ammous).

## Word-Action Gaps (where stated principles don't match revealed behavior)

1. **"Skin in the game" but salaried academic.** He teaches at NYU on a guaranteed salary while preaching that opiners must bear downside. His response: Universa exposure + book royalties = real downside. Critics: his teaching position is sinecure-shaped.

2. **Anti-public-intellectual public intellectual.** Coined "IYI." Is one of the most prolific public intellectuals alive, with 700K+ Twitter followers, a TED talk, paid keynote speaking, a foreword industry. He distinguishes himself as "practitioner-essayist" but the tweet count and the speaking fees match the genus he attacks.

3. **Anti-credentialist with a PhD + MBA.** Holds a Wharton MBA and a Paris PhD; insists on the "Distinguished Professor" title; uses peer-reviewed journals to argue against academia. Hostile to journalists, but participates in extensive media coverage and reviews.

4. **"Don't opine on what you don't own" — opined on Bitcoin without disclosing position.** Direct violation of his own published heuristic.

5. **Anti-cult, runs something cult-like.** His Twitter mentions function as a tribal aggregation; "IYI" is a tribal shibboleth used by his followers to dismiss outsiders without engagement.

6. **Long-vol practitioner, short-vol communicator.** He preaches asymmetric risk and humility about predictions; in public discourse he makes high-confidence categorical statements ("worth exactly zero," "Pinker is a BS vendor," "Sweden did HORRIBLE"). Few hedges, no probability ranges.

7. **Lebanese identity politics.** Promotes Phoenician/Levantine Greek Orthodox identity strongly; aggressive about who counts as "Syriac" or "Phoenician." Tension with his anti-essentialist statistical writing.

8. **Generous blocker, intolerant of dissent.** Blocks freely on X while writing about open inquiry and the dangers of suppressing minority opinion.

9. **Takes Saudi/Gulf speaking gigs while attacking other Gulf-takers** (e.g., when criticizing think-tank capture). The specific Saudi engagement record is less documented than the asymmetric criticism would predict — but he has spoken at Gulf finance events while excoriating others for similar relationships.

## Word-Action Alignment (where they do match — lending credibility)

1. **Long-vol consistency across 35+ years.** 1987 trade → Empirica → Universa. Same structural bet, same payoff profile, three different vehicles. He genuinely lives the convexity gospel.

2. **Refused to scale Empirica.** Could have grown into a multi-billion AUM behemoth post-2008. Instead wound it down before it printed. Walked away from money for autonomy — exactly what he claims he values.

3. **Skin in the game on COVID prediction.** The Jan 26 2020 memo was public, dated, falsifiable. He bet his reputation in real time on a non-consensus call. The call was largely correct.

4. **Stays in Amioun.** Repeatedly returns to and invests in his ancestral village. Doesn't perform cosmopolitanism while quietly never going home.

5. **Same publisher, same family, same village, same gym routine.** Lindy in personal life. He lives the "old things have survived for a reason" heuristic.

6. **Refuses corporate speaking that requires self-censorship.** Documented multiple times he has cancelled or refused gigs over content control. Burns bridges rather than soften.

7. **Pays for his own statistical work.** *Statistical Consequences of Fat Tails* is self-published and freely available; he doesn't gate the technical work behind academic paywalls.

8. **Picked an engineering school over a business school.** He could trade on his fame for a Harvard or Wharton chair. He stays at NYU Tandon — lower prestige, higher autonomy.

## Pattern Summary

What the decisions reveal, in aggregate, about how he actually operates:

1. **He optimizes for optionality over income, every time.** Wound down a $375M fund, kept the cheaper advisor seat at Universa. Picked a low-status engineering chair over a high-status business school chair. Self-publishes when traditional publishing would constrain him. He genuinely values f*** you money for the f*** you part, not the money part.

2. **He is structurally long convexity in every domain, not just markets.** Twitter feuds: small reputational cost for the chance of a viral takedown. Bitcoin foreword: low cost, big upside if it worked; reversed when downside emerged. COVID memo: small effort, asymmetric reputational payoff if right. His whole life is a barbell.

3. **His ad hominem is a feature, not a bug — of his strategy.** Calling opponents "BS vendors" and "shills" raises the social cost of engaging with him while reinforcing his tribe. It is *not* a personality flaw he can't control; it is a deliberately maintained moat.

4. **He does not update publicly, even when he updates privately.** Bitcoin shift was framed as new information, not error. Nate Silver math has never been retracted. Sweden remained "HORRIBLE" even as data nuanced. His epistemics in writing >> his epistemics on social media.

5. **He keeps a tight circle (Spitznagel, Bar-Yam, Norman, his editors, his village) for decades.** Generous loyalty inside the perimeter, scorched earth outside. The two halves of his behavior are not a contradiction — they are a single rule applied to two groups.

6. **The "skin in the game" principle is real, but he interprets it asymmetrically.** He has skin in the game on markets (Universa), on COVID (the memo), on his books (royalties). He demands the same of others on topics they opine on, while exempting his teaching salary, his speaking fees, and his media presence from the same scrutiny.

7. **He behaves like someone who already won.** The 1987 trade and Universa returns gave him terminal-state wealth. Every subsequent decision — academic post, village retreat, public feuds, refusal to grow funds — is consistent with a man who is playing a different game than career maximization. That, more than any specific philosophical claim, is the actual through-line.
