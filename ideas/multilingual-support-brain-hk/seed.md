# Seed — Multilingual customer-support Company Brain for HK / Greater-China SMBs running chatbots

> The chosen card from the Company Brain slate. Input to `/sharpen-hypothesis` — a starting hypothesis to
> TEST, not a proven idea. Full slate + the other 9 backlog cards: `ideas/_exploration/company-brain/slate.md`.
> Grounding evidence: `ideas/_exploration/company-brain/grounding.md`.

## Problem
SMBs across HK / Greater China already run customer-facing chatbots (often multilingual) that answer from
stale, scattered, tribal knowledge — so they hallucinate prices, policies, and procedures in Cantonese /
Mandarin / English. They need a living, structured **company brain** that keeps the bot's answers current
and *correct*, in their actual languages — not an English-first enterprise tool bolted onto a market it
doesn't fit.

## Who (the reachable niche)
Owner-operators / support leads at **10–200-person HK + Greater-China SMBs already operating a customer
chatbot or support desk.** Reachable in-language; several plausibly warm via the founder's ex-AI-Momentum
multi-client chatbot-CMS network.

## Why now
- Support is the **sharpest unmet wedge** in the grounding research; SMB AI investment jumped **42%→57%**
  (2024→2025) — these teams are actively buying.
- The English-first incumbents (Glean $60K/yr min, Dust, Guru $250/mo floor) **structurally underserve**
  Cantonese/Mandarin SMBs *and* are too pricey.
- Chatbot adoption in the region is high while **the company-brain layer feeding those bots doesn't exist
  yet** — YC named the category (Blomfield's "Company Brain" RFS) but the funded teams are chasing English
  enterprise, leaving this segment open ~12–18 months.
- Delivery rails exist: MCP (97M monthly downloads) + the `SKILL.md`/`AGENTS.md` standard.

## Why this idea was the pick (moat + distribution read)
The **only card on the slate that answers *what channel do you own?*** — the founder's documented #1 risk.
It fuses the three AI-era moats:
- **Workflow depth** — the founder maintained a multi-client chatbot CMS; he knows this operator's day.
- **Proprietary access** — ex-employer network + local-language buyers the English-first incumbents won't chase.
- **Owned distribution** — a trilingual, locally-embedded channel, not "we'll do ads."

Lightly regulated, low-cost, solo-AI-buildable — squarely the founder's stated lane.

## Recommended go-to-market motion
**Start concierge** (slate card #6's motion): hand-extract one SMB's support knowledge into a structured,
agent-runnable map *by hand* — no product code — to learn the workflow and prove willingness-to-pay first.

## Riskiest assumption to test first (carry into sharpening + discovery)
**Can the founder actually reach and close these SMBs, and will they pay?** This is the founder's honest
network gap ("limited/none right now") — so it must be validated in customer discovery, *not* assumed away
in code. A secondary open question: do these SMBs perceive bot-answer staleness as a *paid* problem, or a
tolerated annoyance?

## Doctrine flags carried forward
- Distribution is the load-bearing risk — treat reachability of the HK/Greater-China SMB channel as the
  hypothesis to disconfirm, not a given.
- Don't drift back into a horizontal English SaaS; the entire moat is the language + local embeddedness.
