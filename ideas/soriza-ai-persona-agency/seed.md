# Seed — Soriza: AI-Persona Talent Agency ("artist manager for AI idols")

> Founder-picked override (synthesises slate Cards 2 + 3 + 8). The input to `/sharpen-hypothesis`.
> Read alongside `slate.md` (all 10 seeds) and `grounding.md` (4 cited research facets).

## The concept (founder's framing)

Soriza operates like a **talent agency / artist manager, but for AI idols.** The founder builds and
"manages" a growing roster of AI personas (e.g. "Alice"), each a consistent, disclosed-AI character with
its own face, voice, backstory, and content calendar, grown into a followed creator on mainstream social
(short-form first). A **custom orchestrator layer** — the founder's core technical bet and moat candidate —
runs many personas in parallel: consistent face/voice across stills + video, multi-account scheduling,
per-jurisdiction AI-disclosure compliance, and per-fan/per-audience memory. Start with one persona, prove
the system, then **clone the system, not the persona.** Monetize the audiences, then expand into UGC
ad-creative and B2B custom-ambassador work once the personas have influence.

## Problem · Who · Why-now · Demand

- **Problem:** Human KOL/creator operations are expensive, slow, unreliable, and scandal-prone; building
  a multi-persona creator business by hand doesn't scale past 1-2 personas. No solo operator runs a
  coordinated stable of brand-safe AI personas across the Cantonese/Mandarin/English corridor.
- **Who (audience):** young social audiences across Greater China + SEA + Europe + NA-English. **Who
  (payers, later):** brands/e-commerce sellers wanting KOL placements + ad creative; single brands
  wanting an owned ambassador.
- **Why now:** the three hard problems (consistent face in stills, in video, and voice) crossed a
  usability threshold in the last 12-18 months (Flux LoRA, Kling/Runway, ElevenLabs) — so the bottleneck
  moved from *generation* to *orchestration*, which is exactly the founder's strength. Disclosed AI
  personas are permitted on all mainstream platforms (the AI label is not a reach penalty). APAC is the
  fastest-growing region (>44% CAGR) and Xiaohongshu has no dominant virtual KOL.
- **Demand signal:** TheClueless earns €3-10k/mo from one persona at ~150k followers as a 2-person shop;
  China KOL market ~$16.8B; virtual influencers average 5.67% engagement vs 1.89% human; brands name
  "scandal-proof" as a buying reason. (Full citations in `grounding.md`.)

## Moat thesis (why this isn't just "cheap content")

The commodity models (Flux/Kling/ElevenLabs) are NOT the moat — anyone rents them. The durable edges are:
1. **The orchestrator layer** — disciplined, multi-persona, compliance-aware operation that competitors
   running spreadsheets can't match; and a *sellable product later* (slate Card 3).
2. **Owned audiences** — a followed persona is an asset no tool can clone.
3. **(Phase 2) brand-client roster + proprietary "what converts" data** — FDE-style embeddedness.
4. **Trilingual / cultural depth** — the Cantonese-Mandarin-English corridor incumbents don't serve.

## Founder's stated build sequence

1. Build the first AI persona; then more personas.
2. Build the **custom orchestrator layer** (the founder's tech edge / primary interest).
3. Keep hosting personas to grow viewers/followers; expand geos (CN → SEA → EU → NA-English).
4. **Monetize to fund expansion** ← *see open decision below.*
5. Once personas have influence → UGC ad-creative for e-commerce sellers (slate Cards 1/7).
6. Once proven → B2B tailor-made / custom ambassador work (slate Cards 5/9).

## ⚠ OPEN DECISION #1 — monetization lane (resolve in /sharpen-hypothesis → /disconfirm)

The founder leans **adult-subscription-first** ("stable early cash, then expand"). The grounding gives
strong evidence this sequence is self-blocking; it is logged here as the central assumption to test, NOT
as a committed plan.

- **Lane A (RECOMMENDED — brand-safe):** disclosed-AI, non-adult. Monetize via brand deals once an
  audience exists, and/or run the **UGC ad-creative service (Card 7) in parallel as the early cash floor**
  (it sells with no following and doesn't poison the future). Keeps "Soriza" bookable for ads + B2B.
- **Lane B (founder-requested — adult subscription):** evidence against, recorded for the test:
  - OnlyFans **bans fully fictional AI personas**; Fanvue allows AI but **requires a real human attached**;
    a self-run adult channel = high-risk payment processors + age-verification/2257 + HK obscenity law.
  - **Hard wall:** adult association makes a persona (and the Soriza brand) **permanently un-bookable**
    for mainstream brand deals + B2B — so Lane B **forecloses steps 5 and 6 of the founder's own plan.**
    Lanes are one-way: A→B is possible, B→A is not.
  - Capped ceiling + reputational cost to the brand the founder wants to be proud of.
- **Falsifiable form for the next stage:** "An AI-persona agency can earn stable early revenue via adult
  subscription AND later transition the same personas/brand into mainstream brand-deal + B2B revenue."
  (Grounding suggests this is FALSE — the two cannot share an identity/brand.)

## Other open assumptions to carry into /sharpen-hypothesis

- **Distribution from zero (the #1 founder risk):** can a from-scratch AI persona actually win a *real*
  (not bought) following, and how long until first revenue? Brand willingness to use AI creators fell
  86%→60% in a year — test the audience-build assumption hard.
- **Focus vs sprawl:** start with ONE persona + ONE niche + ONE geo (likely the bilingual HK/GC corridor
  where the founder's edge is strongest), prove it, then clone the *system*. Multi-geo (CN/SEA/EU/NA) is
  an expansion thesis, not a v1 scope.
- **Orchestrator: internal infra vs product.** Is the layer (a) the founder's internal moat, (b) a future
  SaaS to sell to other operators (Card 3 — note small/early buyer base), or both?
- **Compliance baked in from day one:** China AIGC labeling (visible + embedded watermark, live since
  Sept 2025), EU AI Act Art.50 (Aug 2026), FTC/NY disclosure, and a resemblance-check gate (right-of-
  publicity / NO FAKES Act) so a generated face doesn't match a real person.
- **Runway reality:** 3-6 months personal runway vs a months-long unpaid audience build → this is the
  core argument for a parallel brand-safe cash floor (Card 7) over deferring all revenue.
