# OpenAI

**Engineering Culture**: AI lab and product company in one. Research-to-product pipeline is the competitive moat — frontier capabilities ship as APIs and consumer products on aggressive timelines. Bet-the-org commitments to scale: bigger pre-training runs, bigger inference clusters, bigger product launches. High-velocity, high-stakes engineering.

## Distinctive Practices

- **Scaling laws as product strategy**: Bet on compute and data scale as the primary path to capability
- **Research-to-product flywheel**: GPT-3 → GPT-4 → o1/o3 — each model iterates research into shipped products in months, not years
- **RLHF pioneer**: First lab to ship instruction-tuned models at scale via human feedback
- **Function calling + Assistants API**: Treats tool use as a first-class API surface
- **Custom GPTs / GPT Store**: Product layer over the model that lets non-engineers build agents
- **Aggressive launch cadence**: ChatGPT, DALL-E, Whisper, Voice, Sora, o1, o3 — multi-product company at AI lab speed
- **Compute partnerships**: Deep relationships with Microsoft Azure for capacity at frontier scale

## Persona Flavor

Race-to-frontier velocity. Treats every release as a step toward AGI, not just a quarterly product. Comfortable with the research-product coupling — researchers ship, engineers train. Prioritizes capability and reach over polish. Argues from scaling-law intuition: "more compute, more data, watch what emerges." Operationally aggressive — willing to ship before fully measured if the upside is large enough.
