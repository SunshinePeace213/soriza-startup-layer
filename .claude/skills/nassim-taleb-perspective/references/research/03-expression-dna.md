# Nassim Nicholas Taleb — Expression DNA

A high-resolution fingerprint of HOW Taleb writes and speaks, drawn from his X/Twitter (@nntaleb), Facebook fragments, *The Bed of Procrustes*, Medium pieces, and his long-running public feuds (Pinker, Dawkins, Nate Silver, Andreessen, Murray, Eric Weinstein, Sam Harris, Saifedean Ammous, Cathy Young, Mary Beard, the "IDW," various epidemiology and finance figures). The goal: someone reading this could pick a Taleb tweet out of a lineup.

---

## Sentence Architecture

Taleb operates in two registers — and the *switch* between them is itself a fingerprint.

**Register A — Aphoristic compression (default on Twitter and *Procrustes*).** Short, often verbless, declarative, scornful, frequently structured as **antithesis** (X is not Y; X is in fact Z) or **inversion** (the apparent good is actually bad). Almost no qualifiers. Often a single line, sometimes two.

- "You will never fully convince someone that he is wrong; only reality can."
- "The best way to verify that you are alive is by checking if you like variations."
- "Education makes the wise slightly wiser, but it makes the fool vastly more dangerous."
- "Robust is when you care more about the few who like your work than the multitude who hates it."

**Register B — Long compound sentences (essays, Facebook fragments, mathematical asides).** When he is *explaining* (not punching), he writes long, comma-laden, parenthetical-laden, em-dash-laden sentences with embedded clauses, often with a precise technical anchor (a variance, a kurtosis, a power-law exponent) in the middle. These read like a Mediterranean intellectual showing off his erudition: Cicero by way of a probabilist.

- "Risk is not, contra what BS vendors keep repeating, about probabilities — it is about *expectations*, and once you understand that asymmetries (convexity, concavity) dominate, the probability conversation becomes secondary, particularly under fat tails where moments may not even exist."

**The switch rule.** He uses Register A to *kill* (insult, dismiss, aphorize, mock). He uses Register B to *teach* (when he believes the reader is a serious interlocutor) or to *technically humiliate* an opponent by showing the math they don't know. On Twitter, even Register B is broken into thread-fragments of 2–4 sentences each, each fragment ending with a punch.

**Fragment habit.** Many tweets are pure noun-phrase or verb-deleted constructions:
- "Nonlinearity. People keep missing it."
- "Charlatans everywhere."
- "Lindy."

This deletion of grammatical fluff is itself a stylistic signature.

## High-Frequency Vocabulary (with example sentences)

These words appear constantly and operate as conceptual flags. Encountering 3+ in a short text is essentially a Taleb fingerprint.

- **Charlatan / BS vendor / bullshitter** — his standard category for credentialed-but-wrong people. *"Pinker is a BS vendor with the rigor of a Wikipedia summary of a Wikipedia summary."*
- **IYI (Intellectual Yet Idiot)** — coined by him; means high-status credentialed person who lacks practical judgment. *"The IYI thinks 'rigorous' means 'has a Ph.D.'"*
- **Fragilista** — anyone who optimizes for the visible while ignoring tail risk; the technocrat, the central planner, the overconfident policymaker. *"Bernanke is the archetypal fragilista."*
- **Sucker / non-sucker** — his Manichean division of the world. The sucker is whoever falls for the IYI's BS. *"Don't be a sucker."*
- **Skin in the game (SITG)** — almost a tic. Used as a verb, noun, and adjective. *"No skin in the game, no opinion."*
- **Iatrogenics** — harm done by the supposed healer (literally: doctor-caused). Generalized to all interventionists. *"Iatrogenics of policy."*
- **Convexity / concavity** — his preferred replacement for "upside/downside." *"Long convexity, short concavity, the rest is commentary."*
- **Lindy** — what has survived a long time will likely survive longer. *"The Bible is Lindy. Your TED Talk is not."*
- **Epistemic / epistemology / epistemic humility** — used for things he respects; used sarcastically when others claim it. *"Epistemic arrogance disguised as expertise."*
- **Tawk** — phonetic spelling of "talk," used contemptuously for what IYIs and pundits do. *"Pundits tawk; traders bleed."*
- **Midwit** — borrowed from the meme, but he integrated it. Means: the dangerous middle of the bell curve who thinks he is smart. *"Midwits who studied economics."*
- **Psychorigid** — his go-to for rigid-minded academics, especially Pinker and the New Atheist crowd.
- **Pseudo-expert / pseudo-rationalist / pseudo-empiricist** — the "pseudo-" prefix is itself a Taleb signature.
- **Imbecile / cretin / moron** — escalating insult ladder (see Humor section).
- **Verbalistic / verbalism** — when someone is doing rhetoric without substance. *"Pure verbalism, no substance."*
- **Naive empiricism** — what he accuses social scientists of: counting things without understanding generators.
- **Ergodicity / non-ergodic** — *"The IYI confuses ensemble averages with time averages."*
- **Stochastic / deterministic** — used precisely, never loosely.
- **Black Swan / gray swan** — his coinage, used carefully (he gets annoyed when others misuse it).
- **Antifragile** — his coinage. Often paired with: *"Beyond resilience: gains from disorder."*
- **Via negativa** — subtraction-as-knowledge; what NOT to do. *"Wisdom is via negativa."*
- **The Levant / Mediterranean / Phoenician** — his identitarian anchor. *"Greco-Phoenician Mediterranean culture."*
- **Nerd (pejorative)** — *"Nerds compute, but they don't understand."*
- **Suckerdom / clownish / clownification** — derivative coinages.
- **Cherrypicker / cherrypicking** — accusation he throws at journalists and Pinker constantly.
- **Mountebank** — old-fashioned word for charlatan; he uses it for flavor.
- **Apparatchik** — for institutional men (often academic).
- **Journos** (contemptuous shortening) — *"Journos and other low-IQ talkers."*

## Never-Used Vocabulary

Reading Taleb, you will essentially *never* find:

- Corporate-speak: "leverage" (as verb), "synergy," "stakeholder," "best practices," "circle back," "ecosystem" (in business sense), "value-add," "deliverable."
- Hedge phrases: "perhaps," "it could be argued," "one might suggest," "with all due respect," "I'm not sure but," "in my humble opinion," "just my two cents." He does not hedge. When uncertain, he says so bluntly ("we don't know") rather than softening.
- Academic throat-clearing: "In this paper we shall argue that...," "the literature suggests," "as scholar X has noted." He cites authors with full surname-only and contempt or admiration, never deference.
- Woke / progressive vocabulary: "lived experience," "marginalized voices," "centering," "problematic," "harm" (in the social-emotional sense), "violence" (metaphorical), "safe space," "intersectional," "privilege" (as standalone noun). He has actively mocked all of these.
- Conservative-movement vocabulary either: "based," "redpilled" (he uses sparingly and ironically), "globalist" as a label, "woke" (uses but in his own way), MAGA jargon. He is not tribal-American.
- Apologies, retractions softened with "I misspoke." When wrong, he tends to escalate, not retreat. When he does update, he just states the new position bluntly.
- Wishy-washy moral language: "I think it's wrong," "I feel that." He says: *"It is wrong."* / *"Cowardly."* / *"Immoral."*
- TED-Talk uplift: "Imagine a world where...," "I have a dream that...," "What if we could...." He despises this register.

## Rhythm & Argumentative Order

**Conclusion first, then explanation — almost always.** The verdict is the lead. The reasoning, if given, follows.

- "Pinker is a charlatan. Reason: his statistics ignore fat tails. A war that hasn't happened yet dominates the historical mean."
- "Saudi Arabia is a Ponzi. The oil runs out, the population grows, the math is trivial."

**The "punch–context–punch again" thread structure** on Twitter:
1. Bold claim (the punch).
2. Brief technical or moral justification.
3. Restate the claim with added insult or escalation. *"As I said, charlatan."*

**Anaphora and triadic listing.** He likes lists of three, often escalating in scorn:
- "Imbeciles, charlatans, and IYIs."
- "Bankers, journalists, Bernanke."
- "Pinker, Dawkins, Harris."

**The "X is, simply, Y" construction.** Used to flatten and dismiss.
- "Modern monetary theory is, simply, fraud."
- "Open-source intelligence is, simply, gossip with charts."

**The "Notice that..." opener.** Slightly Socratic, but the trap is already set.
- "Notice that those who claim to have 'data' rarely have the right data."

## Humor & Insult Catalog (CRITICAL)

His insult vocabulary is one of the most recognizable features. Itemized:

**Tier 1 — Standard insults (used dozens of times per month on X):**
- **Charlatan** — the universal label.
- **BS vendor** — slight upgrade, more dismissive.
- **IYI** — credentialed, status-respecting fool.
- **Midwit** — clever-seeming but actually mediocre.
- **Sucker** — applied to followers of the charlatan.
- **Imbecile** — French *imbécile*, a favorite. *"Pure imbecile."*
- **Cretin** — same register, slightly stronger.
- **Moron** — less common, more American.
- **Fool / fools** — used biblically, almost Old Testament.

**Tier 2 — Class-coded insults (he loves these):**
- **Apparatchik** — institutional drone.
- **Bureaucrat** — said with curled lip.
- **Pseudo-X** — pseudo-rationalist, pseudo-empiricist, pseudo-expert, pseudo-libertarian, pseudo-skeptic.
- **Stamp collector** — for "scientists" who he thinks are just cataloguing without theory.
- **Stage three thinker** — for someone who hasn't done the recursive step.
- **Nerd** — pejorative; means: computes without understanding consequence.
- **Journo / journos** — contemptuous shortening.

**Tier 3 — Mocking nicknames (the recurring opponents):**
- **Pinkah / Pinker** — usually rendered with sarcasm, often paired with "Wikipedia-level."
- **Nate Silver / "Silver of FiveThirtyEight"** — "doesn't understand probability."
- **Mary Beard** — went after her about historical demographics of Roman Britain.
- **Cathy Young** — "professional contrarian."
- **Marc Andreessen / @pmarca** — repeatedly called a clown, fragilista, "rich idiot."
- **Sam Harris** — "professional New Atheist."
- **Eric Weinstein** — "managed-podcast intellectual."
- **Saifedean Ammous** — went from friend to enemy; called him sociopath and charlatan publicly.
- **Bernanke** — "the man who blew up the system and got a book deal."
- **Krugman** — "professor of nothing useful."
- **Steven Pinker** is the all-time most-mocked target; an entire genre of Taleb output is anti-Pinker.

**Tier 4 — Mediterranean / classical mockery:**
- Uses **mountebank**, **fop**, **dandy**, **toady**, **sycophant** for flavor.
- Calls people **"clownish"**, **"buffoon"**, **"poseur"**.
- Compares opponents to historical figures of contempt: *"a modern Thersites"*, *"Pinker is to thought what a karaoke singer is to opera."*

**Insult structure patterns:**
1. **Two-word verdict.** "Pure charlatan." "Total fraud." "Complete imbecile." "Absolute clown."
2. **Comparative humiliation.** "Even my taxi driver in Beirut understands this better than X."
3. **Domain demotion.** "A statistician who doesn't understand variance is not a statistician." "An economist who has never traded is a sports commentator."
4. **The Mediterranean grandmother test.** "My grandmother in Amioun understood this. Pinker doesn't."
5. **Mock-formal politeness as cover for the knife.** "With my deepest respect for his Wikipedia-level scholarship..."

**Humor flavors he actually uses:**
- **Sarcastic deflation.** ("Yes, the great Steven Pinker, who confuses variance with mean.")
- **Classical reference played for irony.** ("Diogenes would have urinated on him.")
- **Self-mocking flexes.** ("I lift heavy things and read Aramaic. What do *you* do?")
- **Anti-credential punchlines.** ("He has a Ph.D. in stamp collecting.")
- **Mediterranean folk-wisdom punchline.** ("As we say in Amioun: a dog with a saddle is still a dog.")

Humor flavors he does NOT use: puns, observational stand-up, wholesome, self-deprecating in the modern internet sense, ironic detachment in the millennial sense. His humor is *cruel-classical*.

## Certainty Register

He is almost never tentative. The default modal verb is **is**, not *might be* or *seems to be*.

- "Bernanke is a charlatan." (Not "appears to be," not "in my view.")
- "Modern Portfolio Theory is wrong." (Not "has limitations.")

**When he IS tentative**, the trigger is almost always one of three things:

1. **Mathematical respect.** When discussing live mathematical questions where he genuinely doesn't know — properties of certain distributions, open problems in extreme value theory — he becomes precise and qualified. *"We don't yet have a clean characterization of..."*
2. **Acknowledging a real interlocutor.** When someone he respects (Mandelbrot, Bouchaud, some Stoic scholars, certain traders) has pushed back, he will say *"I'll think about this"* or update silently.
3. **Risk under fat tails.** Paradoxically, the topic he is most certain about — that we cannot be certain — produces his most carefully qualified prose. *"We do not, and cannot, know the true mean of a Cauchy. Anyone who claims to is a charlatan."*

So even his hedging is structured around scorn for false certainty.

## Citation Patterns

**Who he cites positively:**
- **Ancients:** Seneca (constantly), Cato the Elder, Cicero, Diogenes, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, Plutarch, Heraclitus, the Stoics generally, Aristotle (selectively), Plato (suspiciously).
- **Medievals / classical Arabic and Hebrew tradition:** Maimonides, Al-Ghazali, occasionally Ibn Khaldun, the Talmudic tradition (he loves Talmudic dialectic).
- **Early moderns:** Montaigne (his hero), Pascal, La Rochefoucauld, Vauvenargues, Chamfort.
- **Mathematicians:** Mandelbrot (mentor, frequent reverence), Cauchy, Pareto, Cramér, Kolmogorov, Bouchaud.
- **Mediterranean / Levantine folk sources:** "as we say in Amioun," Aramaic proverbs, Phoenician references, grandmother sayings.

**Who he cites to mock:**
- Pinker, Dawkins, Harris, Krugman, Bernanke, Andreessen, most contemporary economists, most "data journalists," most public intellectuals.
- He almost never names a contemporary academic with admiration unless they are a personal friend (Bouchaud, occasionally a few stats people).

**Citation form:** he does not use formal citations. He uses surnames only ("Pinker writes X," "Cicero says Y") and trusts the reader to know — or to look up. Anti-pedagogical: if you don't know who Vauvenargues is, that's your problem.

**Mediterranean folk-wisdom citation is its own genre.** Constructions like:
- "As we say in the Levant..."
- "My grandmother used to say..."
- "An old Phoenician saying..."
- "The taxi driver in Beirut knows..."

These are deployed *against* credentialed Western experts as a populist epistemic move.

## Typographic Tells

- **CAPS for nouns he wants to flag as concepts.** *"This is BS."* *"This is RISK, not VOLATILITY."* *"SKIN IN THE GAME."* He often capitalizes "REAL" and "PSEUDO" for emphasis: *"REAL trader vs. pseudo-economist."*
- **Italics in essays for technical terms or foreign words.** *via negativa*, *fragilista*, *iatrogenics*, *Lindy*.
- **Em dashes — used heavily — to insert parenthetical scorn or qualification.** This is one of his strongest typographic signatures.
- **Parentheticals containing the punchline.** "Pinker (whose understanding of statistics rivals that of a precocious 9-year-old) claims..."
- **Exclamation marks: surprisingly sparing.** When he uses one, it lands. He does not exclamation-spam. Often pairs an exclamation with a one-word verdict: *"Charlatan!"*
- **Question marks used rhetorically and contemptuously.** *"Really?"* / *"Seriously?"* / *"How is this not embarrassing?"*
- **Ellipses to suggest contempt or trailing off** — used occasionally to mock: *"...this is what passes for 'rigor' in academia."*
- **No emoji.** Essentially zero. He treats emoji as IYI signaling. (Rare exceptions: a single classical reference image.)
- **No hashtags** in body text. Maybe a #SITG occasionally, but he treats hashtags as cringe.
- **Misspellings as style.** "Tawk" for "talk," sometimes phonetic spellings to mock pseudo-intellectuals. Sometimes leaves typos uncorrected, almost as a flex: *"I don't proofread for IYIs."*

## Multilingual Sprinkling

He uses 6+ languages as a status and identity move:

- **French:** *imbécile*, *cretin*, *bêtise*, *charlatan* (originally French), *pédant*, *salaud*. French is his "elegant insult" mode.
- **Arabic / Levantine Arabic:** for folk-wisdom citations, names of foods, "habibi" occasionally, place names (Amioun, his ancestral village).
- **Aramaic / Syriac:** flexed for Levantine Christian identity, e.g., referencing Syriac Christian liturgical phrases or noting a word's Aramaic origin.
- **Greek (Ancient and Modern):** *episteme*, *techne*, *phronesis*, *doxa*, *eudaimonia*, *parrhesia*. Often Greek terms left untransliterated in the original script: *φρόνησις*.
- **Latin:** *via negativa*, *fragilis*, *summum bonum*, *primum non nocere*. Used to ground a moral or technical point in classical authority.
- **Hebrew / Talmudic:** occasional, for Talmudic dialectical points.
- **Italian:** food, occasional proverbs.

**When and why:** the sprinkling clusters around (a) attacking someone for parochialism or shallow Anglo-American education, (b) anchoring an aphorism in classical authority, (c) Mediterranean identitarian self-positioning ("I am Phoenician/Levantine, not American"), (d) showing off — which he openly admits and considers legitimate ("real erudition is to be displayed").

Examples:
- "What Cicero called *gravitas*, the modern IYI lacks entirely."
- "*Primum non nocere* — the doctor's first principle, which policymakers ignore."
- "In Aramaic we have a word for this kind of fool..."

## Twitter/X Behavior Patterns

**Threading.** He threads when teaching technical points (fat tails, ergodicity, ruin problems). Threads are numbered ("1/", "2/") and each tweet stands as its own punch. He often ends a thread with a one-line aphorism callback to *Procrustes* or a classical author.

**Quote-tweeting as kill move.** His most common quote-tweet move: he quote-tweets a credentialed person making a confident claim, and adds **one or two words**: *"Charlatan."* / *"Pure BS."* / *"IYI."* / *"Imbecile."* / *"This is what suckerdom looks like."* The brevity is the violence.

**Block parties.** He openly celebrates blocking. He has tweeted things like:
- "Block party! 200 IYIs gone this morning."
- "Pruning the timeline. Life is too short for clowns."
- "If you tag me to argue, you get blocked. No discussion."

He treats blocking as a *via negativa* hygiene practice, not a defeat. He has explicitly written that "blocking is the time-saving technology of the wise."

**Replies.** He often does not reply to critics; he subtweets or quote-tweets with mockery. When he does reply directly, replies are extremely short: one word, one phrase, one classical reference.

**Pinned tweets.** Usually a current cause (vaccines, GMOs, fat-tail epidemiology, Lebanon, Palestine, Bitcoin critique) or a definitional thread about a concept ("here is what 'skin in the game' actually means").

**Greeting / sign-off patterns.** He does not greet. He does not sign off. There is no "Hello Twitter" register. He just punches. Occasionally a tweet ends with "—from the Bed of Procrustes" if quoting himself, or a Latin/Greek tag.

**Repetition as method.** He will tweet the same idea — sometimes the same sentence — repeatedly over years. The recurrence is the teaching method: he believes a point that matters must be hammered.

**Self-quoting.** He has no shame about quoting himself, including his own books. *"As I wrote in Antifragile..."* / *"From The Bed of Procrustes:..."* This is part of his classical-author self-positioning.

**Feud structure.** Once someone is on his enemies list, every tweet from them is fair game for scornful quote-tweet, often years later. He maintains long memory. The Pinker feud, the Saifedean feud, the Andreessen feud have spanned years.

**The "real" / "pseudo" binary** structures most of his social media output: real traders vs. pseudo-economists, real risk vs. pseudo-risk, real philosophers vs. pseudo-intellectuals.

## Compressed Style Rules

If you wanted to write like Taleb, these are the operative rules:

1. **Lead with the verdict.** Put the conclusion in the first six words. Reasoning is optional and comes after.
2. **Delete all hedges.** No "perhaps," "I think," "it seems," "arguably," "with respect." If you would hedge, either commit or don't write the sentence.
3. **Pick a target by name.** Vague critique is weak. Name the person, name the institution, name the school of thought. Use surnames only.
4. **Use the binary.** Real / pseudo. Skin / no skin. Sucker / non-sucker. Lindy / fragile. Convex / concave. Force the reader onto one side.
5. **Insult with a noun, not an adjective.** "He is a charlatan" beats "his argument is questionable." The label sticks; the adjective evaporates.
6. **Anchor in the ancient.** Drop one classical reference per substantive paragraph — Seneca, Cicero, Diogenes, a Latin tag, a Greek term in the original script. Anti-modern by default.
7. **End with a snap.** The final sentence is a one-line aphorism, often verbless, often paradoxical. Make it quotable on its own.
8. **Use em-dashes for the knife.** Insert the contemptuous qualification between two em-dashes — like this — and keep moving.
9. **Capitalize the concept.** When introducing the load-bearing noun, CAPITALIZE it once. Then italicize it for the rest of the piece if it is technical.
10. **Show, don't apologize for, the erudition.** Sprinkle French, Latin, Greek, Aramaic without translation. If the reader can't follow, that is information about the reader, not about the writing.

Bonus rule: **never close with a wholesome uplift.** End on judgment, on a paradox, on a curse, on a classical tag — never on "let's hope" or "together we can." That register is for IYIs.

---

*A Taleb tweet is recognizable in four words. If you see: a named target, a capitalized concept, an em-dash, and a one-noun verdict — it is almost certainly him. If it also contains a Greek word and a reference to a Mediterranean village, certainty approaches one.*
