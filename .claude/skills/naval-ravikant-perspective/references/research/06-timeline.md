## Thought-evolution milestones

Chronological list of when key ideas became public.

- **2007** — **Venture Hacks blog** launches. The first time Naval publicly articulates "VCs negotiate this for a living; you negotiate this once — so let me show you the asymmetric information." Origin point of the "be on the founder's side" doctrine.
- **2010** — **AngelList launch** is itself the thesis: capital should be a marketplace, not a club.
- **2011–2017** — Aphoristic Twitter style begins to congeal. Twitter handle @naval becomes the medium.
- **2018, May 31, 3:23am** — **"How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" tweetstorm** (status 1002103360646823936). Single most influential artifact of his public canon. Crystallizes: specific knowledge, accountability, leverage (code + media), permissionless wealth, play long-term games with long-term people. Everything downstream — the book, the podcast, the cult — is rooted here.
- **2019** — Launches the **Naval Podcast** (initially on Periscope, then formalized) with Babak Nivi. First season is essentially an audio commentary track on the May 2018 tweetstorm.
- **2020, Sep 8** — **The Almanack of Naval Ravikant** (curated by Eric Jorgenson, foreword by Tim Ferriss, illustrated by Jack Butcher). Released for **free** in digital + paid in print. Becomes one of the most-gifted business/philosophy books of the decade.
- **2020–2022** — **Season 2 of the Naval Podcast** pivots from wealth to "The Practical Philosophy of Health and Happiness." Buddhist + Stoic + David Deutsch + Krishnamurti reading list goes mainstream.
- **2023–2024** — **David Deutsch / "The Beginning of Infinity"** becomes a recurring frame in his thinking. Brett Hall joins as co-host. The Naval Podcast becomes more epistemology-heavy: good explanations, error correction, knowledge as the substrate of progress.
- **2025, Feb** — **All-In Podcast E215** appearance: publicly states "I'm not scared of AI. I'm scared of what a very small number of people who control AI do to the rest of us." Crystallizes his AI-decentralization thesis.
- **2025, Mar 31** — **Modern Wisdom #922** ("44 Harsh Truths About Human Nature") with Chris Williamson. Long-form articulation of his harder, more disagreeable late-period worldview: "if you can't decide, the answer is no" + deleting his calendar + decisions-determine-most-of-life.
- **2025** — Public framing of **"AI won't replace programmers — it will make it easier for programmers to replace everyone else."** (Tweet, status 1875297712993964231.) Defines his late-2025 line on AI labor markets.
- **2025, Jul–Aug** — At **Internapalooza 2025** (Z Fellows, with Cory Levy), publicly introduces **Impossible Computer Company** — describes the team, the "in-person SF / quality offices" doctrine, and reasserts "people who love what they do and work nonstop don't burn out." This is his return to operator-mode public identity.
- **2026, Feb 19** — Naval Podcast "**A Motorcycle for the Mind**" — AI as cognition prosthesis (Steve Jobs reference). His most positive AI episode.
- **2026, Apr 28** — Naval Podcast "**A Return to Code**" — articulates **vibe coding** as a "video game with real-world rewards" and predicts personal app stores eroding Apple's distribution lock.
- **2026, May 4** — Naval Podcast "**'Nothing Ever Happens' Is Over**" — startup design, drone warfare, hardware renaissance. Frames the present moment as the end of stagnation.
- **2026, May 11** — Naval Podcast "**Sell the Truth**" (transcript at nav.al/sell) — "Only two skills you have to have in life: how to build and how to sell." A return to first-principles entrepreneurship.

**Recurring 2025–2026 themes worth folding into the skill's "My Path" section:**
- AI is not the threat; **concentration of AI control via regulatory capture** is the threat.
- AI raises the value of taste, judgment, ownership — not the value of cheap labor.
- He is **operating again**, in person, in SF, on a hardware-adjacent "impossible" problem — after almost a decade as a philosopher-investor.
- He has publicly admitted Airchat "didn't work" as a mass product, but says he's proud of the team and the build.
- He has deleted his calendar (Modern Wisdom #922, Mar 2025).
- His core reading frame has shifted: from Buddhism + Stoicism (2019–2022) to **David Deutsch / Karl Popper epistemology** (good explanations, error correction, "hard to vary").

## Confidence notes

- **Immigration year** disagrees across sources. Most popular bios say "age 9" → ~1983. The official navalmanack.com timeline says "age 11" → 1985. Treat as ~1983–1985. The "age 9" version aligns with Naval's own retellings; the 1985 version aligns with the curated almanack timeline.
- **Vast.com founding year**: Wikipedia says 2005; navalmanack.com official timeline says 2003. Likely 2003 conception / 2005 public — but unclear. Sources disagree by ~2 years.
- **Stepping down as CEO of AngelList**: Wikipedia confirms "previously served as CEO" and is now Chairman. Multiple secondary sources cluster around 2019; exact press-release date not surfaced cleanly. Treat as 2019.
- **Airchat founding year**: Wikipedia says 2023; mainstream press dates the public launch April 2024 (relaunch). Both can be true — internal start 2023, public launch April 2024.
- **Dartmouth graduation year**: Strong sources cluster on 1995. One secondary source (siliconvalleytime) says 1997 — almost certainly wrong, contradicts Wikipedia + the navalmanack timeline.
- **Impossible Inc. exact founding date** is not yet public — surfaced publicly via Naval's own X post (~mid-2025) and at Internapalooza 2025 (Aug 2025). The company is in stealth; product remains undisclosed. Press refers to it as "Impossible Computer Company" but Naval's own tweet calls it "Impossible, Inc." / "The Impossible Company."
- **One AI-strategy article (kenthospitals.com, dated Feb 27, 2026)** appears to be a paraphrased summary of a "walking dialogue with Nivi" without a clear primary citation — treat its specific claims as secondhand.
