# Hypothesis — soriza-ai-persona-agency

> Picked idea: *Soriza — an AI-persona talent agency* (artist-manager for AI idols). This sharpens the
> seed into a falsifiable problem hypothesis + the lean value/growth hypotheses. Everything below is
> **provisional, to-be-tested** — sharpening makes the claim *specific*, not *true*. `/market-map`
> grounds it; the interviews validate it; `/disconfirm` attacks it first.

**Testable sentence:** *HK-based and English-market consumer brands & e-commerce sellers (beauty/lifestyle
first)* need scandal-proof, always-on, on-brand creator content & KOL placements *on a continuous basis*
(monthly campaigns; ad-creative refreshed every 3-7 days), *costing $2-10k/mo + 10-21-day lead times +
30-40% reject rates + open-ended scandal exposure*, because they currently *rely on expensive, slow,
ghost-prone human influencers / UGC creators / agencies, in-house content, or generic stock.*

## The four dimensions — *provisional, to-be-tested*

- **Who** — The **payer**, not the audience: HK-based and English-language-market consumer brands &
  e-commerce/DTC sellers, **beauty/lifestyle vertical first** (Soriza heritage; strongest virtual-influencer
  vertical; shortest path from content → conversion). The actual human to interview: the marketing
  decision-maker — solo brand owner, brand/social manager, or performance marketer — who today writes the
  cheque for influencer placements and UGC. *(Audiences/followers are the **users**, not the buyers; they
  are the growth engine, captured below — not the WHO of the problem.)*
- **How often** — Continuous. Brands run influencer / paid-social campaigns monthly; DTC sellers hit
  creative fatigue and refresh ad creative every **3-7 days**, needing **10-30 fresh assets/month**.
- **How severe** — Human KOL/UGC is expensive, slow, and risky: **$2-10k/mo** (agency avg **~$1,750/creative**),
  **10-21 days per batch**, **30-40% reject rate**, creators who **ghost after deposits**; and a single
  human-creator scandal can trigger 6-7-figure fallout (Ferragni €1M+, Alo $75M, Shein $500M suits, 2025).
  **The payable severity lives with the payer** — the audience has attention to give, not a pain to solve.
- **Status quo** — They hire human influencers / UGC creators / agencies, produce in-house, or use generic
  stock — absorbing the cost, lead-time, reject-rate, and scandal exposure as the cost of doing business.

## Value & growth hypotheses (assumptions the downstream stages test)

- **Value hypothesis** *(DEFERRED — the #1 risk)* — Payers will move budget to an **owned disclosed-AI
  persona's** placements / AI-generated UGC **if** it matches human conversion & engagement at materially
  lower cost, faster turnaround, and zero scandal risk — **despite brand willingness to use AI creators
  having fallen 86%→60% in a year** (96% of non-adopters cite consumer trust). The founder's plan proves
  growth *before* testing this → **you can win the following and still lose the revenue.** This is the
  single most important thing the interviews + `/disconfirm` must trip or clear.
- **Growth hypothesis** *(ELEVATED — the founder's v1 PoC gate)* — A from-scratch, disclosed-AI persona
  can win a **real, organic, engaged following** on short-form social (TikTok-first for reach, IG for
  monetization; Cantonese + English; beauty/lifestyle) — and that **owned audience** becomes the inbound
  channel that brings payers. Engine = **sticky + viral** organic content (not paid). Founder's gate:
  *prove the system can build + launch ONE persona to a real following before investing in monetization.*
  **This is the founder's stated #1 risk** (distribution-from-zero) and is best tested by an actual launched
  persona (a Build-Measure-Learn PoC), not by interviews.

## Founder refinements

The founder steered three load-bearing forks (via `/sharpen-hypothesis` Q&A):

1. **Sequencing → persona-first, PoC-gated.** Founder's words: *"build the persona first → if we cannot
   build a runnable persona, then we cannot move on to UGC ad-creative with some followers… target persona
   first, then PoC success — one persona's success launched and then go."* Interpretation: the persona-build
   is treated as the gating **feasibility + growth** risk; monetization (UGC ad-creative / brand deals)
   is unlocked *after* one persona is proven. → Reflected by elevating the **growth hypothesis** to the v1
   gate and **deferring** the value hypothesis. *Honest caveat fed back to founder:* this proves
   desirability-of-the-persona before desirability-of-the-payer — keep learning ahead of building, and
   treat "no payer at the end" as a live failure mode, not a formality.
2. **Monetization lane → firewall BOTH identities.** Run an adult identity (fully separate, never-linked,
   as an early cash floor) *and* the brand-safe Soriza persona, kept firewalled. This is the founder's way
   to keep Lane-B cash without foreclosing Lane-A's brand-deal/B2B future. → Recorded as the provisional
   plan; the **firewall integrity** and **adult-platform viability** become central flagged assumptions
   (see below). Note tension with fork #1: persona-first PoC = ONE persona, but firewall-both = ≥2
   identities — *which identity is the PoC subject* is an open sub-decision (recommendation flagged below).
3. **Geo/market → Hong Kong + English markets first** (not Mainland-China/Xiaohongshu-only). → v1 trilingual
   edge narrows to **bilingual Cantonese-English**; Mandarin/Xiaohongshu deferred to expansion. *Honest
   caveat:* choosing "English market first" partly trades away the founder's **strongest structural edge**
   (the Greater-China / Xiaohongshu bilingual-KOL gap, where no dominant virtual KOL exists) for a larger
   but **head-on-competitive** arena (TheClueless et al.). Flagged for `/disconfirm`.

*Vertical (beauty/lifestyle) not pinned by the founder — defaulted from Soriza heritage + founder's
e-commerce/cosmetics interest + it being the strongest VI vertical. Provisional; founder may override.*

## Load-bearing assumptions to carry into /disconfirm

Ranked by how much the idea dies if they're false:

1. **Value-after-growth gap (highest).** That a real following actually converts to paying brands/sellers
   — against a falling 86%→60% adoption trend. The whole persona-first sequence stakes runway on this
   being true *later*.
2. **Distribution-from-zero.** That a from-scratch disclosed-AI persona can win a *real* (not bought)
   following at all, and fast enough for a 3-6-month runway. (Brand-safe persona-build is a months-long
   unpaid build.)
3. **Firewall integrity + adult-platform viability.** That an adult identity can be kept provably
   un-linkable to Soriza *and* is even operable: OnlyFans **bans fully-fictional AI personas**; Fanvue
   **requires a real human legally attached**; HK obscenity law + payment-processor/age-verification risk.
   A single leak (shared payment rail, device, voice, face-LoRA, posting fingerprint) collapses the
   firewall and poisons Soriza permanently. *Recommendation to flag: make the brand-safe Soriza persona
   the PoC subject; defer the adult identity until firewall + platform viability are independently proven.*
4. **English-market edge erosion.** That Soriza can win in the English market where its trilingual edge
   doesn't apply and funded incumbents already operate.
5. **Compliance-as-table-stakes.** Disclosed-AI labeling everywhere; China AIGC watermark (if any GC
   audience), EU AI Act Art.50 (Aug 2026), FTC disclosure; resemblance-check gate so a generated face
   doesn't match a real person (right-of-publicity / NO FAKES Act).
