# Hypothesis — soriza-operator-os

> **Founder-picked beachhead (this sharpening pass):** solo/indie operators · English-first global ·
> severity anchored on the **scaling ceiling**. The primary hypothesis is written around **Open
> Assumption #1 — operator willingness-to-pay for the brand-safe OS** (the make-or-break). Everything
> below is a **concrete claim flagged provisional, to-be-tested** — specific ≠ true. `/disconfirm` will
> red-team it; `/market-map` will ground it; real-operator interviews settle it.

**Testable sentence:** *Solo/indie disclosed-AI persona operators who already monetize 1–2 brand-safe
personas hit a wall every content cycle where their hand-stitched 5-tool workflow can't scale past 1–2
personas, costing them most of the portfolio income they could otherwise earn (effort scales ~linearly
with persona count, so each new persona ~duplicates the weekly hours), because they currently glue 5+
disconnected point tools together by hand — tracking character state in spreadsheets/Notion and policing
consistency by eyeballing and re-rolling — with no integrated tool that holds persona state or automates
cross-platform posting + disclosure.*

## The four dimensions — *provisional, to-be-tested*

- **Who** — A **solo or 2–3-person indie disclosed-AI persona operator**, English-first / global, who is
  **already monetizing 1–2 brand-safe personas** (lifestyle / fashion / fitness / tech — explicitly **not**
  adult / GFE / parasocial-romance) across **2+ platforms** (e.g. IG / TikTok / X) plus a monetization
  surface (brand deals, memberships, affiliate). Reachable via operator communities on **X, Discord, and
  Reddit**. *Deliberately excluded for v1:* adult/GFE operators (out of scope by founder decision) and
  brands/agencies (a different buyer with a weaker, slower-closing demand signal — deferred, not the wedge).

- **How often** — The orchestration pain recurs **every content cycle**: a near-daily posting cadence means
  the 5-tool handoff (image → voice → video → schedule → caption) and consistency-fixing happen **multiple
  times per week, per persona**. The *scaling-ceiling* pain bites **the moment they try to add a persona** —
  when weekly effort would have to roughly double to run two.

- **How severe** *(primary bet: the scaling ceiling)* — The manual workflow **caps operators at 1–2
  personas** because effort scales ~linearly with count, so they stall and **most potential portfolio income
  is never realized** (a documented operator clears ~$150K/yr on **one** hand-run persona — the 3rd–Nth
  persona's income is left on the table). *Secondary severity to also probe:* **consistency is the named #1
  build-breaker** — off-model assets force re-rolls that burn time and erode the persona's identity.
  *Provisional magnitude to test in interviews:* ~10–20+ hrs/week per persona on orchestration + re-gen; a
  hard ceiling of 1–2 personas against a desired larger portfolio.

- **Status quo** — They run a **hand-stitched stack of 5+ disconnected point tools** (e.g. Flux/Higgsfield
  or Midjourney for stills, ElevenLabs or open TTS for voice, Hunyuan/Kling/Sora for video, a scheduler like
  Metricool/Hypefury, ChatGPT/Claude for captions), **glued together manually** and tracked in
  spreadsheets/Notion to hold character state + the posting calendar. **Consistency is policed by eyeballing
  and re-rolling.** No integrated tool holds persona state or automates cross-platform posting + compliant
  disclosure — so they either grind solo at 1–2 personas or simply don't scale.

## Value & growth hypotheses (assumptions the downstream stages test)

- **Value hypothesis** *(= Open Assumption #1, make-or-break)* — Solo/indie brand-safe operators will find an
  **integrated, compliance-native operator OS** (holds character state · locks consistency across
  image/voice/video · automates multi-account cross-platform scheduling · bakes in auto-disclosure/audit)
  **valuable enough to pay a real monthly subscription (~$50–200/mo or a per-persona seat, provisional) AND
  switch** from their hand-stitched stack — because it **breaks the 1–2-persona ceiling**, letting them add
  personas at **sub-linear effort** (more personas = more income for ~the same hours).
  **Falsified if** operators won't name a price / won't switch off their current stack / say the ceiling
  isn't their real constraint. **Top risks to disconfirm:** (a) the brand-safe operator slice is *thinner*
  than the full, adult-inclusive market; (b) operators anchored on a ~$77/mo total stack resist a meaningful
  SaaS price; (c) they don't believe an OS actually removes the bottleneck (fear it's just one more tool in
  the juggle).

- **Growth hypothesis** — New operators discover Soriza primarily through **(1) the founder's own brand-safe
  persona portfolio as live, build-in-public proof** ("here's me running N personas with this") + a public
  case study that doubles as the demo, and **(2) presence in the operator communities where the pain already
  lives** (X threads, Discord servers, Reddit subs) via helpful content + word-of-mouth in a tight, networked
  niche. **Engine** = likely **sticky** (retention on a workflow tool the operation runs on) with a
  **word-of-mouth/viral assist** inside a small operator community. **To test:** that build-in-public proof
  actually converts operator-viewers into OS trials, and that the community is reachable + receptive (not
  already saturated with tool-spam).

## Founder refinements

*Injected this pass via the sharpening Q&A (the founder's calls, not the draft's defaults):*

- **Beachhead = solo/indie operators**, not studios or brands. Rationale: most reachable (X/Discord/Reddit),
  clearest and most acute pain. Accepted trade-off — the thinnest wallets, and the brand-safe slice is
  thinner once adult operators are excluded. → makes **WHO** an individual operator, not an org.
- **Geography = English-first / global**, not HK/GBA-trilingual-first. Rationale: the biggest reachable
  operator pool and where the documented pain + communities already live. The founder's trilingual /
  HK-GBA edge is retained as a **later differentiation wedge**, not the v1 bet. → sets the **WHO** segment
  and the **growth** channels to English-language communities first.
- **Severity anchor = the scaling ceiling** (opportunity cost), with **consistency breakage** kept as the
  secondary pain. Rationale: matches the OS's core promise (run many at sub-linear effort) and is the
  strongest "I'd pay real money" trigger. → makes **HOW SEVERE** about unrealized portfolio income, and
  shapes the interviews to probe the 1–2-persona wall first.
