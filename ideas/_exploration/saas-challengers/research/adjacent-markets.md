# Adjacent & Analogous Markets — Findings

## Key findings

- **Legal AI (Harvey, Legora, EvenUp) proved the wedge-then-platform pattern at speed.** Harvey hit $100M ARR / $8B valuation and Legora hit $100M ARR / $5.6B valuation by attacking a single repetitive workflow (legal research / document review) inside law firms paying Westlaw/LexisNexis. EvenUp ($2B+) went even narrower — personal-injury demand letters only. The wedge: automate the single most labor-intensive document task, prove 10x speed, then expand into adjacent workflows. Legal vertical AI captured $650M in enterprise spend in 2025, up from near-zero in 2022. Replicable in any document-heavy professional-services vertical.

- **Campfire and Rillet are actively pulling mid-market companies off NetSuite by solving one painful accounting sub-workflow first.** Campfire ($35M Series A, Accel) and Rillet ($70M Series B, a16z + ICONIQ) both started with revenue recognition / month-end close automation, not full ERP replacement. One Campfire customer went from a 15-day to a 3-day close; Advisor360 migrated from NetSuite specifically for this. Moat being built: own the ledger data layer, then displace the broader system of record. Pattern: don't fight the whole ERP — own the one number a CFO obsesses over.

- **Healthcare RCM (revenue cycle management) is an active displacement battleground — 80% of health systems piloting AI in 2025.** Black Book identified 12 fast-rising AI RCM startups from a field of 270 VC-backed firms. Akasa, ENTER, Smarter Technologies, Procode AI attacking claim processing / medical coding — tasks previously done by offshore BPO armies. Wedge is services-led: act as an outsourced labor replacement, harden into software. 92% of investors increasing RCM bets. Moat: proprietary claims data + payer-specific correction patterns that compound with volume.

- **Zamp is attacking Avalara / TaxJar in sales-tax compliance with a "managed OS" model — AI agents + human guarantee.** Raised $30M (April 2026, Thomson Reuters Ventures + Acrew). 120+ accounting firms, 12x output per compliance resource, 99.97% filing accuracy, $300M+ remitted. Wedge: replace the offshore compliance staff first, not the software license — then become the system of record across 12,000+ jurisdictions. Attackable because Avalara is post-acquisition bloated and TaxJar was absorbed into Stripe, leaving a specialist gap.

- **CMMS/maintenance management (MaintainX, UpKeep) are displacing IBM Maximo in mid-market with mobile-first + AI.** MaintainX raised $150M Series D (July 2025) at $2.5B; #1 CMMS on G2 Winter 2026, 11K+ companies, 11M+ managed assets. Incumbent (IBM Maximo) is priced for F500 and needs heavy integrators. Wedge: mobile-first work-order creation frontline techs actually use, then expand into predictive failure / asset analytics. Early AI CMMS adopters see 50% faster issue resolution, 35% more uptime. Under-attacked sub-niche: EHS compliance on top of CMMS — a $2.27B market → $4B by 2030 with no dominant AI-native player.

- **Automotive dealer management systems (DMS) — 80% controlled by CDK Global, Reynolds & Reynolds, Cox/Dealertrack — are being cracked by Tekion ($4B+).** Tekion (ex-Tesla CIO) is the first cloud-native end-to-end automotive retail platform with AI in core workflows, backed by GM, BMW, Hyundai. $4.85B DMS market 2025 → $7.86B by 2030. 35% of US dealers adopted some AI; ~50% expected by Q4 2025. Wedge: replace the DMS itself (decades-old on-prem), not an add-on. Replicable in any dealer/distributor vertical (heavy equipment, marine, powersports) where a 3-player oligopoly controls legacy on-prem software.

- **GovTech procurement is under-attacked with structural advantages — Hazel and NationGraph wedging in.** Hazel automates the full procurement lifecycle (requirements, market research, solicitation drafting, proposal evaluation) for US federal/state/local agencies. NationGraph raised $18M for AI procurement intelligence. US GovTech spend projected $357B in 2026; early-stage GovTech 30% more likely to reach Series A than five years ago. Attackable because procurement software (Bonfire, Periscope) is ancient and compliance-heavy. Wedge: stay under procurement thresholds, embed in one department's daily workflow.

- **The repeatable wedge playbook (per Bessemer's Jan 2026 vertical-AI book) has three steps:** (1) Start with a standardized, repetitive sub-workflow with a measurable output — not a judgment-heavy core process. (2) Automate it with an "undeniable miracle" demo that proves 10x speed/cost, then use that trust to expand into core workflows. (3) Become the system of record for the data that sub-workflow generates — the data layer is the durable moat, not the model. Durability requires: workflow-integration depth (switching cost), proprietary domain data that compounds, ROI tied to labor-budget displacement (not IT spend). Thin AI features bolted onto old data are not durable.

- **Greater China / HK angle: Kingdee dominates mainland mid-market ERP but has not shipped an AI-native rebuild; the GBA manufacturing cluster generates AI-embedded production data at scale.** Kingdee is the go-to for companies bridging HK and mainland needing Chinese regulatory compliance. Jiushi Intelligence, Megagenta, SmartVoice are embedded next to GBA production lines. Signal real but thin for a specific wedge — the gap is an AI-native replacement for Kingdee's mid-market modules (HR, procurement, manufacturing scheduling) in Cantonese/Mandarin that incumbents haven't rebuilt natively for the LLM era.

- **AI-native vertical plays earn durable moats when they own proprietary workflow data; they are features when they don't.** Across Harvey (legal corpus), Zamp (filing history), MaintainX (asset failure data), Akasa (claims adjudication) the pattern is identical: the AI improves because it sees more domain-specific feedback loops than any general model. Best targets: categories where incumbents hold the data but not the AI (legacy on-prem ERP, old CMMS, paper-based compliance) — the data can be extracted on migration and the new system trains on it. Categories where data is already commoditized (generic doc summarization, basic chatbots) have no durable wedge.
