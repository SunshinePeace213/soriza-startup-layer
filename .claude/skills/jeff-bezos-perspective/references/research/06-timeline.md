## Timeline

| Year | Event | Why it matters for how he thinks |
|------|-------|----------------------------------|
| 1964 (Jan 12) | Born Jeffrey Preston Jorgensen in Albuquerque, NM, to teenage mother Jackie and Ted Jorgensen. | Origin story he later disowns: the absent biological father becomes a non-factor; identity is *chosen*, not inherited. Frames his later "we are what we choose" thesis. |
| 1968 | Adopted by Cuban immigrant Miguel "Mike" Bezos (stepfather) after Jackie remarried. Takes Bezos surname. | Mike Bezos arrived in the U.S. via Operation Pedro Pan at 16, alone, with nothing. Bezos cites this as proof that one resourceful person can rebuild anything from zero — the immigrant-grit imprint. |
| Late 1960s | Montessori preschool: teachers report having to "physically lift him out of one activity" to start another. | Mother Jackie tells this story repeatedly. Bezos points to it as the earliest evidence of his single-task absorption and stubborn focus — a trait he never tries to soften. |
| 1968–1977 | Summers on grandparents' Cotulla, TX ranch with Lawrence Preston "Pop" Gise (former senior director at the Atomic Energy Commission). | Pop fixed every broken thing — cattle, fences, a Caterpillar D6 bulldozer — himself. Bezos's "resourcefulness over resources" ethic, and his belief that *kindness is harder than smartness*, both come from Pop. |
| 1982 | Valedictorian, Miami Palmetto Senior High. Speech mentions colonizing space and saving Earth as a "nature preserve." | The space obsession is in place at 18, not invented later. Blue Origin (2000) is the continuation, not a pivot. |
| 1986 | Princeton, BSE in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (originally physics). | Switched out of physics after meeting students who "could do in 30 seconds what would take me three hours." First clean public example of *circle-of-competence recalibration*: change the domain, don't fight the verdict. |
| 1990–1994 | D.E. Shaw & Co. — became youngest SVP at 30. Met MacKenzie Tuttle (his future wife). | The quant-finance training. He learns to think in expected value, probability, and asymmetric outcomes — the substrate beneath every later "type 1 vs type 2 decision" framework. |
| 1994 | Reads stat that web is growing 2,300% annually. Quits D.E. Shaw using the **Regret Minimization Framework** (project to age 80, choose what you'd least regret). Drives to Seattle, writes the Amazon business plan in the car. | The decision algorithm that defines him. He runs every major life decision through this engine for the rest of his life. |
| 1995 (Jul 16) | Amazon.com launches as an online bookstore from a Bellevue, WA garage. | "Get big fast" — chooses books because the catalog is huge and ships flat. The choice of category is itself a *first-principles* output: maximize selection where atoms allow it. |
| 1997 (May 15) | Amazon IPO at $18/share. **First shareholder letter** establishes the Day 1 doctrine: long-term thinking, customer obsession, willingness to be misunderstood. | The founding document of his entire public philosophy. Every annual letter thereafter ends by reattaching this 1997 letter — an explicit anti-drift mechanism. |
| 1999 | *Time* Person of the Year. | First peak of cultural hype — and the immediate setup for the next milestone. |
| 2000–2002 | Dotcom crash. Amazon stock collapses from ~$107 to ~$6 (-94%). *Barron's* publishes "Amazon.bomb." Bezos doesn't change strategy. | The crucible moment. Bezos publicly demonstrates that *stock price is not the company*. Cements his "we're not a Q4 company, we're a 20-year company" voice. The stoic disposition toward markets is forged here. |
| 2000 (Sep) | **Founds Blue Origin** quietly. Motto: *Gradatim Ferociter* ("step by step, ferociously"). | The childhood thesis becomes a corporation. He runs it on a different time-clock than Amazon — the patient capital, "millions of people living and working in space" vision. |
| 2002–2004 | **Bans PowerPoint internally**; institutes the six-page narrative memo, read silently at the start of every meeting. | Bezos's most distinctive cognitive intervention: forces full-sentence thinking, kills bullet-point hand-waving, and democratizes information because anyone can read prose. The shape of Amazon's thinking is now permanent. |
| 2005 (Feb) | **Amazon Prime launches** ($79/year, free 2-day shipping). Internal finance team predicts catastrophe. | The "willingness to be misunderstood" applied operationally. Bezos overrules the spreadsheet because he can see the customer-loyalty flywheel the spreadsheet can't price. |
| 2006 | **AWS launches** — S3 in March, EC2 in August. | The platform thesis: sell the picks and shovels of computing. Born from the internal observation that Amazon spent months on infrastructure plumbing for every new project. Eventually becomes the profit engine that pays for everything else. |
| 2007 (Nov) | **Kindle launches** after years of secret development. Bezos quote: "You have to be willing to be misunderstood for long periods of time." | Bezos cannibalizes his own bookstore before anyone else does. The internal lesson: *if you don't disrupt yourself, someone will*. |
| 2010 (May 30) | **"We Are What We Choose"** Princeton commencement address. Publicly narrates the regret-minimization origin story and the gifts-vs-choices distinction. | His personal philosophy crystallizes into a portable speech. Becomes the single most quoted Bezos text outside the shareholder letters. |
| 2013 (Aug 5) | **Buys *The Washington Post* for $250M cash** — personally, not through Amazon. | Marks the move from "operator" to "civic actor with infrastructure." Frames the paper as a *complement* to democracy, not a profit center. Sets up the 2024 endorsement controversy years later. |
| 2013 (Oct) | Brad Stone publishes *The Everything Store*. MacKenzie posts a 1-star Amazon review of it. | First serious external attempt to render Bezos's psychology. He hates the book but it becomes the public template; he will spend the next decade publicly modifying that image. |
| 2017 (Jun) | Amazon **acquires Whole Foods for $13.7B**. | The atoms-meet-bits moment. Bezos translates Day 1 thinking into physical retail — selection, price, convenience — and forces the legacy grocery industry to revalue itself overnight. |
| 2018 | Becomes the world's richest person (~$160B). | Wealth and identity decouple publicly. The next phase — divorce, philanthropy, Blue Origin focus, Lex podcast — is a long, observable shift away from "Amazon CEO" as primary identity. |
| 2018 (Sep) | Announces the **Bezos Day One Fund — $2B** for homelessness aid and a network of nonprofit Montessori-inspired preschools. | The Montessori loop closes. His own first formative environment becomes a philanthropic template. Choosing preschools (not universities) is itself a *long-term-thinking* tell. |
| 2019 (Jan) | Announces divorce from MacKenzie after 25 years. *National Enquirer* publishes texts; Bezos publishes "No thank you, Mr. Pecker" on Medium, accusing AMI of extortion. Saudi phone-hack story breaks shortly after. | The first time the world sees him fight back in public, in his own voice, in real time. Marks a shift toward direct authorship — fewer corporate filters. |
| 2020 (Feb) | Announces **Bezos Earth Fund — $10B commitment** to climate and nature by 2030. | His largest single philanthropic commitment. Re-orients his post-Amazon identity around long-horizon civilizational risks (climate + space) rather than current-quarter operations. |
| 2021 (Apr) | Final shareholder letter as CEO. Ends with the line: "If you want to be successful in business (in life, actually), you have to create more than you consume." Reflects on universe, death, distinctiveness. | The most personal of all the letters. Bezos uses his last operator-microphone to deliver a closing thesis: *the universe wants you to be typical, resist it*. |
| 2021 (Jul 5) | **Steps down as Amazon CEO**, becomes Executive Chairman. Andy Jassy takes over. | The handoff he had been engineering since at least 2016. Reframes himself as inventor, not operator. |
| 2021 (Jul 20) | **First crewed Blue Origin flight (NS-16)**, exactly 15 days after stepping down — Bezos onboard with brother Mark, Wally Funk, Oliver Daemen, on the 52nd anniversary of Apollo 11. | The proximity is the point. The CEO chair is vacated *to climb into the rocket*. Bodily evidence that Blue Origin is now the primary canvas. |
| 2022 (Nov) | Pledges to give "the majority of his wealth" away in his lifetime — in a CNN interview with Chloe Melas, alongside Sánchez. | First explicit Giving Pledge-style commitment. The post-CEO voice is now about deployment, not accumulation. |
| 2023 (Nov 1) | Announces **move from Seattle to Miami** via Instagram. Cites parents nearby and proximity to Cape Canaveral; tax avoidance (Washington's new 7% capital-gains tax) is the unspoken third reason. | Geographic untethering from Amazon HQ. Locates himself near the rocket pad, not the warehouse. Symbolic completion of the identity shift. |
| 2023 (Dec 14) | **Lex Fridman Podcast #405** — first long-form interview of his life. ~2 hours, candid on Pop, six-page memos, type 1/type 2 decisions, trillion-people-in-space vision. | Establishes the new public Bezos voice: looser, more philosophical, willing to wander on camera. The primary text for distilling his post-CEO thinking. |
| 2024 (early) | Move to Miami completed; takes up residence on Indian Creek Island ("Billionaire Bunker"). | — |
| 2024 (Oct 25) | **Washington Post non-endorsement**: Bezos overrules the editorial board's drafted Harris endorsement. ~250,000 subscribers cancel within days; columnists and editorial board members resign. Bezos publishes his own op-ed Oct 28 defending the call as a stand against "perception of bias." | First time his media ownership produces direct civic consequences. He chooses *appearance of independence* over institutional momentum — and absorbs the resignations as the cost of the call. Foreshadows his 2025–26 alignment with the Trump administration. |
| 2025 (Jan 16) | **Blue Origin's New Glenn reaches orbit on first flight (NG-1)** from Cape Canaveral. Booster lost on descent. | Two-thirds win, one-third lesson — the gradatim ferociter pattern in real time. Bezos public statement frames the booster loss as data, not failure. |
| 2025 (Jan 20) | **Attends Trump's inauguration** seated behind the President with Sánchez. Amazon donated $1M to the inauguration fund. | The Bezos-Trump rapprochement is now visual and public. A direct break from his 2016–2020 posture. Strategic alignment with state power for the AWS/Kuiper/Blue Origin regulatory surface area. |
| 2025 (Apr 14) | **Blue Origin NS-31** — all-female suborbital flight organized by Sánchez (Katy Perry, Gayle King, Aisha Bowe, Kerianne Flynn, Amanda Nguyen). Heavy public backlash. | First publicly polarizing Blue Origin event. Shows how the family-and-brand fusion (Sánchez as crew organizer + fiancée) can backfire when symbolism outruns substance. |
| 2025 (Apr 28) | **Project Kuiper's first 27 operational satellites launched** on Atlas V. Eventually rebranded as **Amazon Leo**. | The Starlink challenge is operational, not paper. By April 2026 the constellation reaches 300+ satellites with FCC's July 2026 deadline (1,618 satellites) as the binding constraint. |
| 2025 (Jun 26–28) | **Wedding to Lauren Sánchez in Venice.** Three days, ~200 guests (Gates, Altman, DiCaprio, Oprah, Brady, Ivanka Trump). Local protests; "If you can rent Venice for your wedding you can pay more tax" banner. | Cultural inflection point. The Bezos image fully completes its transition from frugal-Amazon-founder to globalized-billionaire-spectacle. He absorbs the criticism without flinching — the *willingness to be misunderstood* doctrine applied to personal life. |
| 2025 (Nov 13) | **New Glenn NG-2 launches NASA's ESCAPADE mission to Mars and successfully lands its first-stage booster** on the *Jacklyn* in the Atlantic — Blue Origin becomes the second company to land an orbital-class booster. | The Blue Origin moonshot pays off concretely. After 25 years of "tortoise" mockery, Bezos delivers a SpaceX-class capability — and silences the comparison cleanly. |
| 2025 (Nov 17) | **Project Prometheus revealed**: Bezos's new AI startup, co-CEO with Vik Bajaj (former Google X), $6.2B initial funding. Focused on AI for the physical world — manufacturing, aerospace, drug design. Building an "artificial general engineer." | His first operational role in any company since stepping down from Amazon. He explicitly returns to founder-mode for AI applied to atoms — the place his comparative advantage sits. |
| 2026 (Apr ~) | Bloomberg reports Prometheus closes a **$10B round at $38B valuation** (JPMorgan, BlackRock among investors). Headcount >120, with researchers poached from OpenAI/Meta/DeepMind. Offices: SF, London, Zurich. | Validates that the post-CEO Bezos brand can still pull tier-1 capital and talent at speed. |
| 2026 (Apr 9) | Andy Jassy publishes his 2026 letter defending Amazon's **$200B 2026 capex** ("not on a hunch"). Discloses **$15B AI revenue run-rate** at AWS, **$20B/year** Trainium/Inferentia chip business. Appends Bezos's 1997 letter as always. | The 1997 Day 1 letter is now a 29-year unbroken doctrinal anchor. Bezos's *operating system* outlives his operating role. |
| 2026 (May 20) | **CNBC/Andrew Ross Sorkin interview** on Squawk Box. Bezos: defends billionaires, dismisses AI bubble fears ("the bubble is driving investment"), calls for **zero federal income tax on bottom 50%**, praises Trump on regulation, downplays the 2-3 year timeline for orbital data centers as "a little ambitious." | The most direct public political-economic positioning of Bezos's life. The post-CEO voice is now fully a public intellectual / political voice — pro-growth, pro-tax-cut-for-poor, pro-deregulation, AI-maximalist. |
| 2026 (May 24) | Net worth ~$266B (Bloomberg), #3 globally behind Musk and Page. Owns ~8.2% of Amazon. Project Prometheus, Blue Origin, *Washington Post*, Bezos Earth Fund, Day One Fund all running. | Operationally: a multi-front compounder. Cognitively: a single thesis (*Day 1, long-term, customer-obsession, willingness to be misunderstood*) applied across atoms (Blue Origin, Prometheus, Kuiper), capital (Amazon stake), and civic surface (WaPo). |

### 2025-11: Blue Origin Catches SpaceX (Almost)
On Nov 13, 2025, New Glenn's NG-2 mission launched NASA's ESCAPADE probes toward Mars *and* recovered the first-stage booster ("Never Tell Me the Odds") on the landing platform *Jacklyn*, 375 miles offshore. This makes Blue Origin the **second company in history** to land an orbital-class booster (after SpaceX). 25 years after founding, Blue Origin produces its category-defining proof. Bezos publicly: composed, gracious, "step by step." [Sources: [NASASpaceflight](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2025/11/ng-2-escapade-launch/), [Blue Origin](https://www.blueorigin.com/news/new-glenn-ng-1-mission)]

### 2025-11: Project Prometheus Goes Public
On Nov 17, 2025, TechCrunch broke the story: Bezos is **co-CEO** of Project Prometheus, a stealth AI company with $6.2B in initial funding focused on AI for engineering, manufacturing, drug design, aerospace. Co-led with Vik Bajaj (formerly Google X, Foresite Labs). First operational role for Bezos since 2021. By April 2026, the company has >120 employees (recruits from OpenAI/Meta/DeepMind), offices in SF/London/Zurich, and reportedly a $38B valuation on $10B raised. Bezos's framing in May 2026: it deserves "its own special focus" as "its own big idea." ## Thought-Evolution Inflection Points

**1. The 2000–2002 Crash — Stoicism as Trained Reflex.** Before the crash, Bezos was a wunderkind quant who'd had a single, brilliant call. After it, he was something tougher. Watching the stock fall 94% while continuing to spend on infrastructure, fulfillment, and AWS-precursor work taught him something more durable than any framework: *the price the market quotes for your company on any given day is not information about your company*. The 1997 shareholder letter had said this in advance, but the crash welded the belief to his nervous system. Every subsequent "we are misunderstood and it's fine" moment — Prime in 2005, Kindle in 2007, AWS losses for a decade, Whole Foods in 2017 — runs on the same hardened circuitry.

**2. 2013 — Washington Post and the Civic Self.** Buying the *Post* with personal cash (not Amazon stock or corporate funds) was the first time Bezos cleanly separated three categories of capital: Amazon equity, personal wealth, and civic infrastructure. Before 2013, he was an operator with stock. After 2013, he was a *steward of multiple kinds of asset*, each with its own time-horizon and accountability. This separation made the 2020 Earth Fund, the 2018 Day One Fund, and even the awkward 2024 non-endorsement decision logically possible. It also planted the seed of his eventual operational return: once the categories are separate, you can take a new operating role (Prometheus 2025) without contaminating the others.

**3. 2021-July — The 15-Day Sequence.** Stepping down as Amazon CEO on July 5 and climbing into a Blue Origin rocket on July 20 wasn't just symbolism — it was a public commitment device. Bezos had been engineering this transition since at least 2016, but executing the handoff and the launch within fifteen days was a way of making the identity shift socially irreversible. From that moment forward, journalists asking "what's Bezos working on" got Blue Origin as the default answer, not Amazon. The Day 1 doctrine survived; the daily emails to S-team about defect rates did not. He moved from operator to architect of operators.

**4. 2023–2024 — Untethering.** Three moves in fifteen months: the Lex Fridman podcast (Dec 2023) where he speaks freely on camera for the first time; the move to Miami (Nov 2023 / early 2024) putting him next to Cape Canaveral and away from Amazon HQ; and the WaPo non-endorsement (Oct 2024) where he overrides his own editorial board. The common thread: *Bezos becoming directly editable to himself*. Fewer intermediaries — no PR layer in the Fridman interview, no editorial buffer at the *Post*, no Seattle reminder that he's the Amazon guy. The voice gets blunter, the political stance gets visible, the willingness-to-be-misunderstood doctrine starts cutting against allies rather than just analysts.

**5. 2025–2026 — Return to Founder Mode, This Time With State Alignment.** Three events define this phase: (a) sitting behind Trump at the Jan 2025 inauguration, ending any ambiguity about his political-economic alignment; (b) New Glenn NG-2's successful booster recovery in Nov 2025, the long-awaited proof that gradatim ferociter actually delivers; (c) Project Prometheus, where Bezos accepts a co-CEO role for the first time in 25+ years. Bezos at 62 is now operating on *three* clocks simultaneously: Amazon as legacy compounder (handed off but doctrinally anchored to him), Blue Origin / Kuiper as multi-decade civilizational bets, and Prometheus as a five-year AI sprint. The thinker who once said "I'm not a Q4 company, I'm a 20-year company" is now publicly mapping his own portfolio of time-horizons. The May 2026 Sorkin interview — defending billionaires, calling for zero tax on bottom 50%, dismissing AI-bubble fears — is the first time he sounds like a candidate for *cultural authority*, not just commercial dominance. The arc bends from operator → architect → public intellectual. The doctrine (*Day 1, long-term, customer obsession, willingness to be misunderstood*) is unchanged. The *deployment surface* is now civilization-scale.
