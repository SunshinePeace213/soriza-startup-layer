# Existing solutions & competitors — Findings

Scope: AI-native (and traditional outsourced) back-office *services* a startup could replace, by category. Saturation vs whitespace called out, with HK/SG specifics and licensing flags. Lens = YC "sell the service, not software" thesis, technical solo-ish builder, HK/APAC, B2B, low regulatory appetite.

## Key findings

- **Bookkeeping/accounting is the most SATURATED, US-led space — avoid head-on.** Crowded with AI-native, well-funded players: Pilot (250+ US experts, full-service), Puzzle (founded 2022, Plus tier ~$200/mo software-only), Digits ($350/mo, in-house CPA + AI agents), Zeni ($549 / $799 / custom tiers, daily bookkeeping for tech startups), plus Fondo, Finally, Truewind. Cautionary tale: **Bench collapsed** when its AI books produced inaccuracies and delays — proof the "do the work" promise is unforgiving in accounting. Implication: don't build "another AI bookkeeper for US startups." If you touch this, it must be a localized APAC/HK wedge, not a generic clone.

- **APAC bookkeeping is comparatively OPEN but the wall is localization + standards, not tech.** Regional incumbents are mostly thin tooling: 247 AICFO (HK, "AI accounting for SMEs," HKFRS + e-filing), AI Account (Singapore-based, cloud, consolidation in 23 countries), 41 Labs (Singapore, "AI for accounting firms, handle 50% more clients"). Adoption is real — 70% of Singapore SMEs already use some AI accounting tool; SEA firms lead globally (53% use AI for tax/accounting/audit research vs 36% ANZ). The moat to build is HKFRS/SFRS compliance, local bank-feed integration, IRD/IRAS e-filing — a *climbable* domain wall for a technical builder who partners with one accountant, but you're competing partly on trust, and the Bench lesson means errors kill you.

- **HK/SG traditional incumbents price high and are ripe to undercut — but the company-secretary slice is LICENSE-GATED (deprioritize).** Bookkeeping in HK runs HK$740–5,000/mo for SMEs (HK$3,000–10,000 for mid-size); ~65% of HK firms now sell fixed monthly packages. Company-sec / incorporation incumbents: Sleek (incorporation ~HK$5,349 Starter; co-sec from ~HK$1,300/yr), Osome (co-sec HK$1,400–3,600/yr; incorporation from HK$4,650), easyCorp (HK$980/yr co-sec), and the heavyweight **Tricor — now merged into Vistra** (full-stack entity mgmt, co-sec, accounting, payroll, tax). CAUTION: in HK, acting as company secretary / forming companies / providing registered office requires a **TCSP licence** (3-yr validity, mandatory Compliance Officer + MLRO, AML/CTF systems). That's a regulatory wall — incorporation/co-sec/registered-office plays should be **deprioritized** given low regulatory appetite. The *accounting/bookkeeping/payroll* adjacencies around it are NOT TCSP-gated.

- **Customer support is a proven "sell the outcome" model but already a megafunded global race.** Sierra hit **$100M ARR (Oct 2025, +400% YoY), $10B valuation**; Decagon ~$17M ARR (Apr 2025, +900% YoY), $650M val; Intercom **Fin at $0.99/resolution**, no seat/platform fees. Pricing is outcome-pegged (~$1.50/resolution; Sierra deals ~$150k/yr + $50–200k implementation). They cut tier-1 headcount 50–85%. Lesson for thesis: this is the canonical proof that "charge per resolved ticket, not per seat" works — but the English-language enterprise lane is taken. Whitespace = **APAC SME / multilingual (Cantonese, traditional Chinese, SEA languages) support**, where Sierra/Decagon are not localized and the buyers are smaller.

- **Recruiting/HR admin: AI sourcing is crowding fast, but it's tool-priced, not service-priced — and RPO economics leave room.** GoPerfect ($250/mo per open role, 800M profiles), Pin ($100/mo start, 850M profiles, sourcing+outreach+scheduling), Dover, Paradox. RPO incumbents charge $3,000–8,000/hire; staffing firms take 20–30% of first-year salary ($20–30k per $100k role). The arbitrage is huge. BUT profiles coverage is "100% North America/Europe" — **APAC candidate-data coverage is a gap**. A done-for-you "we fill the role" service (not a tool) priced against the $20k staffing fee, focused on APAC tech/SME hiring, is whitespace-adjacent. No license gate.

- **Tax/audit support: hot money, but the gating is CPA/sign-off — keep to the *support* layer, not the licensed deliverable.** Funding is flooding in: Basis ($1.15B valuation, AI agents for financial statements/tax returns, used by 30% of top-25 US firms), Juno ($12M seed, ~500 customers), Accordance ($13M), Black Ore, TaxGPT. India offshore-CPA disruption is an explicit narrative ("AI removes the cost-arbitrage that drove offshoring"). Almost all US-centric. The audit *opinion* and tax *filing sign-off* require a licensed CPA — deprioritize owning that. The **un-gated** play: sell prep/workpaper/reconciliation *labor* to HK/SG accounting firms as a back-office service (the offshore-team replacement), letting the licensed firm sign off. Domain wall is moderate; partner with one firm.

- **Payroll/EOR is consolidated and capital-intensive — not a lean-team entry.** Deel ($300M Series E, 120+ countries, owned infra), Rippling ($450M Series G), Multiplier (Singapore, APAC-strong), Talenox (localized for SG/HK/MY payroll + leave). Running payroll = money movement + multi-jurisdiction statutory compliance + banking infra. Heavy regulatory + capital lift; contradicts lean/low-reg constraints. Deprioritize as a core, though payroll-*adjacent* admin (e.g., MPF filing prep in HK) could be a thin wedge.

- **AML/KYC: structurally a RegTech/licensed-buyer space — wrong fit for low-reg appetite.** Buyers are banks/FIs (90% projected to use AI for AML by 2025); HK Stablecoins Ordinance (live Aug 1 2025) and FATF tightening drive demand. Players: KnowYourCustomer (HK), Sumsub, Moody's, iFinGate, Faceki. Selling *into* regulated FIs means long enterprise cycles and you inherit their compliance burden. Deprioritize unless you sell pure ops labor (e.g., periodic-review document gathering) rather than the regulated decision.

- **E-commerce seller back-office is OPEN, un-licensed, and APAC-relevant — under-claimed by AI-native service players.** No dominant AI-native "we run your Amazon/Shopify back-office" service yet; the field is Amazon's own agentic Seller Assistant/Creative Studio (helps the seller, doesn't replace the agency), thin point tools (Ecomtent $1.4M seed for AI listings; eDesk helpdesk), and traditional human agencies whose "manual delivery can no longer scale profitably." HK/SG/Shenzhen has a dense cross-border seller base. A done-for-you AI back-office (listings, catalog, case/dispute handling, reconciliation, multilingual buyer messages) priced against agency retainers looks like genuine whitespace with a low domain wall. No license gate.

- **The cleanest thesis fit = un-gated, service-priced, APAC-localized ops labor.** Ranking by founder constraints (low-reg, lean, no deep vertical, HK/APAC, B2B): **most attractive** = e-commerce seller back-office, APAC/multilingual customer support, accounting-firm back-office labor (tax prep/recon as a service to licensed firms); **mid** = AI recruiting-as-a-service for APAC, localized SME bookkeeping (Bench-risk); **deprioritize** = company-secretary/incorporation (TCSP), payroll/EOR (capital+statutory), AML/KYC (regulated buyer), audit/tax sign-off (CPA). Saturation is concentrated in US bookkeeping and English enterprise support; APAC localization is the recurring open seam.
