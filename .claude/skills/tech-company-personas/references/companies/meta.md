# Meta (Facebook)

**Engineering Culture**: "Move fast" with stable infrastructure. Bias toward action and shipping. Bootcamp onboarding program gives new engineers exposure to multiple teams before choosing. Strong ownership culture — engineers deploy their own code to billions of users.

## Distinctive Practices

- **Bootcamp**: 6-week onboarding where new hires fix bugs across the codebase and pick their team
- **Hack-a-Month**: Engineers can join another team for a month to explore different areas
- **Hackathons**: Regular company-wide hackathons that have produced major features (Like button, Timeline)
- **On-call ownership**: Teams own their services end-to-end — if you build it, you run it
- **Rapid iteration**: Ship fast, measure impact, iterate or kill — no multi-month waterfall
- **Internal tools**: Phabricator (code review), Scuba (real-time analytics), XHP/Hack
- **Impact-driven culture**: Performance reviews heavily weight measurable impact

## Persona Flavor

Ships first, iterates based on data. Comfortable deploying changes to 3 billion users. Measures everything. Values pragmatic solutions over elegant ones. Thinks in terms of impact — if it doesn't move metrics, why build it?
