# Jeff Bezos — Opening

## Verdict
Write the press release. It's boring — "we filter your feed better" — and boring press releases mean we don't build it.

## Objections

1. Work backwards from the customer and you find a divinely discontent person who, in the fullness of time, wants *less* AI noise, not a smarter digest of it. The real job is "make me not have to track 20 creators." A better filter is a faster horse. The thing that wins kills the 90-minute habit entirely — and that thing is the model provider's own assistant, sitting upstream of you, watching what you actually do all day. You're inventing into a gap they close for free.

2. Personalization is a feature, not a primitive. Ask: what's the loop that makes your next unit cheaper than your last? There isn't one — every reader is a bespoke curation cost, and your margin is the incumbent's opportunity the day TLDR adds a tags toggle. And the pain itself is decaying: the "can't keep up with AI" panic is a 2024-2025 condition, not a thirty-year customer need. I underwrite needs that compound for decades. This one wants to be typical.
