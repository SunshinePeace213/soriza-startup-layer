# Hypothesis: Legal Document Review Interface — Per-Lawyer Customizable Layout

## Sharpened Hypothesis Sentence

**Solo lawyers and small law firms (2–10) in Hong Kong reviewing 2–5 contracts/week spend 30–90 minutes per contract manually annotating and cross-referencing clauses in PDFs or generic document tools. A document review tool that renders each contract in the lawyer's preferred layout (checklist-ordered-by-severity, obligation-timeline-as-calendar, risk-flagged-by-category) would reduce review time by >20% and achieve adoption via outcome-based pricing (pay-per-completion).**

---

## Four Dimensions (Provisional, To-Be-Tested)

### WHO
**Solo practitioners and small law firms (2–10 lawyers) in Hong Kong, handling commercial contracts, conveyancing, and employment law in English and Chinese bilingual documents.**

- *Segment context:* No in-house support staff; handle high-stakes legal work independently.
- *Geography:* Hong Kong-based; English–Chinese bilingual is standard.
- *Practice areas:* Commercial contracts, conveyancing, employment law.

### HOW OFTEN
**2–5 document reviews per week per solo lawyer.**

- *Rationale:* Typical solo-lawyer caseload based on Hong Kong commercial/employment/conveyancing practice. (To be validated in customer discovery.)

### HOW SEVERE
**30–90 minutes per contract spent on manual annotation, cross-referencing, and clause extraction in unspecialized tools (PDFs, Word, generic document platforms).**

- *Cost drivers:* No specialized legal tool; manual clause flagging, cross-referencing clauses across the document; extracting obligations and deadlines; re-organizing for risk assessment or timeline view.
- *Magnitude:* For a solo lawyer at ~HKD 500–1000/hour effective rate, this represents HKD 250–1500 of non-billable time per contract.

### STATUS QUO
**Solo lawyers currently:
- Review contracts inside PDFs or generic document tools (Word, Adobe, Notion, unspecialized LMS platforms).
- Manually annotate clauses, highlight risks, and cross-reference terms.
- Use checklists or spreadsheets to track obligations and deadlines.
- Each lawyer has a different workflow (clause-by-clause, risk-category-based, deadline-backward), but all tools force a single read–annotate interface.**

---

## Testable Claims (To-Be-Validated in Gate 2 & Customer Discovery)

1. **Pain quantification:** Solo lawyers in Hong Kong spend 30–90 min/contract on manual annotation/cross-referencing.
2. **Workflow divergence:** Each lawyer has a distinct review workflow (checklist, timeline, risk-flagged) that current tools do not support.
3. **Time savings:** A multi-layout tool reduces review time by >20%.
4. **Pricing model fit:** Outcome-based pricing (pay-per-completion) aligns with the lawyer's billing model and achieves adoption.
5. **Market signal:** Vertical legal AI (Harvey, $195M ARR) demonstrates 30–45% WTP premium in legal; solo-firm tier in Asia is unaddressed.
