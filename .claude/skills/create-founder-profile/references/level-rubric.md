# Skill level rubric — the objective standard

A **founder-agnostic** scale for rating any skill (technical or non-technical) in the dossier. It is a
general guideline, not tailored to any one founder — the same way a VC holds one bar per competency and
measures every founder against it. Keeping the standard here (and the founder's actual claims in the
dossier) is the same separation the layer enforces for `CLAUDE.md` vs the profile: the **standard** is
reusable and shared; the **data** is per-founder.

A rating is a **level + the evidence that earns it**. A level with no evidence behind it is not a
rating — it's a guess, and intake challenges it rather than recording it.

## The four levels, anchored on five axes

Judge a skill on these five axes, then pick the level that most of them point to. They won't always
agree (someone can architect systems solo but only on small-scale projects) — name the split in the
evidence rather than forcing a single clean number.

| Axis | junior | mid | senior | expert |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Years** of real use | < 1 | 1–3 | 3–7 | 7+ (or less with exceptional depth) |
| **Scope** owned | a feature, with guidance | a component end-to-end | a whole system / multiple components | architecture across systems; sets the approach |
| **Scale** shipped to | toy / coursework / sandbox | internal or low-traffic production | real production users | high-scale / high-stakes production |
| **Autonomy** | needs review and direction | works independently on defined tasks | defines the task, unblocks self | sets direction for others; the person others ask |
| **Recency** | dormant / dated | used within ~2 years | current, in active use | current and at the leading edge |

## Calibration notes

- **Recency is a real axis, not a footnote.** A skill last used five years ago is not "senior" today
  regardless of past depth — record it at the level it's currently usable at, and note the gap.
- **Non-technical skills use the same scale.** "Senior" at sales means owning a quota / closing real
  deals at real scale autonomously — not "I've read about sales." Hold the same bar.
- **Self-directed learning counts as evidence, but at the level it has actually reached.** "Ramped
  hard on X for six months and shipped two real projects with it" is genuine mid-level evidence;
  "did a course in X" without shipped work is junior, however intensive the course.
- **The evidence must match the level.** "Expert, 8 years, led the platform team, ran it at millions
  of requests/day" earns expert. "Expert" with "built a side project once" does not — that's the
  mismatch intake is there to catch.
