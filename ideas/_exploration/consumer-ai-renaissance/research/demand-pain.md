# Demand / Pain Validation — Findings

## Key findings

- **Wedding planning: $3,000–$15,000 for full-service planners, with a measurable DIY exodus.** Couples' active workaround is spreadsheet-based self-planning — dedicated Facebook groups and sites (A Practical Wedding, Bellamyloft) distribute free Google Sheets covering budget, guest list, vendor tracking, and day-of timelines. The Knot publishes its own spreadsheet template to capture couples who won't pay a planner. The sheer volume of "free wedding planner spreadsheet" resources signals mass demand for structured guidance that people cannot afford to outsource. Average wedding: 200–500 hours of coordination labor. AI-guided event planning with light human review sits directly in this unserved gap.

- **Career coaching: $272/session average, $1,500–$5,000 per engagement, and job seekers are already using raw ChatGPT as a DIY substitute.** The career coaching market was $17.8B in 2024. Harvard's career center and multiple universities now publish "Career Coach GPT" guides, acknowledging that job seekers use ChatGPT to draft resumes, cover letters, and prep interview answers — but the output "is likely not enough to help you land a job interview" without human personalization. The demand signal is strong: people are self-serving with free AI, tolerate mediocre results, and would pay for a structured, human-reviewed version they trust.

- **Immigration legal services: 65%+ of people facing removal are unrepresented; standard filings cost $1,000–$7,000+ in lawyer fees.** The immigration legal services market was $20.54B in 2024 and growing at 6.7% CAGR. Demand spiked in 2025 with deportation policy changes driving attorney backlogs and higher fees. CitizenPath (self-filing guide SaaS) has grown as direct evidence of DIY demand. The "most people have straightforward situations but want confidence they're doing everything correctly" quote from CitizenPath names the exact product gap: structured, stepwise AI guidance with a human review checkpoint is a direct substitute for the $1,000+ retainer entry point.

- **Probate / estate admin: California alone sees statutory fees >$16,000 on a $500K estate, with families dealing with grief and complexity simultaneously.** Real user pain language: "you're often left managing not only the emotions but also the financial tasks that follow" and "enduring the arduous and time-consuming probate process may feel like an overwhelming responsibility." The task list (court filing, newspaper notice, referee appraisal, asset inventory, IRS filing) is procedural and repetitive — exactly the kind of multi-step admin that an AI workflow agent can absorb. No major consumer-facing AI product addresses this yet; the market is served almost entirely by estate attorneys billing $300–$500/hr.

- **New parent admin: 79% of parents with children under 18 already use AI (vs. 54% non-parents), making it the highest-propensity cohort.** Menlo Ventures 2025 data shows parents use AI daily at nearly 2x the rate of non-parents, with childcare management cited by 34%. The "new baby" admin load — birth certificate, SSA number, insurance deadline (30 days), pediatrician selection, will update, beneficiary update — is a finite, time-pressured checklist that causes real anxiety for first-time parents. This is a well-defined, completable task set rather than an open-ended service, making it a natural AI product scope.

- **Mental health / therapy: 41% of people sought mental health support but only 9% used AI for it — the largest demand-usage gap in the Menlo data.** But trust is a hard blocker: 8% of Americans trust AI for mental health recommendations, 53% distrust it; 53% specifically cite "lack of human emotional nuance" as their concern, and 49% fear inaccurate/harmful advice (YouGov 2025). This vertical has huge latent demand but the trust floor is structurally low. An indie product here needs a strong human-in-the-loop signal to be credible — not a pure AI play.

- **Home buying: 5.57% total commission unchanged after 2024 NAR settlement; "junk fees" of $200–$1,895 added on top; first-time buyers bear the full cost.** The post-NAR settlement landscape created pricing transparency without reducing fees, and it opened a window for flat-fee alternatives (NPR, May 2025 coverage). First-time buyers face overlapping stressors: mortgage qualification, neighborhood research, inspection, closing admin. 66% of people do home maintenance tasks but only 13% use AI — suggesting AI-guided home ownership is another under-penetrated life-stage vertical.

- **Relocation / moving to a new city: no organized consumer market for personal relocation consulting (relocation packages are employer-paid for corporate moves).** Individual movers cobble together generic guides. One survey found 29% of people say "they'd rather get a divorce than move again." Pain is real but the market for paid personal relocation guidance is thin compared to other verticals — signal is moderate. The job-to-city move use case (first job in a new city: neighborhood, lease, banking, registrations) may be more tightly scoped and thus more viable as an AI product than general relocation.

- **Human-in-the-loop is the unlock, not the exception.** Zendesk's July 2025 global survey: 46% of consumers say "availability of human oversight or support" would be the top factor increasing their willingness to use personal AI assistants. TD Bank survey: 80% of Americans prefer interacting with people over machines for important tasks. Across every vertical above, the product framing of "AI does the heavy lifting, a human expert reviews and approves" converts skeptics — pure AI chat loses them. This is why the thesis's human-in-the-loop angle is a genuine differentiator, not just a hedge.

- **AI willingness-to-pay is concentrated in life-stage complexity, not demographics.** Menlo Ventures 2025: "life-stage complexity drives adoption more than age or income alone." Parents, students (85% usage rate), and employed adults (75%) are highest adopters. This directly validates the life-event targeting angle: targeting people mid-event (planning a wedding, navigating a job search, having a first child) gets users at peak motivation and peak willingness to pay — unlike trying to sell a generic AI subscription to disengaged users.
