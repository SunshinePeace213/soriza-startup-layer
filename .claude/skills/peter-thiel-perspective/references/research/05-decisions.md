## Decision Log

### Decision 1: Founding *The Stanford Review* (1987)
- **Date / Context:** 1987, sophomore year at Stanford. The university had just replaced its required "Western Culture" curriculum with "Culture, Ideas and Values," emphasizing multiculturalism. Reagan was president; conservative students felt embattled on elite campuses.
- **Stated rationale:** Provide an "alternative voice" against a dominant cultural-liberal orthodoxy that "alienated and misrepresented" a moderate mainstream.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Build the first node of a *contrarian network*. The Review functioned as a recruiting funnel — Sacks, Howery, and later PayPal Mafia members ran through it. Thiel didn't want a debate; he wanted an institution he controlled.
- **Outcome:** Still publishing in 2026. Produced PayPal Mafia, *The Diversity Myth* (1995, co-authored with Sacks), and a pipeline that fed Founders Fund, Palantir, and Anduril leadership.
- **Reveals:** At 19 he already preferred building parallel institutions to reforming existing ones. The contrarian-as-institution-builder pattern is present from the start.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia *Stanford Review*; Stanford Review history page; *The Contrarian* (Chafkin).

### Decision 2: Quitting Sullivan & Cromwell after 7 months (1993)
- **Date / Context:** After Stanford Law ('92), clerked, then joined S&C's NY office. Lasted seven months and three days. Quit to trade currency derivatives at Credit Suisse (1993–96).
- **Stated rationale:** Later told Fortune (2014) it was the "unhappiest period" of his life; competition was "cutthroat" and he felt the prestige treadmill led nowhere.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Lost the Supreme Court clerkship competition (Kennedy / Scalia, 1992-93) — a wound he later said taught him to "stop competing on metrics other people set." S&C was a consolation track; the wound, not the work, drove the exit.
- **Outcome:** Three years on the CS derivatives desk gave him capital, options-pricing intuition, and risk vocabulary he later applied at Clarium and to startups. Returned to California 1996 with starter capital for what became Thiel Capital → Confinity.
- **Reveals:** When a competitive game falls short of his self-image, he doesn't grind — he *exits the game* and reframes it as the game's fault. Sour-grapes-as-strategy.
- **Sources:** Stanford Lawyer Q&A; Fortune 2014; Chafkin, *The Contrarian*.

### Decision 3: Founding Confinity / PayPal and selling to eBay (1998–2002)
- **Date / Context:** Confinity founded Dec 1998 with Levchin, Nosek. Merged with Musk's X.com in 2000. IPO Feb 2002. Sold to eBay Oct 2002 for $1.5B.
- **Stated rationale (early):** Create a "new world currency" free of government control — explicit libertarian / digital-cash vision pitched to staff.
- **Actual rationale at exit (inferred):** Post-dot-com crash, with regulatory threats and fraud bleeding cash, took the certain $55M (his 3.7%) over the moonshot. Pragmatism beat ideology when the numbers got real.
- **Outcome:** $55M personal payday; created the "PayPal Mafia" network (Musk, Levchin, Sacks, Howery, Hoffman, Botha, Rabois, Nosek). That network is arguably his greatest produced asset.
- **Reveals:** He'll utter revolutionary rhetoric and then take the practical exit. The *network* he built was more valuable than the IPO; he optimized for the long-tail relationships, not the headline price.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia *PayPal*; Britannica; Quartr write-up.

### Decision 4: $500K into Facebook for 10.2% (Aug 2004)
- **Date / Context:** Sean Parker introduced Zuckerberg, June 2004. Thiel met him in August. Wrote a $500K convertible note at a $4.9M valuation — conditioned on Facebook hitting 1.5M users by year-end. They missed; he converted anyway.
- **Stated rationale:** Cited Girard's mimetic theory — Facebook *was* mimetic desire instantiated as software. Said you couldn't lose much on $500K and it could be huge.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Wrote a small check on a *very* favorable convertible because (a) he liked the founder, (b) the deal terms were friendly, (c) he wanted a foothold in consumer internet after PayPal. Girard came later as the *narrative*; the *behavior* was a cheap call option.
- **Outcome:** ~$1B+ realized over 2012-2017 sales. Joined board 2005, departed Feb 2022.
- **Reveals:** Pattern-match for big-asymmetry bets; uses intellectual scaffolding (Girard) to dignify what was structurally a small bet on a young founder.
- **Sources:** Startup Archive interview with Thiel; CNN Money 2012; Wikipedia.

### Decision 5: Founding Palantir (2003–04)
- **Date / Context:** Conceived 2003 with Karp, Cohen, Lonsdale, Gettings, immediately post-9/11. Initial $30M largely from Thiel's own pocket after Sequoia and other VCs passed. In-Q-Tel (CIA) put in ~$2M.
- **Stated rationale:** Apply PayPal anti-fraud techniques to terrorism; preserve civil liberties by giving government better-targeted tools ("more security AND more privacy").
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** A bet on the post-9/11 surveillance build-out being a multi-decade government appetite. Civil-liberties framing was real *and* commercially useful — it differentiated Palantir from defense incumbents who wouldn't lead with that line.
- **Outcome:** IPO 2020. Market cap reached ~$300B+ range in 2025. Now a core US/Five-Eyes contractor. Thiel's stake worth tens of billions.
- **Reveals:** Sees government as a customer, not just an opponent. Comfortable building infrastructure for the surveillance state while publicly defending small-l liberty. Operational pragmatism > ideological purity.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia *Palantir*; Fast Company 2020.

### Decision 6: Founding Founders Fund + "What Happened to the Future?" (2005 / 2011)
- **Date / Context:** Fund founded 2005 with Howery and Nosek; $50M Fund I. Manifesto written by Bruce Gibney in 2011: "We wanted flying cars, instead we got 140 characters."
- **Stated rationale:** VC had become "a funder of features, widgets, irrelevances." Need to fund the future again — biotech, energy, defense, space.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Branding/positioning. By 2011 Sequoia, A16Z, and Benchmark had locked up the consumer-tech mindshare. Founders Fund needed differentiation; "atoms not bits" was a positioning wedge as much as a worldview.
- **Outcome:** Backed SpaceX (first institutional check), Anduril, Stripe, Palantir, Airbnb, Anthropic, OpenAI. By 2025: ~$6B late-stage AI fund raised. Pattern is now mainstream.
- **Reveals:** He invents categories *and* the language to justify them, then occupies the category before competitors arrive. Marketing is downstream of philosophy, and both are inputs to deal flow.
- **Sources:** Founders Fund manifesto; Wikipedia *Founders Fund*.

### Decision 7: The Thiel Fellowship (Sept 2010)
- **Date / Context:** Announced at TechCrunch Disrupt 2010. $100K (later $200K) to people under 23 who would skip or drop out of college for two years.
- **Stated rationale:** Higher ed is the "next bubble," analogous to housing and dot-com. Talented kids would learn more building things.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** A *publicity-and-recruitment instrument* dressed as philanthropy. The fellowship gave Thiel first look at exceptional young talent (Vitalik Buterin, Laura Deming, Austin Russell, Dylan Field, Paul Gu) at zero competition. Also: an attack on the credentialing system that had wounded him at S&C.
- **Outcome:** Fellows produced Ethereum (Buterin), Figma (Field), Luminar (Russell), Upstart (Gu). Far more impactful than most university research grants of comparable size.
- **Reveals:** When he says "this institution is broken," translate to: "I want to be the parallel institution." Also, willingness to spend small to identify big — same option-pricing instinct as the Facebook check.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia *Thiel Fellowship*; Chronicle of Higher Education.

### Decision 8: Secretly funding Hulk Hogan v. Gawker (~$10M, 2012–2016)
- **Date / Context:** Gawker outed him as gay in Dec 2007 ("Peter Thiel is totally gay, people"). In 2012 he began secretly bankrolling lawsuits via attorney Charles Harder. Bollea (Hogan) won $140M in March 2016; Gawker filed bankruptcy June 2016. Thiel's role revealed by Forbes May 2016.
- **Stated rationale:** After exposure: "one of my greater philanthropic things." Framed as defense of privacy against bullying outlets.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Revenge, executed cold over nine years through a proxy. The choice of Bollea — a case with strong privacy facts — let him weaponize a personal grievance through a sympathetic plaintiff.
- **Outcome:** Gawker destroyed. Chilled tabloid coverage of billionaires. Established litigation-financing as a tool of the rich against the press. His personal cost: ~$10M, which he could afford easily.
- **Reveals:** Long memory. Patient. Comfortable using legal infrastructure as a weapon. Asymmetric warfare via the courts — he doesn't fight on the press's terms; he changes the terrain.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia *Bollea v. Gawker*; Forbes 2016; First Amendment Watch.

### Decision 9: Endorsing Trump at RNC + $1.25M (July–Oct 2016)
- **Date / Context:** Spoke at the Republican National Convention in Cleveland, July 21, 2016 — the first openly gay speaker at an RNC to identify as such on stage. Donated $1.25M to Trump and aligned super-PACs in October.
- **Stated rationale:** "Fake culture wars distract us from economic decline." Trump was the only candidate "being honest" about American decline. Identified as a heterodox figure willing to break ranks.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Bet on disruption itself. Saw Trump less as a leader to be enthroned and more as a wrecking ball against the technocratic consensus Thiel had been attacking since the Founders Fund manifesto. Cheap option: $1.25M is rounding-error money for the entry it bought to a possible administration.
- **Outcome:** Joined Trump transition team. Placed allies (Michael Kratsios) in government. Palantir contracts grew. Reputation in Silicon Valley took a hit — but he was already moving to LA.
- **Reveals:** Treats politics as venture investing — buy when others won't, accept reputational drawdown for asymmetric upside.
- **Sources:** Fortune Jul 2016; CBC Oct 2016.

### Decision 10: $32M+ on Vance & Masters (2022 Senate)
- **Date / Context:** $15M to Protect Ohio Values PAC for Vance's primary; ~$15M to Saving Arizona PAC for Masters; additional sums to other candidates. Total cycle: ~$35M to 16 federal candidates.
- **Stated rationale:** Reshape the GOP toward a more economically nationalist, anti-tech-monopoly, anti-China posture. Vance and Masters were ex-Thiel-Capital employees.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Vertically integrate his political stack. He didn't fund *types* of candidates; he funded his *own people*. Vance had worked for him at Mithril; Masters had co-authored *Zero to One*. He was buying *his* faction influence inside the Republican party.
- **Outcome:** Vance wins Ohio. Masters loses Arizona. Vance becomes Trump's VP pick in 2024 — arguably the highest-ROI political investment in modern US history. Cost: $15M. Result: a heartbeat from the presidency.
- **Reveals:** Thinks in *talent pipelines*, not policy platforms. Fund the person you trust; the policies will follow.
- **Sources:** WaPo May 2022; CNBC Nov 2022; CBS News 2024.

### Decision 11: Announcing 2022/2023 he'd stop political donations — then re-engaging
- **Date / Context:** After Vance's primary win (May 2022) said he'd wind down. April 2023 Bloomberg: "no plans to give in 2024." Nov 2023 Atlantic interview ("scream for help" quote): said he was *locking himself in* by talking publicly. By 2024 cycle he had given >$1.7M federal. By 2024-25 reportedly remained behind-the-scenes for Vance and the administration.
- **Stated rationale:** Republicans too focused on culture-war; politics is a depressing time-sink; "comparative advantage isn't here."
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** The headline number dropped, but influence didn't. Pulled back from being the *visible* megadonor (which had attracted backlash) while keeping the *operative* relationships (Vance as VP). Public withdrawal as a brand-management move.
- **Outcome:** Got the upside (Vance as VP, allies in admin) without owning the downside (visible-megadonor scrutiny).
- **Reveals:** "Said X / did Y" is a recurring move. He'll telegraph withdrawal to lower expectations while continuing the underlying activity at lower visibility.
- **Sources:** Bloomberg Apr 2023; The Atlantic Nov 2023; Yahoo Finance follow-up; FEC filings.

### Decision 12: Selling Palantir shares — timing (2020 onward, accelerating 2024–26)
- **Date / Context:** Direct-listed Sep 2020 at ~$10. Began sales soon after. Sept 2024: filed to sell up to $1B by Dec 2025 under 10b5-1 plan. Through March 2026 sold ~$600M at avg $36.90; further sales ongoing. Indirect holdings via Rivendell 7, Rivendell 25, PLTR Holdings still substantial.
- **Stated rationale:** Routine pre-arranged 10b5-1 trades, diversification.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Selling into euphoric AI-adjacent retail demand at multiples of his cost basis. Classic Thiel: take chips off the table at the top of the narrative cycle while keeping enough exposure to participate in further upside.
- **Outcome:** Locked in ~$1B+ in liquidity; Palantir stock continued volatile. Diversifies into Founders Fund deals, real estate, Bitcoin, etc.
- **Reveals:** Same exit pattern as Facebook 2012 (sold most at lock-up). He's *not* a HODL ideologue. When liquidity is generous, he sells, regardless of his public bullishness on the asset.
- **Sources:** SEC Form 4 filings; Globe & Mail; Motley Fool; Nasdaq.

### Decision 13: Backing Anduril, SpaceX, Neuralink, OpenAI, Bitcoin early
- **Date / Context:**
  - SpaceX: Founders Fund first institutional check, 2008 ($20M, when Musk was nearly broke after Falcon 1 failures).
  - Anduril: Founders Fund led 2017 seed after backing Palmer Luckey post-Oculus/Facebook firing; $1B Series G commitment June 2025.
  - Neuralink: Founders Fund early backer.
  - OpenAI: Thiel personally among 2015 founding donors (~$10M cohort with Musk, Hoffman, Altman).
  - Bitcoin: Founders Fund quietly buying since 2012; built ~$1B+ position; sold for ~$1.8B spring 2022; reinvested ~$200M in 2023.
- **Stated rationale:** "Atoms not bits" / future-funding thesis from the 2011 manifesto. Anduril: counter China militarily. Bitcoin: honest market, hedge against fiat.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Concentrated bets on *people he already knew* in domains where regulation, scale, or contrarianism kept fast-following competitors out. Musk (SpaceX), Luckey (Anduril after Facebook fired him for political donation — a Thiel-grievance match), Altman (mentee). The "network" decides the deals; the thesis decorates them.
- **Outcome:** SpaceX ~10% stake, valued at $35B+ paper. Anduril ~$30B valuation 2025. Bitcoin trade returned ~10x. OpenAI position highly valuable post-restructuring.
- **Reveals:** Personnel scouting is his real edge. He invests in people who have already been rejected/ousted/marginalized by mainstream institutions — and who therefore owe him loyalty.
- **Sources:** Hustle Fund analysis; CB Insights; Wikipedia *Founders Fund*; DL News.

### Decision 14: Funding longevity research (Methuselah / SENS, 2006 onward)
- **Date / Context:** 2006: $500K over 3 years to Methuselah Foundation; pledged 50¢-on-the-dollar match up to $3M through 2009. 2017: another $3.5M pledge. Continued through Thiel Foundation. Also invested in longevity biotechs (Unity, Genomic Prediction, Halcyon, Counsyl).
- **Stated rationale:** Death is "the great enemy." Aging is a "solvable engineering problem." Has said he takes HGH and is signed up for cryonics with Alcor.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Personal stakes. He wants to live as long as possible. Philanthropy aligned with self-interest is honest; he's said as much.
- **Outcome:** Pulled longevity research into mainstream credibility before Bezos (Altos Labs), Brin, Ellison joined later. Catalyzed a sub-sector.
- **Reveals:** Will fund his own personal anxieties (death, decline, mediocrity) at scale and call it civilization-saving. Self-interest as legitimate philanthropic motive.
- **Sources:** Methuselah Foundation press; FightAging archives; Inside Philanthropy.

### Decision 15: Pronomos Capital + Praxis (charter cities, 2017–present)
- **Date / Context:** 2019: anchored Pronomos Capital with $9M alongside Andreessen, Ver, Srinivasan. Founded by Patri Friedman (post-seasteading). Invested in Próspera (Honduras), Praxis (Brown's NYC-based "next great city" project — raised up to $525M late 2025).
- **Stated rationale:** Need to "experiment with new forms of government" outside incumbent regulatory regimes; competition between jurisdictions improves them.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** A land-based replacement for failed seasteading. Same goal — *exit* from democracy — by less science-fictional means.
- **Outcome:** Próspera mired in legal fight with new Honduran government (post-2022). Praxis still vaporware as of late 2025. Returns minimal; ideological signal large.
- **Reveals:** "Exit over voice" is a *durable* preference, not a phase. When one exit mechanism fails (seasteading) he funds the next (charter cities). When that fails he'll fund the next.
- **Sources:** Daily Beast on Pronomos; Truthout on Próspera; InsideHook on Praxis.

### Decision 16: Seasteading donation to Patri Friedman (2008, 2011) — then distancing (2017)
- **Date / Context:** April 2008: $500K to the Seasteading Institute. 2011: $1.25M but resigned from board same year. 2017 NYT interview: "Not quite feasible from an engineering perspective."
- **Stated rationale (early):** Build floating libertarian micro-nations as competitive jurisdictions. Stated (late): engineering was the problem.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Got bored / saw it wouldn't scale on his timeline. Cut losses without admitting the *vision* was wrong — only that this *implementation* was. Recycled goal into Pronomos.
- **Outcome:** ~$1.75M in donations, no operational seasteads. French Polynesia project collapsed 2018 amid local opposition.
- **Reveals:** He'll abandon a *project* he funded publicly and reuse the underlying *ideology* in new packaging. Treats philosophical commitments as durable, specific bets as disposable.
- **Sources:** Seasteading Institute press; Phys.org 2018; Wikipedia.

### Decision 17: Moving from San Francisco to Los Angeles (2018)
- **Date / Context:** Feb 2018: announced relocation of personal residence, Thiel Capital, and Thiel Foundation to LA. Publicly trashed SV as "monocultural," "toxic," intolerant of conservatives.
- **Stated rationale:** Group-think, ideological intolerance, cost of living.
- **Actual rationale (inferred):** Post-Trump-endorsement, his social standing in SF was untenable. Pre-emptive exit framed as principled denunciation. Also: LA is closer to Hollywood/media (relevant to political battles) and equidistant to DC.
- **Outcome:** Catalyzed a small but real SF-to-LA exodus of right-leaning techies. By 2020-22 a larger SF-to-Austin/Miami wave followed.
- **Reveals:** Doesn't tolerate environments where he's the loser of the social game. Will exit a city before staying and losing. Same pattern as S&C.
- **Sources:** Seattle Times Feb 2018; CNBC Feb 2018.

## Cross-Decision Patterns

**1. Exit over voice (Hirschman, weaponized).**
S&C → Credit Suisse. SF → LA. SV consensus → MAGA. US → NZ citizenship hedge. Democracy → seasteading → charter cities. When losing an environment's game, he doesn't reform — he leaves and tries to build a parallel one. Seen in 9+ decisions (1, 2, 6, 7, 15, 16, 17, 21, partial in 18).

**2. Invest in his own people, not in theses.**
Vance hired at Mithril → funded for Senate → installed as VP. Masters co-authored *Zero to One* → funded for Senate. Altman mentored → backed at OpenAI → defended against Musk. Luckey fired by Facebook → funded as Anduril CEO. Karp (Stanford Law classmate) → CEO Palantir. The "theses" (atoms-not-bits, anti-China, longevity) are post-hoc decoration. The actual unit of investment is *a person he already trusts*. Seen in decisions 1, 5, 6, 7, 10, 13, 18, 20.

**3. Small bets with asymmetric option-value, then scale on winners.**
$500K Facebook. $500K Seasteading. $9M Pronomos. Initial $10M Clarium. $100K-per-Thiel-Fellow. He rarely makes huge initial bets. He prefers cheap-call-options on people, then doubles down when the bet works. Seen in decisions 4, 7, 13, 15, 16.

**4. Use philosophical framing as marketing for tactical moves.**
Girard for the Facebook check. "What Happened to the Future?" for VC differentiation. Antichrist lectures for anti-AI-regulation positioning. Bubble theory for the Fellowship. The frame is real to him *and* it's a positioning tool. He's the rare operator who can both *believe* his ideology and *deploy* it instrumentally. Seen in decisions 4, 6, 7, 14, 15, 18.

**5. Long-memory grievance, executed cold.**
Gawker outed him in 2007. He started funding the Hogan suit ~2012. Verdict 2016. Nine years of patient destruction. Sullivan & Cromwell rejection → contempt for credentialism → Thiel Fellowship.
