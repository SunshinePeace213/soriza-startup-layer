# Seed — HK/GBA Chinese-Wedding Bilingual Concierge

## The card

- **Problem:** HK/Greater Bay couples plan weddings the global tools ignore — bilingual vendor sourcing,
  quote comparison, contract negotiation, banquet-hall booking, and Chinese-wedding customs (tea ceremony,
  過大禮, dual ceremonies) — and either overpay a $3–15k planner or grind spreadsheets with no leverage.
  86% of couples report stress; 43% say it strains the relationship.
- **Who:** Budget-conscious engaged couples in Hong Kong / the Greater Bay Area planning a bilingual,
  customs-heavy wedding without a full planner.
- **Why now:** Wedding is the #1 life event by spend + stress and a proven per-event payer; The Knot/Zola
  don't serve HK/GBA; non-US wedding SEO + HK wedding communities are uncrowded and *reachable*; AI can now
  benchmark quotes and draft bilingual negotiation/contract language at near-zero marginal cost (token cost
  −98%; agentic payment rails live).
- **Demand signal:** Couples already pay $3–15k planners and hundreds to multiple vendors; dense
  DIY-spreadsheet workaround; active HK wedding communities (ESDlife, Baby-Kingdom, FB groups).
- **Moat / distribution read:** **Distribution is the win** — founder is trilingual + local and can *own*
  HK wedding communities incumbents can't reach (his #1 documented risk becomes his edge). Moat = local
  vendor-pricing data + cultural-workflow depth + the relationship (not the code). Per-event pricing dodges
  the AI-app churn trap (~21% annual retention); Zola→Zola Baby adjacency + referral credits solve
  one-and-done.

## Why it was recommended (advisory rationale carried forward)

Only seed on the slate where all five FDE "good idea in 2026" tests hold at once: **reachable niche ·
real painful recurring workflow · customer access · ownable distribution · solo-AI-buildable.** It is the
single idea where the founder's biggest documented risk — distribution — flips from weakness to structural
advantage (trilingual local owning HK wedding communities the global incumbents can't serve). Low
regulatory risk (vs the emigration runner-up and the MPF/fintech card), evergreen demand (vs the volatile
emigration wave), and runnable as a hand-delivered Lean **concierge MVP** before any agent is built.

## Open risks to carry into sharpening / disconfirm

1. **One-and-done churn** — a wedding is a single event. Load-bearing assumption: adjacency expansion
   (→ baby / home / anniversary) + referral credits + a vendor-side revenue line make the LTV work. Test it.
2. **Vendor-data cold start** — the moat is local vendor-pricing data the founder doesn't have yet. How is
   it bootstrapped (manual concierge gathering it by hand first)?
3. **Two-sided pull?** — is this a couple-side concierge, a vendor marketplace, or both? (idea_type tagged
   `services/gig` but it has marketplace dynamics — sharpen which side pays and who you serve first.)
4. **Margin discipline** — concierge cautionary tale (Magic/Saiga): automate 80% (research/drafting/
   benchmarking), spend human minutes only on judgment calls; price per-event/outcome, never per-hour.
5. **Is the pain acute enough to pay vs. free spreadsheets + The Knot's bolt-on AI?** — the core
   value/growth hypothesis to trip in `/sharpen-hypothesis` and real-couple interviews.
