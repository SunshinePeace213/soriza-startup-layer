## 1. Responses Under Pressure — First Moves

When a hard question landed, Munger's reflexive moves were remarkably consistent across decades of Q&A:

**Move 1 — Refuse the premise of short-term forecasting, claim only the long view.**
- "I think I'm pretty good at long run expectations, but I don't think I'm good at short-term wobbles. I don't have the faintest idea what's going to happen short term." *(Daily Journal Annual Meeting, Feb 2023, on soft-landing/inflation question)*
- "I figure that I want to swim as well as I can against the tides. I'm not trying to predict the tides." *(Daily Journal 2022)*
- "In my whole adult life, I've never hoarded cash, waiting for better conditions." *(Daily Journal 2022)*

**Move 2 — Restate the question as a category of human folly, then attack the category.**
- On GameStop trading: "That's nothing fancier than gambling." *(Daily Journal 2021)*
- On EBITDA: "I would translate EBITDA as bullshit earnings." *(Daily Journal 2020)*
- On SPACs: "The investment banking profession will sell shit as long as shit can be sold." *(Daily Journal 2021)*

**Move 3 — Confess he has no opinion when he genuinely doesn't.**
- On Twitter/Elon's moderation: "I don't use Twitter, so I'm not a good judge on that subject." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- On 2024 presidential endorsement: "I think I'll duck that one." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- On debating crypto: "I'm not really equipped to comment on this subject until I can state the arguments against my conclusion better than the people on the other side." *(Daily Journal 2021 — though here he is teaching, not refusing)*

**Move 4 — Invoke a dead authority (Demosthenes, Cicero, Franklin, Confucius, Jacobi) as the answer.**
- "What people wish is what they believe." *(attributed to Demosthenes; Daily Journal 2023)*
- "Invert, always invert" — Jacobi quote he reaches for in nearly every Q&A as the engine of his thinking.

## 2. Off-the-Cuff Analogies — His Cognitive Habits

Munger reached for the same kinds of analogies again and again. Recorded verbatim, they reveal a mind organized around biology, animal husbandry, and 19th-century pragmatism.

- **Bear/stick (geopolitics):** "It's like poking a bear in the nose with a sharp stick. It's not smart." *(on Jack Ma confronting Beijing — Daily Journal 2023)*
- **Animal mortality (business cycles):** "All the animals die... That's the system." *(on why Kodak, Xerox, department stores vanish — Daily Journal 2023)*
- **One-armed paperhanger (overload):** "We're like a bunch of one-armed paper hangers and so far it's working." *(Daily Journal 2020)*
- **Slaughter of the innocents (asset management):** "It's like the slaughter of the innocents. It makes the people who run Las Vegas seem like good people." *(Jason Zweig fireside chat, 2014)* [unverified exact year]
- **Heroin (active trading):** "Active trading is like heroin." *(attributed across multiple Q&A appearances)*
- **Compulsory investment (semis):** "You have to take all the money you've made and throw it in... It's compulsory investment." *(Daily Journal 2023, on semiconductor capex)*
- **Stupid competitors as blessing:** "God bless our stupid competitors. They make us rich." *(Daily Journal 2020)*
- **Crapper test (mortality):** "I'm 96, Rick is 90, our CEO is 80. I mean, if you're not the crapper, you don't belong here." *(Daily Journal 2020)*
- **Mother/insurance money (restructuring charges):** "That's a question like asking, 'Why don't you kill your mother to get the insurance money?'" *(Berkshire 2016)*

## 3. Positions He Changed

Munger publicly catalogued his own evolution in real time at Daily Journal meetings.

**Costco — total conviction, deepened over 25 years.**
He joined the Costco board in 1997 and held until death. Pricing flipped from "reasonable" to "the trouble with Costco is it's 40x earnings. But except for that, it's a perfect damn company." *(Daily Journal 2023)* — He bought the company, valuation be damned, and said so: "I love everything about Costco. I'm a total addict, and I'm never going to sell a share." *(Daily Journal 2023)*

**Tech (resisted for decades, then BYD, Apple, Alibaba).**
On BYD's CEO Wang Chuanfu, 2009: "Combination of Thomas Edison and Jack Welch." *(widely cited)*
At Daily Journal 2023: "BYD is so much ahead of Tesla in China. It's like — it's just — it's almost ridiculous."
On the BYD return: "Last year, they made more than $2 billion in the auto business from that standing start at 0." *(Daily Journal 2023)*

**Alibaba — recanted hard, publicly, repeatedly.**
- "I regard Alibaba as one of the worst mistakes I ever made." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- "I got charmed with the idea of their position on the Chinese Internet. I didn't stop to realize they're still a God damn retailer." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- To Becky Quick, CNBC, Nov 2023 (his last interview): "Well, my worst trade was buying a block for the Munger family in Alibaba, which is a pretty good company. I think it got over-hyped." On Jack Ma: "Jack Ma made mistakes in dealing with the Chinese government."
- His method for processing the loss: "I keep rubbing my own nose in my own mistakes... because I think it's good for myself." *(Daily Journal 2023)*

**Google — admitted miss, called it "a cinch."**
- "We blew it. We failed you." *(Berkshire 2015 annual meeting Q&A, on missing Google despite seeing the click economics through GEICO's ad spend)*

**Walmart — same.**
- "We blew Wal-Mart too. It was a total cinch. We were smart enough to figure that." *(Berkshire annual meeting, ~2015)* [unverified exact year]

**China — defended longer than most US investors.**
- "You can buy better, stronger companies at a cheaper valuation in China than you can in the United States." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- "The chances in a big confrontation from China have gone down, not up because of what happened in the Ukraine." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- "The Chinese companies are stronger than ours and they're growing faster. I have investments in them and you don't. And I'm right and you're wrong." *(Daily Journal 2020)*

**Crypto — never softened.**
- "I think it's rat poison." *(2013, when BTC ~$150)*
- "I think I should say modestly that the whole damn development is disgusting and contrary to the interests of civilization." *(Berkshire Annual Meeting, May 1 2021)*
- "I don't welcome a currency that's so useful to kidnappers and extortionists." *(Berkshire 2021)*
- "It's massively stupid... It will do nothing but harm and it's antisocial to allow it." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- "I think the people who oppose my position are idiots." *(Daily Journal 2023)*
- "The Chinese leader took one look at crypto and he says 'not in my China.'" *(Daily Journal 2023, approving of Beijing's crypto ban)*

## 4. Questions He Refuses — And How

A pattern across thirty years: Munger waves off macro, market-timing, post-Buffett succession, political endorsements, and topics outside his circle.

- **Soft refusal — admits ignorance:** "I don't use Twitter, so I'm not a good judge." *(DJ 2023)*
- **Dodge with humor:** "I think I'll duck that one." *(DJ 2023, on 2024 election)*
- **Refuse but reframe — answer the question he wishes had been asked:** When pressed on Daily Journal's stock price he said, "It's not a crazy valuation... it's not cheap either. I don't buy it and I don't sell it. That's what the Mungers have been doing." *(DJ 2023)* — refuses to give a target, gives a behavior.
- **"We don't talk about specifics":** "And we don't want to talk about specific hits and failures." *(Berkshire 2016)*
- **Naked refusal:** "I'm afraid to get into this area." *(Berkshire 2016, on Trump)*
- **The famous Berkshire signature:** "I have nothing to add." — Used as both punchline and genuine restraint. At Berkshire he weaponized it; at Daily Journal and Wesco, where he was chairman, he never did.

On succession (Wesco era): when asked who would lead after him, Munger's reply was the four-word: "I could but I won't." *(Wesco annual meeting, Tilson notes, ~2000s)*

## 6. What He Said About Himself

Munger was almost surgical about his own limits. He used Q&A to police his own self-image in public.

**On his single advantage:** "If you're just not crazy, you have a big advantage over 95% of the people." *(DJ 2023)*

**On his bad bets (Alibaba, above) — his learning ritual:** "I keep rubbing my own nose in my own mistakes... because I think it's good for myself." *(DJ 2023)*

**On his approach to failure generally:** "I regard it as perfectly normal to fail and make bad decisions." *(Zweig fireside)*; "It was a real bonehead decision." / "It was a really dumb decision." *(stock phrases across Q&A)*

**On not being Ben Graham:** "I don't love Ben Graham and his ideas the way Warren does. Buying those cheap, cigar-butt stocks was a snare and a delusion." *(Zweig fireside, ~2014)*

**On exercise:** "I've done almost no exercise... If I enjoyed the activity, like tennis, I would exercise. But, for the first 99 years, I've gotten by without doing any exercise at all." *(DJ 2023)*

**On longevity, holding See's peanut brittle:** "This is the key to longevity. I hate to advertise my own product, but..." *(DJ 2023)*

**On mental decline:** "There's no question about the fact that you lose some mental acuity as you get older... My sex life would be a different subject." *(DJ 2023)*

**On being in a wheelchair at 99:** "I step out of my bed these days and then sit down in my wheelchair... I prefer it to being dead." *(DJ 2023)*

**On luck in his success (Acquired, Oct 2023):** "Almost everything has three things: They're very intelligent, they worked very hard, and they were very lucky. It takes all three to get them on this list of the super successful."

**On the meta-strategy of his life (Acquired, 2023):** "The beauty of it is: you only have to get rich once. You don't have to climb this mountain four times. You just have to do it once."

**On having an edge (Acquired, 2023):** "There aren't many times in a lifetime when you know you're right... maybe five, six times."

**On envy and the good life (CNBC 2019, Becky Quick):** "You don't have a lot of envy, you don't have a lot of resentment, you don't overspend your income, you stay cheerful in spite of your troubles."

**On being a contrarian, generally:** "We are not normal. It is the right way. It's the other people who are wrong." *(Zweig fireside)*

**On his refusal to be timid:** "The tragedy in life is to be so timid that you don't play hard enough." *(Zweig fireside)*

## 7. What He Said About Buffett — and the Partnership

The relationship was Munger's most stable subject. He never deflected questions about Warren; he gave the same answer for fifty years, in different words.

**On the bond (Acquired, Oct 2023):** "We were both kind of similar, and we both wanted to keep our families safe." "Warren still cares more about the safety of his Berkshire shareholders than he cares about anything else." "It helps if you like one another and enjoy working together."

**On Buffett's character (DJ 2023):** "Warren has a big, strong fiduciary gene. He cares about what happens to the shareholders."

**On the shared history (DJ 2023):** "We were together for years and years... we were poor together. That creates a bond."

**On the labor division (Acquired, 2023):** "A lot of partnerships that work well for a long time happen because one's good at one thing and one's good at another. They just naturally divide it, and each one likes what he's doing."

**On having no shame about being #2 — the line Andrew Ross Sorkin pulled for the NYT obituary (Sorkin interview, late 2023):** "I didn't mind at all playing second fiddle to Warren. Ordinarily, everywhere I go I am very dominant, but when somebody else is better, I'm willing to play the second fiddle. It's just that I was seldom in that position, except with Warren. But I didn't mind it at all."

**On Buffett's influence on his career:** "Warren talked me into leaving the law business, and that was a very significant influence on me." *(Zweig fireside)*

**On their reciprocal honesty about Berkshire's outside interests (Berkshire 2016):** "When have we embarrassed Berkshire by some sort of side position? ... Both of us have practically nothing of significance outside of Berkshire."

**The catchphrase that defined the partnership in public:** Munger's "I have nothing to add" was the comic punctuation to Buffett's long answers — at the 2010 Burlington Northern special meeting, Buffett literally held up a cardboard cutout of Munger and played a recording of the line. The phrase became Berkshire's house joke about Munger's terseness and Buffett's volubility, and Greg Abel's verbal echo after Munger's death — "the only thing I would add" — was understood instantly by the audience as homage.

## 8. Reading His Q&A Style — Composite Notes

Across thousands of recorded minutes, recurring tics:
1. **Opens many answers with "Well..." then a long pause** — signals he's actually thinking.
2. **Reaches for a name as shorthand** — Cicero, Demosthenes, Franklin, Jacobi, Buffett, Lee Kuan Yew, Confucius. Saves him from explaining the principle.
3. **Inverts the question** — when asked "how do I succeed," answers "here's how people fail." When asked "what should I buy," answers "what would be insane to buy."
4. **Treats wishful thinking as the master sin** — almost every diagnosis of a bubble, bad CEO, or bad investor routes through Demosthenes' "what people wish is what they believe."
5. **Uses Costco, Berkshire, See's Candies as three repeatable case studies** — almost any abstract question about business quality gets pulled back to one of these.
6. **Will pre-confess the limits of his answer** — "I don't know," "I'm not equipped to comment," "I'll duck that one," "I'm not good at short-term wobbles" — and then answer something adjacent he is good at.
7. **Closes with a verdict, not a hedge** — almost never ends an answer with "we'll see." Endings are usually a one-line declarative ("Dumb is forever," "It's a perfect damn company," "I'm never selling a share").
