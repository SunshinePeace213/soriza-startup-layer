# Garry Tan — Timeline Research

A chronological reconstruction of Garry Tan's life and thought, from birth in Winnipeg to launching "Garry's List" in February 2026. Dates are cited where verifiable; approximate dates are flagged with `~`.

---

## Early Life & Education

- **1981** — Born in **Winnipeg, Canada** to immigrant parents.
  - Father: **Chinese Singaporean**, foreman in a machine shop.
  - Mother: **Burmese Chinese**, nursing assistant.
  - (Often described in shorthand as "Chinese-Filipino-American," but the more documented heritage is Chinese-Singaporean / Burmese-Chinese with a Canadian birth.)
- **1991** — Family relocates to **Fremont, California** (age ~10). Tan would later describe Fremont as a formative environment of immigrant strivers.
- **~1995** — Begins programming at age 14. Tells the story of cold-calling the Yellow Pages to land his first paid programming job.
- **~1999** — Graduates **American High School** (Fremont, CA).
- **1999–2003** — **Stanford University**, **BS in Computer Systems Engineering**. Hardware/software hybrid program — formative for his "designer-engineer" identity.

## Microsoft → Palantir → Posterous

- **2003** — Graduates Stanford. Joins **Microsoft** as a program manager (brief stint, ~1–2 years). He has publicly said the bureaucratic, slow culture of Microsoft is what radicalized him into startups.
- **~2005–2007** — Joins **Palantir Technologies** as approximately **employee #10**. Roles: designer, product manager, engineering manager. Founding member of the Palantir Finance engineering team.
  - **Designed the Palantir logo** (the head poring over a book, "the human-intelligence" mark). He has said it took five iterations.
- **2008 (Spring)** — Co-founds **Posterous** with Sachin Agarwal — a blogging platform whose hook was "post anything by email."
- **Summer 2008** — Posterous accepted into **Y Combinator Summer 2008 (S08) batch**. (Tan thus has the rare profile of YC alum → YC partner → YC CEO.)
- **2009–2011** — Posterous grows rapidly, briefly competes with Tumblr.
- **March 12, 2012** — **Twitter acquires Posterous** for a reported ~$20M (talent-and-tech acquisition). Most engineering staff moves to Twitter; the product is wound down.
- **February 15, 2013** — Tan announces Posterous will be shut down on April 30, 2013; **co-launches Posthaven** the same day with Brett Gibson to "save" users' sites — explicit promise: "Posthaven will never be sold." This becomes Tan's first stated "no exit" principle.

## YC Partner Era (2011–2015)

- **December 2010 / January 2011** — Joins **Y Combinator as a partner / designer-in-residence**. Works directly under Paul Graham and Jessica Livingston.
- **2011–2015** — Advises and funds ~700 companies, ~1,000+ founders.
  - Builds **Bookface** (YC's internal founder social network — still the central nervous system of YC alumni).
  - Builds the **Demo Day website** and key parts of the YC founder experience.
- **2012** — While still a YC partner, writes the **first seed check into Coinbase** (~$150,000) to Brian Armstrong, whom he had met in YC's S12 batch. This check eventually returns ~6,000x — one of the most-cited seed-stage wins in VC history.
- **November 2015** — Officially steps down as a YC partner to focus full-time on Initialized Capital.

## Initialized Capital Era (2012–2022)

- **2012** — Co-founds **Initialized Capital** with **Alexis Ohanian** (Reddit co-founder, also a YC partner) and **Harj Taggar** (also a YC partner). Operates a **$7M first fund**.
- **2013** — Raises a **$39M second fund**. Around this time, Initialized writes seed checks into **Instacart** and **Flexport** (both became unicorns).
- **2013–2014** — Seed checks into **Cruise (autonomous vehicles)** — later acquired by GM for ~$1B in 2016.
- **2015** — Harj Taggar leaves Initialized and returns to YC as a partner.
- **2018–2022** — Tan named on the **Forbes Midas List** every year 2018–2022.
- **2019** — Tan launches his **YouTube channel** (first upload September 6, 2019, a founder interview). Initially slow growth; channel becomes major after 2020.
- **2020** — During COVID, Tan dramatically increases content output: starts posting "Ask Garry" style explainer videos, founder interviews, and short startup advice clips. Channel grows to **60K+ subs by late 2021**, eventually crosses **150K+ subs**.
- **June 26, 2020** — **Alexis Ohanian publicly departs Initialized.** Officially, Ohanian wants to focus on pre-seed and his own fund (later 776). The split is framed publicly as amicable ("setting each other up for success"), but it leaves Tan effectively running Initialized solo.
- **August 14, 2020** — Initialized closes **Fund V at $230M** without Ohanian. **Brett Gibson** promoted to general partner.
- **2021 (April)** — **Coinbase direct-lists at ~$100B valuation.** Tan's $150K seed check is now worth several hundred million; reports estimate his personal Coinbase shares at ~$680M paper value at peak. Tan publicly states he is keeping the "vast majority" of his shares.
- **2021** — **Jennifer Wolf** named President of Initialized.
- **2022** — Tan increasingly visible on Twitter as a public-facing VC voice; YouTube channel becomes a primary recruiting funnel for founders.

## YC CEO Era (Jan 2023 → present)

- **August 29, 2022** — Y Combinator announces **Tan will become President and CEO**, effective January 2023, replacing **Geoff Ralston**. Tan becomes the **first Asian American to lead YC** and the first since Sam Altman to be both President AND CEO.
- **January 2023** — Tan formally takes over YC. Becomes "strategic advisor" (not active partner) at Initialized.
- **2023 batches under Tan:**
  - **Winter 2023 (W23)** — first batch overseen by Tan (kicked off January 2023, Demo Day April 2023). Heavy AI focus already.
  - **Summer 2023 (S23)** — Tan publicly leans into AI thesis; batch heavily AI-skewed.
- **September 27, 2023** — SF Standard publishes piece titled **"Y Combinator's Garry Tan Declares War on San Francisco Politics."** Tan becomes the public face of SF tech moderate-Democrat backlash against the Board of Supervisors.
- **November 13, 2023** — SF Standard: **"Garry Tan Is Organizing SF's Next Political Wave"** — profiles him as the de facto convener of the SF moderate Democrat tech coalition.
- **2024 batches:**
  - **Winter 2024 (W24)** — kicked off January 2024, Demo Day April 2024.
  - **Summer 2024 (S24)** — kicked off June 2024.
- **January 27, 2024** — **"Die slow motherfuckers" tweet.** Tan posts a vulgar tweet from his liquor cabinet wishing death on 7 of 11 SF Supervisors (Chan, Peskin, Preston, Walton, Melgar, Ronen, Safai). Cites Tupac's "Hit 'Em Up" as the reference. Apologizes the next morning. Two supervisors file police reports; threatening letters arrive at supervisors' homes citing the tweet.
- **February 2024** — National media firestorm. *Fortune*, *The New Republic*, *Mission Local*, *TechCrunch*, *SF Standard* all run pieces. YC board does not publicly discipline him; he remains CEO.
- **March 5, 2024** — **SF Democrats for Change** slate (backed by Tan, GrowSF, TogetherSF) **wins control of the SF Democratic County Central Committee (DCCC).** Tan personally contributes ~$12K to four DCCC candidates.
- **May 22, 2024** — TechCrunch: "Garry Tan has revealed his 'secret sauce' for getting into Y Combinator."
- **September 5, 2024** — Tan at **Reboot 2024 (Fort Mason)** publicly addresses whether he would run for mayor; demurs but does not rule it out.
- **September 2024** — **"Founder Mode" essay** by Paul Graham (Sept 1, 2024) — based on Brian Chesky's talk at a YC retreat. Tan was one of the small group of pre-readers Graham thanks. Tan amplifies the essay relentlessly on Twitter and YouTube, becoming the chief evangelist of the "founder mode vs. manager mode" frame.
- **September 13, 2024** — TechCrunch: YC announces **expansion from 2 to 4 batches per year** in 2025 (adding Spring + Fall). Cohort size cuts roughly in half (~100 startups per batch) to keep annual total at ~500. Tan's signature structural reform.
- **September 25–26, 2024** — Summer 2024 Demo Day; **first to feature in-person seats for top VCs** (those who'd invested $50K+ in YC companies in last two years). Reversal of the COVID-era virtual-only Demo Day.
- **September 29, 2024** — **First-ever YC Fall batch (F24)** begins. Demo Day December 4, 2024.
- **October 2–3, 2024** — **Initialized Capital layoffs.** Restructuring announced by managing partner Brett Gibson. Headcount drops from 33 to ~21. **Jennifer Wolf** (managing partner, former president) and **Jennifer Fleiss** (Rent the Runway co-founder, recent hire) both exit. Coverage is brutal — *SF Standard*, *TechCrunch*, *Fortune* frame it as the end of an era. Tan is no longer operationally involved but is the public face.
- **November 5, 2024** — General election. Tan-backed moderate slate has **mixed results** in SF Supervisor races — some progressive incumbents survive; **Daniel Lurie wins SF mayor**. Tan publicly cool on Lurie's campaign during the race (because Lurie's spokesperson Max Szabo had previously backed Chesa Boudin) but warms to him post-election.
- **November 9, 2024** — SF Standard profile: **"San Francisco techies find God"** — features Tan and **Epic Church SF** as the locus of a tech-Christian revival. Tan publicly identifies as Christian (converted "after college").
- **2025 batches:**
  - **Winter 2025 (W25)** — 163 startups; **Demo Day March 13, 2025**.
  - **Spring 2025 (X25)** — YC's **first-ever Spring batch**; ~90% AI companies, 11% hard tech; Demo Day mid-June 2025.
  - **Summer 2025 (S25)** — ~160–169 startups; 88% AI-native.
  - **Fall 2025 (F25)** — second Fall batch.
- **March 6–13, 2025** — Tan's viral "**25% of W25 startups have 95% of code LLM-generated**" framing. Coins (or popularizes) "**the age of vibe coding is here**." Becomes a defining quote of 2025 AI discourse.
- **March 15, 2025** — CNBC: "Y Combinator startups are fastest growing, most profitable in fund history because of AI." Tan reports batch-wide weekly revenue growth jumping from historical 2–4% to **10–20% per week**.

## Political Activism Timeline

- **2015** — First documented YIMBY donations.
- **2020** — COVID-era homelessness, retail crime, and school closures radicalize Tan into active SF politics.
- **2022 (Feb)** — Donates ~$20K to the **SF School Board recall** (Collins, López, Moliga successfully removed Feb 15, 2022).
- **2022 (June)** — Donates ~$100K to the successful **Chesa Boudin DA recall** (June 7, 2022). This is Tan's first six-figure political bet and the inflection point of his public political identity.
- **2022–present** — Board member of **GrowSF**; donates at least $54,500 cumulatively.
- **2023** — Backs **TogetherSF Action** in addition to GrowSF. Hosts coalition events at his Mission District church-conversion property (350 Valley St area, Noe Valley/Mission border).
- **Jan 27, 2024** — The "Die slow" tweet. Watershed event in his public political identity.
- **March 5, 2024** — DCCC moderate sweep.
- **November 5, 2024** — Mixed results on Supervisors; Daniel Lurie wins mayor; Tan retreats slightly from SF-only focus.
- **Late 2025** — Donates ~$40K to **Matt Mahan** (San Jose mayor) for his California gubernatorial run.
- **Late 2025–early 2026** — Publicly opposes California's proposed **billionaire tax**; warns YC may relocate offices if it passes.
- **February 11, 2026** — Launches **Garry's List**, a **501(c)(4)** statewide California nonprofit. Self-described as a **"Rotary Club for radical centrism."** Modeled after Emily's List. Activities: voter guides, blog, Commonwealth Club–style events at his Mission church property, training of moderate candidates. Funded personally by Tan (amount undisclosed); staff of two including an LA-based political strategist.

## Last 12 Months (May 2025 → May 2026) — Detailed

- **May 2025** — Spring 2025 (X25) batch in full session — YC's first-ever Spring cohort. Tan tweets the **Spring 2025 Requests for Startups**: "YC wants founders who treat AI agents not as features but as the core operating system of brand-new companies and industries."
- **June 11, 2025** — TBPN coverage of **X25 Demo Day**: 12% batch-wide revenue growth (up from ~10% in prior batches); **110% YoY jump in 18–22-year-old founders**. Tan frames it as "the last window to start a company before superintelligence locks in the winners."
- **Mid-June 2025** — Spring 2025 Demo Day held in-person with top VCs. Tan's "in-person, invite-only" Demo Day format now stable across all four batches.
- **June–August 2025** — **Summer 2025 (S25) batch** runs. ~160 startups, 88% AI-native, 60%+ explicitly reference "AI" in one-liner. Hard-tech share growing.
- **June 2025** — Appears on **Vanta's "Frameworks for Growth"** podcast. Argues teams can now hit $10M ARR in <12 months with <10 people.
- **September–December 2025** — **Fall 2025 (F25) batch** — second YC Fall cohort. Tan publicly happy with the 4-batches-per-year rhythm; says it's "smaller cohort, deeper relationship."
- **November 2025** — **Mixergy interview**: Tan repeats the "5x faster" framing. Weekly growth rates 10–20% vs. historical 2–4%.
- **Late 2025** — Donates ~$40K to Matt Mahan's California gubernatorial campaign. Publicly opposes the proposed CA billionaire tax. Rumors circulate that Tan and other tech leaders are leaving California — he publicly denies it: "**I'm ride or die.**"
- **December 2025** — Tan re-amplifies "techies find God" narrative; tweets about Michelle and Trae Stephens "bringing people to church" as "more direct than Ayahuasca." Continues to host events at his Christian Science church conversion in the Mission.
- **January 2026** — **W26 batch begins.** Tan tweets that this is the strongest batch in YC history by founder-quality metrics.
- **February 11, 2026** — **Launches Garry's List** statewide California nonprofit (501(c)(4)). Frames as "political infrastructure for the next 20 years."
- **February 2026** — Appears on **The Peel** podcast. Sets a 5–10 year goal of getting YC's network-effects score to 30–50%. Discusses re-batching founders post-Series A. Demos his personal AI workflow: meta-prompting, comparing model outputs head-to-head.
- **March 24, 2026** — **W26 Demo Day.** Tan announces:
  - **14 companies hit $1M ARR by Demo Day** — highest ever for any YC cohort
  - **3x more companies at $1M ARR than W25**
  - **14% week-over-week revenue growth on average** across ~200 startups — fastest in YC history
  - 35% of W26 startups score in the top 20% of all YC companies ever evaluated (per Rebel Fund analysis)
  - Batch composition: ~64% B2B, ~60% AI, only ~5% consumer-facing
- **April–May 2026** — Spring 2026 batch underway. Garry's List ramping up California operations. Tan still publicly defending his decision to stay in California and run nonprofits rather than move to Texas/Florida.

## Personal Life

- **Marriage:** Wife **Stephanie Lim** — co-founded **Third State Books**, one of the first Asian-American publishing houses. They appear together publicly at Asian-American literary events (e.g., Stanford, Feb 2024).
- **Children:** Two sons — Garrison and Solomon (sometimes called "Stephanson" in records). As of 2023 they were ages ~4 and ~8.
- **Residence:** **Noe Valley, San Francisco**, primary home. Also owns a **converted Christian Science church** in the Mission District (350 Valley St area, near Dolores Park) purchased for **~$3.95M** — used for community events, political gatherings, Christian/tech meetups, and the "Code & Cosmos" event.
- **Faith:** **Christian** (converted "after college"). Attends **Epic Church SF** alongside other tech leaders (Pat Gelsinger, Trae Stephens). One of the most prominent publicly-Christian Silicon Valley CEOs.
- **Politics:** Self-identifies as "**moderate Democrat**." Sometimes branded "radical centrist" or "pro-growth Democrat."
- **Public hobbies:** Cycling SF hills, photography (his Twitter is heavy with city shots, especially 20th & Church near Dolores Park, which he calls his "favorite spot in the world"), live music (e.g., Canvas at The Midway), Christian community events, building/hosting at the converted church.
- **Net worth estimates (2025–2026):** Range widely — Forbes-cited ~$300M; other sources claim up to $2B (largely a function of Coinbase share price). Most Coinbase shares believed still held.
