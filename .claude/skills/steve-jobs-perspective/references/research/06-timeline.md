# Steve Jobs — Timeline of Thought-Shaping Turning Points

Scope: Feb 24, 1955 – Oct 5, 2011, plus an epilogue covering Apple and Jobs's legacy from 2011 to the research cutoff of 2026-05-27. Tags: [PRIMARY] = Jobs's own words / on-record sources, [SECONDARY] = Isaacson, Schlender/Tetzeli, Hertzfeld, contemporaneous press, [INFERENCE] = analytical reading not directly quoted. Each entry includes one line on why it mattered to how he thought.

## Early Life (1955–1972)

- **Feb 24, 1955 — Birth and adoption.** [SECONDARY] Born in San Francisco to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali; adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs of Mountain View. *Thought-impact:* the adoption story — biological parents gave him up but Paul and Clara "chose" him — became a load-bearing self-narrative he repeatedly described to biographers as a source of feeling special plus a slight chip about being abandoned. Seeds the lifelong binary of "chosen ones" vs. "bozos."

- **Late 1950s–60s — Paul Jobs's workbench.** [SECONDARY] His father, a machinist and used-car restorer, taught him that the back of a fence or cabinet should be as well-made as the front, even if no one sees it. *Thought-impact:* origin of the "fanatical care about what you can't see" doctrine — visible later in PCB layouts of the Apple II and Mac, the team signatures inside the original Mac case, and the unibody MacBook.

- **~1968 — Calls Bill Hewlett at age 12 for parts.** [PRIMARY, 1995 Smithsonian oral history] Looked Hewlett up in the phone book, asked for frequency-counter parts, got both the parts and a summer job. *Thought-impact:* cemented his lifelong axiom that "people will help you if you just ask" — later applied to recruiting, partnerships, and component sourcing.

- **1969 — Meets Steve Wozniak via Bill Fernandez.** [SECONDARY, Wozniak *iWoz*] Jobs at Homestead High, Woz five years older and already a hardware prodigy. *Thought-impact:* established the career-defining pattern — Jobs as taste/vision/closer, paired with a deep-craft engineer (Woz, later Ive, Forstall, Rubinstein, Tevanian). He stops trying to be the best engineer in the room and starts picking them.

- **~1971 — Blue boxes with Woz.** [SECONDARY, Isaacson; Esquire/Rosenbaum article they read] Built and sold devices to phreak the AT&T phone system. *Thought-impact:* first proof that two guys in a garage could outwit a monopoly with a small box of intelligence — he later told Isaacson there would have been no Apple without the blue box.

- **~1972 — High-school typography exposure begins.** [PRIMARY, Stanford 2005] *Thought-impact:* not yet causal, but the seed of his insistence that humanities and engineering must marry — later expressed as "technology alone is not enough."

## Founding Apple (1972–1985)

- **Fall 1972 — Enrolls at Reed College.** [SECONDARY, Isaacson] *Thought-impact:* Reed's counterculture / Eastern-religion ferment radicalized him — Robert Friedland's Hare Krishna farm, *Be Here Now*, zen practice. Installs the asceticism-plus-perfectionism dyad.

- **Winter 1972 / Spring 1973 — Drops out; drops in on Robert Palladino's calligraphy class.** [PRIMARY, Stanford 2005] Studied serifs, sans-serifs, letter spacing. *Thought-impact:* the canonical "you can't connect the dots looking forward" story — ten years later he insisted the Mac ship with multiple proportionally spaced fonts. Installs the doctrine that aesthetic detours compound into product differentiation. Major thought-evolution event.

- **1974 — Atari, then India.** [SECONDARY, Isaacson; Kottke recollections] Hired at Atari by Al Alcorn (told to put him on the night shift because he smelled). Travels to India ~7 months with Dan Kottke; the intended teacher Neem Karoli Baba died before they arrived; returns shaved, robed, vegan, and committed to Soto Zen (later under Kobun Chino Otogawa). *Thought-impact:* hardens the intuition-over-intellect bias he carried for life ("intuition is more powerful than intellect," he told Isaacson of Indian villagers). Anchors his lifelong fasting/vegan phases and willingness to use silence as a management tool.

- **1975 — Atari Breakout bonus.** [SECONDARY, Bushnell/Alcorn interviews] Tells Woz the contract paid $700 and splits evenly; the real bonus was several thousand. *Thought-impact:* early evidence of the reality-distortion / ends-justify-means streak; early data point that he treated information asymmetry as a tool.

- **April 1, 1976 — Apple Computer founded.** [PRIMARY, founding contract] Jobs, Woz, and Ron Wayne (who sold his 10% back twelve days later for $800). Apple I built in the Jobs garage. *Thought-impact:* the company is the canvas — from day one he saw Apple itself as the product, not the Apple I.

- **1977 — Apple II launch (West Coast Computer Faire).** [SECONDARY] First mass-market PC with color graphics and a finished case (Jerry Manock industrial design). *Thought-impact:* validates the integrated-hardware-software-case thesis he would re-impose in 1997. Industrial design becomes part of the product, not decoration.

- **Dec 1979 — Xerox PARC visit.** [PRIMARY, *Triumph of the Nerds*, 1996] Sees the Alto's bitmapped display, mouse, overlapping windows; Apple gives Xerox pre-IPO stock for the demo. *Thought-impact:* the single most important "they had no idea what they had" moment of his life. Hardens his belief that great research is wasted on management that can't recognize it — the Sculley critique in embryo. Major thought-evolution event.

- **Dec 12, 1980 — Apple IPO.** [PRIMARY, S-1] Jobs worth ~$250M at 25. *Thought-impact:* he later said money didn't change his values but changed his ability to ignore no. The IPO also burns him: he denies paternity of Lisa Brennan-Jobs in the prospectus despite a court-ordered finding — a fact he later said haunted him.

- **1980 — Pushed off Lisa, takes over Macintosh from Jef Raskin.** [SECONDARY, Hertzfeld *Folklore.org*] *Thought-impact:* turns adversity into a smaller, faster team — the prototype for his lifelong preference for small A-player teams over committee org charts.

- **May 1983 — Hires John Sculley with "sugar water" pitch.** [PRIMARY, quote verified by both parties] *Thought-impact:* both a sales artifact and a self-defining line — framing business as world-changing-mission becomes his permanent recruiting and product register.

- **Jan 22, 1984 — "1984" Super Bowl ad; Mac launches Jan 24.** [PRIMARY, keynote] Ridley Scott directs; Apple as rebel against IBM/Big Brother. *Thought-impact:* installs marketing-as-narrative-warfare permanently. Product launch is theater; positioning the company in a story matters as much as the spec sheet.

- **May 1985 — Boardroom defeat; pushed out by Sculley. Resigns Sept 1985.** [SECONDARY, Isaacson; Sculley's *Odyssey*] *Thought-impact:* the formative trauma of his adult life. He later called it "awful-tasting medicine the patient needed" (Stanford 2005). Strips his armor, forces creative rebuilding outside Apple's gravity well, and produces the meta-lesson he repeats forever: *focus is the discipline of saying no.* Major thought-evolution event.

## Wilderness Years (1985–1996)

- **Sept 1985 — Founds NeXT.** [SECONDARY] Brings five Apple lieutenants (Tribble, Page, Crow, Lewin, Barnes). Ross Perot invests $20M for 16% in 1987. *Thought-impact:* lets him design end-to-end without a board check — and over-designs catastrophically (magnesium cube, $9,999 price, optical-only drive). The over-correction itself becomes a lesson he carries into 1997.

- **Feb 1986 — Buys Pixar from Lucasfilm for $10M.** [SECONDARY] Pays $5M to Lucas plus $5M working capital. *Thought-impact:* he initially thought he was buying a hardware company (Pixar Image Computer); discovering slowly that the asset was Lasseter and the storytelling culture rewires him. He learns *the institution itself — its taste, its discipline of revision — is the durable moat,* not any single product (he says this explicitly to Schlender/Tetzeli). Major thought-evolution event.

- **Oct 1988 — NeXT Computer unveiled at Davies Symphony Hall, San Francisco.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* his first keynote-as-art-form. Black cube, demos, Mozart at the end — template for every later Apple keynote.

- **1989 — Tim Berners-Lee writes the first World Wide Web client on a NeXT cube at CERN.** [PRIMARY, Berners-Lee's statements; original machine displayed at CERN] *Thought-impact:* validates his "make tools for the smart and the world catches up" thesis. He cites this for the rest of his life as proof NeXT was not a failure.

- **Mar 18, 1991 — Marries Laurene Powell at Yosemite.** [SECONDARY, Isaacson] Officiated by Kobun Chino. *Thought-impact:* domestic stabilization correlates with the slow softening that produces the *second* Jobs — still demanding, but capable of long-game patience (visible in Pixar 1991–95).

- **1993 — NeXT exits hardware.** [SECONDARY] 280 layoffs; becomes software-only (NEXTSTEP → OpenStep → WebObjects). *Thought-impact:* the public humiliation that teaches him to separate the *technology asset* from the *business model.* He stops believing every great idea must ship as Jobs-built hardware.

- **Nov 22, 1995 — *Toy Story* releases; Pixar IPO Nov 29.** [PRIMARY] First fully computer-animated feature; IPO values Jobs's stake at ~$1.1B. *Thought-impact:* in one week, financial and cultural vindication. Restores the public mandate he lost in 1985 and gives him cash and platform to negotiate the NeXT-Apple deal from strength. Major thought-evolution event.

- **Dec 20, 1996 — Apple acquires NeXT for $429M plus 1.5M shares.** [PRIMARY, Apple press release] Gil Amelio CEO; NEXTSTEP becomes the foundation of Mac OS X. *Thought-impact:* he returns *technically* before he returns *culturally* — the OS is the Trojan horse for the man.

## Return and Renaissance (1997–2003)

- **Aug 6, 1997 — Macworld Boston: $150M Microsoft investment and Office for Mac announced** (Gates on screen behind him). [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* public ritual of swallowing pride to save Apple. Installs the mature operating principle: *win the war, not the press conference.* Major thought-evolution event.

- **Sept 1997 — Becomes iCEO; kills 70% of the product line on a whiteboard 2×2** (Consumer/Pro × Desktop/Portable). [PRIMARY, multiple recountings] *Thought-impact:* the 2×2 becomes his most-cited management artifact. Focus by subtraction is permanent doctrine from here.

- **Sept 28, 1997 — "Think Different" launches** (Chiat/Day; Jobs reviewed every frame). [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* he re-installs Apple's *mythology* before re-installing its products — brand-as-belief-system as a precondition for product credibility.

- **Aug 15, 1998 — iMac G3 ships.** [PRIMARY, keynote] Bondi blue, no floppy, USB-only. *Thought-impact:* validates that *industrial design plus removing legacy ports* is a feature, not a risk. Establishes the via negativa product muscle he reuses through iPhone (no keyboard) and MacBook Air (no optical drive).

- **May 19, 2001 — First Apple Stores open (Tysons Corner, Glendale).** [PRIMARY, Ron Johnson partnership] *Thought-impact:* he had concluded the channel was killing the product experience; advertising couldn't substitute for the customer touching the device. End-to-end control extends from the product to the point of sale.

- **Mar 24, 2001 — Mac OS X 10.0 ships.** [SECONDARY] NeXTSTEP wins. *Thought-impact:* vindication of the 1985–96 wilderness.

- **Oct 23, 2001 — iPod launches** ("1,000 songs in your pocket"). [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* first proof, post-return, that Apple could move beyond the Mac. The "device + service + store" trinity he reuses for iPhone is born here (iPod + iTunes + iTMS in 2003).

- **April 28, 2003 — iTunes Music Store opens.** [PRIMARY, keynote] Negotiates with the five major labels personally. *Thought-impact:* learns that *content owners will move if you give them a clean win and one throat to choke* — a lesson reused with publishers (iBooks) and carriers (iPhone/AT&T).

## Cancer and Late Period (2003–2011)

- **October 2003 — Pancreatic neuroendocrine (islet-cell) tumor diagnosed.** [PRIMARY, Stanford 2005; Isaacson] Rare, slow-growing, operable. He delays surgery nine months trying diet, acupuncture, and alternative therapies. *Thought-impact:* the single most consequential decision of his late life; he later told Isaacson he regretted it. Reinforces — and ultimately punishes — his lifelong faith that diet and intuition can override expert advice. Major thought-evolution event.

- **July 31, 2004 — Whipple procedure at Stanford.** [PRIMARY, email to Apple staff] *Thought-impact:* the first true mortality reckoning, which he deliberately metabolizes into the Stanford commencement.

- **June 12, 2005 — Stanford commencement address.** [PRIMARY] Three stories: connecting the dots, love and loss, death. *Thought-impact:* the closest thing to a written philosophy he produced. Distills 50 years into "Stay hungry, stay foolish" and "Your time is limited, don't waste it living someone else's life." Canonical primary source for late-period Jobs.

- **June 6, 2005 — Intel transition announced at WWDC.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* second platform transition (after 68k→PowerPC). Confirms his rule: *the platform is the company's lifeblood; switch when the physics demands it, not when the customer demands it.*

- **Jan 9, 2007 — iPhone keynote (Macworld SF)** ("an iPod, a phone, and an internet communicator… these are not three separate devices"). [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* apotheosis of his "integration is the moat" doctrine. He privately concluded that the Mac was no longer the center of computing — the post-PC era starts in his head here, years before the iPad.

- **Jan 9, 2007 — Company renamed "Apple Inc." (drops "Computer").** [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* an explicit naming-as-strategy act — he believed the name had to lead the strategy, not lag it.

- **Jan 15, 2008 — MacBook Air pulled from a manila envelope.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* the showmanship is by now an industrialized weapon. Also: visible weight loss; press speculation about his health begins.

- **Jan 5, 2009 — "Hormone imbalance" letter** followed days later by a medical-leave letter. [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* the moment his secrecy doctrine collides with shareholder-disclosure norms. He never fully reconciles the conflict.

- **April 2009 — Liver transplant at Methodist University Hospital, Memphis.** [SECONDARY, hospital confirmed 2009] *Thought-impact:* he privately knows the cancer has spread. From here his time-allocation discipline tightens — fewer meetings, more concentrated bets (iPad, Apple Park).

- **Jan 27, 2010 — iPad unveiled.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Thought-impact:* he publicly articulates the "post-PC" framing — cars vs. trucks. The last big product category he personally designs and launches.

- **April 29, 2010 — "Thoughts on Flash" open letter.** [PRIMARY] Six arguments against Adobe Flash on iOS. *Thought-impact:* his only long-form written product-strategy memo as post-return Apple CEO. Codifies the doctrine: *control the stack; do not let a dying middleware tax your platform.*

- **June 1, 2010 — D8 interview with Mossberg and Swisher.** [PRIMARY, on-record video] His last major sit-down interview. *Thought-impact:* clearest late-period articulation of his frameworks — cars/trucks, integration vs. fragmentation, the duty to "tell the user what they want before they know it." Primary source for the late Jobs.

- **June–July 2010 — iPhone 4 Antennagate** (July 16 press conference). [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* a rare public mistake. He responds with data and free bumpers. Confirms his rule: *when you're wrong, fix it fast and publicly; don't let a narrative metastasize.*

- **Jan 17, 2011 — Third medical leave announced.** [PRIMARY, letter to employees] *Thought-impact:* he begins explicit succession transition to Tim Cook in operations.

- **Mar 2, 2011 — iPad 2 keynote at Yerba Buena.** [PRIMARY] His last product keynote. Closes with the "intersection of technology and liberal arts" slide. *Thought-impact:* final on-stage articulation of the marriage-of-humanities doctrine that began in the Reed calligraphy class.

- **June 6, 2011 — WWDC iCloud keynote.** [PRIMARY] *Thought-impact:* he publicly demotes the Mac from "hub" to "just another device" — the post-PC frame becomes Apple's official architecture.

- **June 7, 2011 — Cupertino City Council "spaceship" presentation.** [PRIMARY, on-record video] His last public appearance. Presents Apple Park to the council in person. *Thought-impact:* he treats the headquarters itself as a product — the final extension of his Paul-Jobs-cabinet-back doctrine to the institution itself.

- **Aug 24, 2011 — Resigns as CEO; recommends Tim Cook; becomes Chairman.** [PRIMARY, letter to the board] *Thought-impact:* his final act of operational discipline — the succession is clean, on his terms, and Cook is already de facto running the company.

- **Oct 5, 2011 — Dies at home in Palo Alto, age 56.** [PRIMARY, family statement] Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor with metastases. The iPhone 4S — including Siri — is unveiled the day before.

## Epilogue (2011–2026)

- **Oct 24, 2011 — Isaacson's *Steve Jobs* published.** [PRIMARY] Authorized but not edited by Jobs. *Legacy impact:* becomes the canonical (and most-criticized) text. Schlender/Tetzeli's *Becoming Steve Jobs* (2015) is the in-family counter-text — Cook, Cue, Ive, and Laurene Powell Jobs cooperated specifically to soften Isaacson's portrait. The two books bracket the legitimate range of interpretation.

- **Oct 2012 — Apple Maps launch / Scott Forstall departure.** [SECONDARY] Cook fires Forstall after the Maps debacle and his refusal to sign the apology letter. *Legacy impact:* first major Cook-era divergence — Forstall was the most Jobs-like executive remaining; his exit ends skeuomorphism (Forstall/Jobs) and elevates Ive's flat design in iOS 7. Apple's *aesthetic* leadership shifts from a Jobs-trained PM to a Jobs-trained designer.

- **April 24, 2015 — Apple Watch ships.** [SECONDARY] *Legacy impact:* first major new product category designed without Jobs. Initial positioning (fashion, $17,000 gold Edition) is widely read as Ive-without-Jobs-counterweight; Apple later quietly retreats to health/fitness — a course-correction toward the kind of *job-to-be-done* clarity Jobs insisted on.

- **Dec 13, 2016 — AirPods ship** (headphone jack removed Sept 2016). [INFERENCE] *Legacy impact:* the clearest Jobs-doctrine product of the Cook era — removes a port, uses Apple silicon (W1), integrates tightly with iOS, and creates a category. Validates *via negativa + integration* in absentia.

- **April 2017 — Apple Park opens to staff; Steve Jobs Theater dedicated Sept 12, 2017.** [PRIMARY] *Legacy impact:* his last project ships. The campus is over-budget and over-spec — exactly the bet Jobs would have defended. Cook makes the dedication explicit at the iPhone X keynote.

- **June 27, 2019 — Jony Ive announces departure (LoveFrom founded).** [PRIMARY, Apple press release] *Legacy impact:* the last of the Jobs-era creative principals leaves. Design at Apple passes to Jeff Williams / Evans Hankey / Molly Anderson — competent stewards, not auteurs. Tripp Mickle's *After Steve* (2022) argues this is when the *taste* function decoupled from the *operations* function for the first time since 1997.

- **2014–Feb 2024 — Project Titan (Apple Car).** [SECONDARY, multiple Bloomberg/NYT reports] Roughly $10B spent over a decade, cancelled February 2024 with team redirected to AI. *Legacy impact:* the canonical Cook-era cautionary tale — read by critics as exactly the kind of indefinite, scope-shifting program Jobs's "focus is saying no" would have killed in year two.

- **June 5, 2023 — Vision Pro unveiled at WWDC; ships Feb 2, 2024.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Legacy impact:* the most ambitious Cook-era new-category bet. Reception (through 2024–25) is muted — sales reportedly below internal targets, production cut. The Jobs-doctrine reading is split: he would admire the integration depth and ambient-computing thesis but likely kill the $3,499 price and tethered battery. By 2026 the live test is whether Apple will *cut* and re-ship a v2 at half the price — Jobs's "courage to kill your darlings" doctrine under direct examination.

- **June 10, 2024 — Apple Intelligence announced at WWDC; partial rollout 2024–25; broader features delayed into 2025–26.** [PRIMARY, keynote] *Legacy impact:* Apple arrives late to the generative-AI wave, with an on-device + private-cloud + OpenAI-fallback architecture. Jobs-doctrine reading (per 2025–26 Stratechery and the *After Steve* school): on-device privacy and integration are vintage Jobs; the delayed Siri overhaul and OpenAI fallback are not. As of the May 2026 cutoff, Apple Intelligence is the central business-school referendum on whether post-Jobs Apple can still *lead* a platform shift rather than refine one.

- **2020–2026 — Reappraisal in the AI era.** [INFERENCE, synthesizing HBR / Stratechery / podcast canon] Three trends by May 2026: (1) Jobs's "integration is the moat" thesis is *reascending* as foundation-model providers vertically integrate (chips → models → apps); (2) his "small A-player team" thesis is being re-examined against OpenAI/Anthropic-style research orgs; (3) the Sculley cautionary tale — "great companies die when the salespeople take over" — is invoked constantly in critiques of post-Ive Apple. The dominant 2026 reading: *the Jobs frameworks aged better than the Jobs persona.* The via negativa, the 2×2, the integration thesis, and "focus is the discipline of saying no" are now near-default in technology strategy; the reality-distortion / contempt-driven management style is treated as a non-replicable cost.

- **Research cutoff: 2026-05-27.** Apple market cap, leadership (Cook still CEO as of cutoff), and the open status of the Vision Pro v2 / Apple Intelligence Siri overhaul are the live variables that will further test the Jobs frameworks beyond this document.
