## Locked decisions (settled in the generate-ideas Q&A — not open for re-litigation)

1. **Brand-safe only.** Adult / OnlyFans / Fanvue / GFE / parasocial-romance is permanently excluded — not
   because of any platform's policy, but because the model (AI posed as real + romance tuned to extract from
   vulnerable "whales") is one the founder ruled out. Not a test variable.
2. **Soriza = the company / product brand (the OS), NOT a beauty influencer.** The founder is **not** a
   beauty-industry expert; the prior "Soriza" cosmetics app was an **unlaunched agentic-coding PoC**, not
   domain expertise. The beauty-domain moat claim is dropped.
3. **Founder's real earned edges** (what this idea is actually anchored on):
   - **Agentic build speed** (proven by building Soriza end-to-end).
   - **Multi-tenant chatbot CMS + LLM-integration** shipped in production at AI Momentum — *architecturally
     the same thing* as a persona operator OS (managing many AI agents/clients with state). This is the
     non-obvious advantage.
   - **Trilingual Cantonese / Mandarin / English**, HK/GBA reach — a geo/language edge incumbents don't serve.

## The concept

**Operate → productize.** The founder becomes operator #1: run one disclosed-AI persona by hand to *feel*
the operational pain, then build the **Soriza operator OS** — the orchestration layer that holds character
state and automates the 5-tool grind (consistency across image/voice/video, multi-account scheduling,
cross-platform posting, auto-disclosure/compliance, analytics). The OS then lets the founder run a
**portfolio** of personas at sub-linear effort — and *that portfolio is the income, the live proof, and the
proprietary brand-safe workflow data* sold, as a tool, **B2B** to other operators / small studios / brands.

## Problem · Who · Why-now · Demand

- **Problem:** A working AI-persona operator's stack is a 5-platform juggle (image → voice → video →
  schedule → monetize) with every handoff manual; character consistency is the #1 breakage point; nobody
  holds persona state or automates cross-platform posting + compliant disclosure in one product. Running a
  *multi*-persona operation by hand doesn't scale past 1–2.
- **Who:** **OS buyers (B2B, the business):** disclosed-AI persona operators + small brand-safe studios +
  brands wanting an owned ambassador (reachable via operator communities on X/Discord/Reddit, and via the
  founder's own portfolio as proof). **Persona audiences (B2C, the cash/proof engine):** young social
  audiences, HK/GBA/English-first.
- **Why now:** persona build-cost fell ~93% ($380K→$28K) and the production stack is near-zero-COGS / partly
  local (Flux LoRA + Hunyuan/Wan video + open voice on a 4090; text on a paid API) — so the operator
  population is exploding while the bottleneck moved from *generation* to *orchestration* (the founder's
  exact strength). Disclosure is becoming mandated infrastructure (a compliance-native OS is ahead of the curve).
- **Demand signal:** a documented operator clears ~$150K/yr on a **$77/mo hand-stitched 5-tool stack**;
  consistency is named "the technical problem that breaks most builds"; "missing layer = an orchestrator that
  holds character state + automates cross-platform posting." (Full citations in `grounding.md`.)

## Moat thesis (why this isn't "just another generator")

The commodity models (Flux/Hunyuan/ElevenLabs) are **not** the moat — anyone rents them, and build-cost is
collapsing. The durable edges:
1. **Workflow depth in the orchestration layer** — multi-persona, compliance-aware operation competitors
   running spreadsheets can't match; and the founder's *actual* earned-knowledge area (multi-tenant CMS).
2. **The founder's own persona portfolio** — owned audiences + proprietary "what works, brand-safe" data
   no competitor has; doubles as the unbeatable demo.
3. **Trilingual / HK-GBA depth** — language + cultural edge the funded Western tools are documented-weak on.
4. **Compliance baked in** — disclosure/audit as a feature, ahead of EU AI Act / China AIGC / FTC.

## Strategy: operate → productize (the sequence)

1. **Operate ONE brand-safe persona, manually — to LEARN (weeks, dogfood; NOT to live off).** Niche chosen
   for learnability + growth (founder's own interest is fine — lifestyle / tech / fintech, etc.), **not beauty.**
2. **Interview real operators IN PARALLEL** about willingness-to-pay for an operator OS — validate the B2B
   demand *before* heavy build.
3. **Build the Soriza operator OS** to automate the grind discovered in step 1.
4. **The OS runs a portfolio of personas at sub-linear effort** → portfolio = B2C income + live proof +
   proprietary data (the moat).
5. **Sell the OS B2B** (operators / studios / brands). **NOT B2C self-serve** — worst fit for a solo,
   GTM-light founder (volume marketing, low price, churn, support) and it's the commoditized, adult-skewed layer.

## The dual engine (12–24 month vision — not the v1 scope)

- **B2C personas = cash floor + proof.** **B2B OS = the durable business + moat.**
- The personas are the OS's **output**, not a competing business — the OS is the leverage that makes
  multi-persona operating feasible. **Trap to avoid:** scaling persona *count* manually before the OS
  automates it (that's a content sweatshop that eats the runway).

## Fallback (de-risk — keep, don't build yet)

**#7-generalized:** a Cantonese / traditional-Chinese **AI-UGC ad-creative service for HK/GBA DTC sellers.**
Demand is already proven and paid (HeyGen ~$95M / Creatify ~$9M / Arcads ~$10M ARR; incumbents weak on
Cantonese). **If operator willingness-to-pay for the OS doesn't validate in step 2, pivot here** — it sells
from day one with no audience required.

## Open assumptions to carry into /sharpen-hypothesis (ranked by how hard the idea dies if false)

1. **Operator willingness-to-pay (highest / make-or-break).** Will brand-safe operators/studios actually PAY
   for the OS? The tooling layer is crowded + well-funded (HeyGen $100M ARR) and the brand-safe buyer slice
   is **thinner** once adult operators are excluded (a big share of today's operator market is the GFE crowd).
   Validate via operator interviews *before* building.
2. **Distribution / B2B selling = the founder's #1 documented weakness.** Reaching + closing operators/brands.
   More tractable than audience-from-zero, but still real selling.
3. **Can the founder grow even ONE brand-safe persona enough to dogfood + demo?** (the operate phase). VI-
   character brand willingness fell 86%→60% — but here the bar is "enough to learn + prove," not "profitable."
4. **Does operating actually teach what to build?** The dogfood loop must surface real OS requirements, not
   just content grind (the core operate→productize premise).
5. **Compliance as table stakes.** China AIGC labeling (live Sept 2025), EU AI Act Art.50 (Aug 2026),
   FTC/platform disclosure, resemblance-check (NO FAKES) — turn it into a selling point.
6. **Runway reality (3–6 months).** v1 scope = *validate*, not full-build: operate one persona + interview
   operators + a thin OS PoC + a few paying/committed operators. The multi-persona portfolio + full SaaS is
   the 12–24mo vision, not v1.
