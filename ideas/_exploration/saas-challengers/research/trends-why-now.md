# Trends / Why-Now — Findings

## Key findings

- **Agentic coding has structurally changed small-team buildability:** Anthropic's 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report documents that 78% of Claude Code sessions now involve multi-file edits (up from 34% in Q1 2025), average session length jumped from 4 min (autocomplete era) to 23 min (agentic era), and agents complete ~20 autonomous actions before needing human input — a figure that doubled in six months. One Rakuten team implemented a complex feature across a 12.5M-line codebase in a single 7-hour unattended run. This is the empirical basis for YC's "10–100x cost collapse" claim: a solo founder with Claude Code can sustain development velocity that previously required a 5–10-person eng team.

- **YC / Jared Friedman's "SaaS Challengers" RFS is live and explicit:** The Summer 2026 Requests for Startups names chip design software (EDA), ERPs, industrial control systems (ICS/SCADA/MES), and supply chain management (SCM/TMS/WMS) as the priority target categories. The exact framing: "The last generation of great software companies was built by replacing on-premise with cloud. The next will be built by replacing legacy SaaS with AI-native software." Friedman's playbooks: (1) clone at 1/10th price, (2) AI-native rebuild rethinking the workflow entirely, (3) bundle 10 point-solutions into one suite, (4) open-source replacement monetized via services. This is a direct investor mandate, not a trend report — YC is actively funding this category now.

- **Campfire is the clearest proof-of-wedge against a "invulnerable" incumbent:** 12-person team, ~100 customers at seed, raised $35M Series A (Accel, Jun 2025) and $65M Series B just 12 weeks later — over $100M in under a year. The wedge: AI-native ERP that cut one customer's financial close from 15 days to 3 days; auto-reconciles AWS bills; customers with 100+ employees are "ripping out NetSuite and putting in Campfire" within 9 months. Named wins: Advisor360, Rhumbix, Fooji. This validates that a tiny team CAN displace entrenched ERP with a workflow-native AI wedge — not by being cheaper, but by being faster and automating drudgery.

- **Rillet raised $100M in under a year (Sequoia → a16z/ICONIQ) targeting the same ERP gap:** $25M Series A (Sequoia) followed by $70M Series B (a16z + ICONIQ, Aug 2025) in 10 weeks, 200+ customers, doubled ARR in 12 weeks. Wedge: AI-native ERP for mid-market SaaS companies that have outgrown QuickBooks/Xero but balk at a 12-month NetSuite implementation. Postscript ($100M+ ARR) now closes books in 3 days using Rillet. The pattern — fast close, no implementation pain, AI automation of revenue recognition/reconciliation — is the repeatable AI-native wedge formula.

- **NetSuite's pricing structure is the vulnerability:** Total first-year cost for a midsize company runs $50K–$100K+ with 20–50% in hidden costs; standard implementations take 6–12 months; customer reviews cite data held "at ransom" and charges "for every click." This is a textbook incumbent pricing moat turning into a switching catalyst — exactly when AI-native alternatives can offer faster onboarding and lower TCO.

- **EDA challengers are raising at Series A scale on agent-specific unlocks:** ChipAgents (ex-Synopsys/Cadence/Mentor team) closed a $50M Series A1 after a 6,377% increase in monthly platform usage in H1 2025, claiming 10x productivity on RTL design, debugging, and verification. Cognichip raised $60M Series A (Apr 2026, total $93M) with 30+ semiconductor design customers. Normal Computing raised $50M (Samsung Catalyst, Mar 2026). The unlock: LLMs can now parse HDL/RTL, assist with verification, and automate design iteration loops — tasks that required senior EDA specialists. This is the "why 2025–2026 and not 2022" for EDA: LLMs crossed the threshold for structured hardware description languages.

- **Reshoring + CHIPS Act create a mandatory new-fab software demand spike:** The U.S. semiconductor sector generated 35% of all reshoring jobs announced in 2024, attracting ~$102.6B in capital investment; U.S. fab capacity projected to triple by 2032 with 2025–2027 as critical build years. New fabs need MES, ERP, and yield-optimization software — but incumbent MES vendors (Siemens Opcenter, Rockwell FactoryTalk) are legacy stacks not optimized for AI-era data volumes. Semiconductor manufacturing generates 10–100x more data than other manufacturing; current analytics systems process only 10% of it. This is a forced-adoption window: greenfield fabs will not choose 1990s MES software if an AI-native alternative exists.

- **55% of U.S. IT executives expect to replace some commercial software with AI-generated tools (Recognize survey, Nov 2025, n=200+):** And Gartner projects 35% of point-product SaaS tools will be replaced by AI agents or absorbed into larger agent ecosystems. This is demand-side pull, not just supply-side capability — buyers are actively evaluating replacement, which shortens the sales cycle for challengers vs. the previous era where switching inertia was the dominant force.

- **Doss raised $55M (Mar 2026) as an inventory/ERP layer sitting between AI-native ERPs and existing systems:** Co-led by Madrona and Premji Invest, with Intuit Ventures participating; Rillet and Campfire are named partners. The wedge: AI inventory management that integrates with both legacy ERPs and new AI-native ones — a "Swiss Army knife" integration play. This signals a meta-opportunity: even the challengers need connective tissue, and that connective layer is itself fundable.

- **Enterprise vertical AI spend tripled to $3.5B in 2025 (Bessemer):** And 47% of top-500 U.S. enterprises migrated at least one business process from SaaS to a vertical AI agent in 2024–2025, up from 11% in 2023 (Stanford HAI). The "why now" for a solo founder: the buyer is no longer skeptical — they're actively looking. The window is 2025–2027 before the next wave of AI-native incumbents locks in their own moats.
