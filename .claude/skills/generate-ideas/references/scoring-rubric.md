# Scoring Rubric — narrowing 10 → 1-3

Transparent judgment scoring. Score each of the 10 ideas on the five dimensions below, then recommend the 1-3 that clear the bar. There is no script and no persona vote — the point is that the founder can see *why* each pick won.

## The five dimensions

Score each 1-5 (5 = strongest). Hold the meaning of the dimension, not a false-precision number — the score exists to make the ranking legible, not to compute a winner to two decimals.

1. **Pain severity** — how acute is the problem this idea attacks? Is it a painkiller (frequent, costly, felt) or a vitamin (mild, occasional, nice-to-have)? Ground this in the demand/pain-validation findings, not optimism.
2. **Why-now timing** — what changed recently that makes this buildable/winnable *now* and not three years ago or three years from now? Pull from the trends/why-now findings. An idea with no "why now" is a 1-2 here regardless of how good it sounds.
3. **Founder-market fit** — does this fit the founder's background, skills, unfair advantage, and constraints captured in the intake? An idea the founder is uniquely positioned to build (or uniquely motivated to) outscores a generically-good one they have no edge on.
4. **Wedge / defensibility** — is there a sharp entry wedge and some path to defensibility (a real gap competitors leave open, a hard-to-copy mechanism, a network/data/integration moat)? Pull from the competitor findings. "Better UI on a crowded market" is a 1-2.
5. **Feasibility** — can a small team get to a first testable version within the founder's stated constraints (capital, regulatory appetite, geography)? Heavy capex / multi-year regulatory paths score low unless the founder signaled appetite for them.

## How to weight

- Honor the founder's Step-6 flags: ideas they flagged as favorites get a thumb on the scale, but a flagged idea that scores poorly on pain severity or why-now should still be told so plainly — don't launder a weak favorite into a recommendation.
- **Pain severity and why-now are the dimensions that most often kill an idea.** A high feasibility / high founder-fit idea with weak pain or no why-now is a trap; say so.
- Don't average mechanically. A `4-5-2-4-4` (real pain, great timing, but no founder edge) and a `2-2-5-5-5` (founder loves it, but no one else hurts) are different stories — narrate which story each pick is.

## Selecting the 1-3

- Recommend only ideas that clear a real bar — strong on pain severity AND why-now, with at least a credible wedge. **One genuinely strong idea beats three padded ones.** It's a correct outcome to recommend a single pick.
- Prefer picks that are distinct from each other (different user, wedge, or angle) so the founder gets a real choice, not three flavors of one bet.
- For each recommended pick, the explanation in `ideas.md` names the dimensions it won on and the one it's weakest on — honesty about the weak dimension is what makes the recommendation trustworthy and seeds the founder's disconfirmation thinking.
